# Trust Structuring — Skill

## Purpose

Advise on the establishment, structuring, and administration of onshore Hong Kong trusts under the Trustee Ordinance (Cap 29), common law principles, and the Recognition of Trusts Ordinance (Cap 76). Includes trust creation, trustee selection, investment powers, and variation of trusts.

## Scope

- Types of trusts available under HK law
- Trust creation: the three certainties
- Express trusts (fixed, discretionary, charitable)
- Trusts of land vs personalty
- Trustee duties and powers
- Investment powers under Cap 29
- Variation of trusts
- Charitable and purpose trusts
- Off-shore trusts in HK (onshore not offshore regime)

## Workflow

### 1. Determine the Client's Objectives

**Common objectives**:
- **Asset protection**: Protecting assets from future creditors, divorcing spouses, or bankruptcy — note: HK does not have asset protection trust legislation; fraudulent conveyance principles apply
- **Estate planning**: Avoiding probate delays, ensuring controlled distribution, providing for vulnerable beneficiaries
- **Tax planning**: While HK has no inheritance/capital gains tax, consider the tax position in the beneficiaries' home jurisdictions
- **Commercial**: Employee benefit trusts, pension trusts, unit trusts, security trusts (for bondholders)
- **Charitable**: Establishing a charitable trust

**Key questions**:
- What type of property will be settled (land, cash, shares, business interests)?
- Who are the intended beneficiaries and in what proportions?
- Does the settlor wish to retain any control or benefit?
- Is the trust intended to be fixed or discretionary?
- Should the trust be made during the settlor's lifetime (inter vivos) or by will (testamentary)?

### 2. Choose Type of Trust

**Express trusts — Fixed vs Discretionary**:
| Factor | Fixed Trust | Discretionary Trust |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Beneficiaries' entitlement | Defined shares | At trustees' discretion |
| Certainty of objects | Necessary | Class must be ascertainable |
| Flexibility | Low | High |
| Protection | Less protection from creditors | Greater asset protection potential |
| Tax | Transparent | May be transparent for some jurisdictions |

**Charitable trusts**:
- Must be for exclusively charitable purposes (relief of poverty, advancement of education, advancement of religion, other purposes beneficial to the community)
- Certainty of objects not required (but must be certain as to purpose)
- Perpetual (not subject to the rule against perpetuities)
- Registered charities: Inland Revenue Department registration required for tax exemption

**Non-charitable purpose trusts**:
- Generally void unless expressly saved (pets, tombs, monuments)
- Unincorporated associations: check if the gift is to the members or to the purposes

### 3. Draft the Trust Deed

**Essential clauses**:
1. **Settlement clause** — settlor transfers property to trustees on the trusts declared
2. **Trust period/closing date** — perpetuity period (usually 80 years under Cap 257)
3. **Trust for beneficiaries** — defined class and shares (fixed) or class (discretionary)
4. **Powers of trustees** — investment, sale, insurance, maintenance (s.36), advancement (s.37), delegation (s.27)
5. **Remuneration clause** — professional trustee charging clause
6. **Appointment and removal of trustees** — power to replace trustees
7. **Retirement of trustees** — procedures
8. **Exclusion of beneficiaries** — power to add or exclude beneficiaries
9. **Protector clause** (optional) — appoint protector with powers to veto trustee decisions, remove trustees, direct investments
10. **Governing law** — HK law (express choice)

**Additional clauses for discretionary trusts**:
- **Memorandum of wishes** — non-binding guidance letter from settlor to trustees

**Additional clauses for charitable trusts**:
- Charitable purpose clause (drafted to meet IRD requirements)
- Cy-près clause (what happens if the charitable purpose becomes impossible)

### 4. Appoint Trustees

**Selection criteria**:
- **Individuals**: Trusted family members or professional advisers
- **Corporate trustees**: Licensed trust companies (regulated by the Hong Kong Monetary Authority — TCSP Ordinance)
- **Number**: At least one; two or more is common (minors and persons lacking capacity cannot be trustees)

**Trustee capacity**:
- s.34–42 Cap 29 — appointment and removal of trustees
- s.35 — professional trustees may charge for services (need charging clause in trust deed or beneficiary consent)
- Consider: conflicts of interest, independence, expertise, continuity

### 5. Settle the Trust Property

**Constitution**:
- The trust must be completely constituted (property vested in trustees)
- **Land in HK**: Transfer by assignment/deed of conveyance — register at Land Registry (memorial)
- **Shares in HK companies**: Transfer by share transfer form; update the company's register of members; note in the SCR
- **Cash**: Transfer to trustee bank account
- **Insurance policies**: Assign policy or nominate trustees
- **Other assets**: Formal transfer as required by law

**No consideration**: A voluntary settlement (no consideration from beneficiaries) requires proper constitution — equity will not perfect an imperfect gift.

### 6. Trustee Duties and Administration

**Initial duties**:
- Review trust deed and follow terms
- Open trustee bank account
- Manage and safeguard trust assets
- Maintain trust accounts and records
- Provide information to beneficiaries (subject to discretion)

**Ongoing duties**:
- **Investment**: Wide power under s.16–16C Cap 29 — must consider suitability, diversification, risk, need for advice
- **Annual accounts**: Prepare annual trust accounts
- **Trustee meetings**: Minutes of all decisions
- **Beneficiary communication**: Respond to reasonable requests for information

**Investment considerations**:
- Standard investment criteria: suitability, diversification, risk
- Ethical/socially responsible investing — if relevant to the trust
- Professional advice required under s.16B unless reasonably considered unnecessary
- Trustee must act as a prudent person of business

### 7. Variation of Trusts

**By agreement (Saunders v Vautier)**:
- All beneficiaries of full age and capacity, acting together, may require the trustees to transfer the trust property to them
- They may vary the terms of the trust by consent

**By the court (Cap 253 Variation of Trusts Ordinance)**:
- Court may approve variation on behalf of:
  - Beneficiaries who lack capacity
  - Minor beneficiaries
  - Unborn beneficiaries
  - Persons with future discretionary interests
- Court must be satisfied the variation is for the benefit of the represented beneficiaries

**By express power**:
- Trust deed may include power of variation — follow the procedure carefully

### 8. Termination

- Trust terminates on the **closing date** (end of trust period) or on the happening of a specified event
- On termination: trustees distribute trust property to beneficiaries according to the trust terms
- Final accounts prepared and approved
- Trustees retire and are discharged

## Common Pitfalls

- **Imperfect constitution**: If trust property is not properly transferred, the trust fails for want of constitution
- **No memorandum of wishes**: For discretionary trusts, a letter of wishes is essential guidance for trustees
- **No trust accounts**: Trustees must keep proper accounts — failure to do so may lead to removal
- **Investment without advice**: s.16B Cap 29 requires trustees to obtain and consider proper advice (unless reasonably unnecessary)
- **Trustee conflicts**: Ensure independent trustees are appointed; a trustee who is also a beneficiary must not favour their own interests
- **Offshore assets**: HK trusts holding overseas assets may trigger foreign tax, reporting, and regulatory requirements

# Will Drafting — Skill

## Purpose

Draft valid and effective wills for Hong Kong clients under the Wills Ordinance (Cap 30), including standard, privileged, Muslim, and Chinese customary wills. Advise on testamentary capacity, revocation, and cross-border considerations.

## Scope

- Will formalities (s.5 Cap 30)
- Testamentary capacity and undue influence
- Revocation and alteration
- Standard will clauses
- Trusts in wills (testamentary trusts)
- Privileged and special wills
- Cross-border wills
- Attestation and execution

## Workflow

### 1. Initial Client Meeting

**Information gathering**:
- Full details of assets (HK and overseas): property, bank accounts, investments, shares, insurance policies, MPF, business interests
- Family structure: spouse, children (including from previous relationships), dependants, stepchildren
- Prior wills — request copies for revocation
- Other estate planning documents — powers of attorney, trust deeds, pre-nuptial agreements
- Beneficiary nominations for MPF, insurance, pension

**Key questions**:
- Who should be the executor(s)? (consider number, relationship, professional capacity)
- Who are the intended beneficiaries and in what shares?
- Any specific gifts of particular assets?
- Any trusts for minors or disabled beneficiaries?
- Any charitable legacies?
- Funeral wishes and organ donation preferences?
- Cross-border issues — do they own assets outside HK?

### 2. Assess Testamentary Capacity (Banks v Goodfellow)

The testator must:
- Understand the nature of making a will and its effects
- Understand the extent of their property
- Appreciate the claims of those who might expect to benefit
- Not be affected by a disorder of mind that perverts their sense of right or moral duty

**Special considerations**:
- **Golden rule**: If there is any doubt about capacity, have a medical practitioner assess the testator and witness the execution
- **Capacity report** — particularly important for elderly clients or those with degenerative conditions
- **Bradshaw v Beachly (HK)** — reinforces the Banks v Goodfellow test in HK

### 3. Check for Undue Influence or Suspicious Circumstances

**Red flags**:
- A beneficiary is present at the meeting or involved in arranging the will
- Sudden change of instructions excluding close family members
- Testator is dependent on the beneficiary for care
- Testator appears hesitant, uncertain, or pressured
- Language barrier or lack of independent interpreter

**Action**: If suspicious circumstances exist, consider:
- Separate meeting with the testator alone
- Medical evidence of capacity
- Recording the instructions and execution
- Consider using a solicitor not connected to any beneficiary

### 4. Draft Standard Will Clauses

**Essential clauses**:

1. **Opening** — "I, [name], of [address], declare this to be my last will..."
2. **Revocation clause** — "I revoke all former wills and testamentary dispositions made by me..."
3. **Appointment of executors and trustees** — usually 1–2 executors; consider professional trustee for complex estates
4. **Guardian appointment** — for minor children
5. **Specific gifts** — "I give [specific property] to [beneficiary]..."
6. **Pecuniary legacies** — "I give the sum of HK$X to [beneficiary]..."
7. **Residuary gift** — "I give the residue of my estate to [beneficiary]..."
8. **Survivorship clause** — "If [beneficiary] predeceases me, the gift passes to [alternative]..."
9. **Minority clause** — trusts for beneficiaries under 18 (vesting age typically 18 or 21)
10. **Funeral wishes** — optional but recommended
11. **Execution clause** — "Signed by the testator in our presence..."

**Optional clauses**:
- Powers of investment for executors/trustees
- Charging clause (professional executors can charge)
- Powers of appropriation (for distribution in specie)
- Indemnity clause for executors
- Exclusion of or restriction on family provision claims (limited effectiveness)
- Digital assets clause (social media, crypto, domain names)

### 5. Consider Testamentary Trusts

**When to use a testamentary trust**:
- Minor beneficiaries (trust until age 18 or later)
- Disabled or incapacitated beneficiaries
- Spendthrift or vulnerable beneficiaries
- Asset protection (divorce, creditors, bankruptcy)
- Tax planning in the beneficiary's jurisdiction

**Common structures**:
- **Life interest trust**: Income to one beneficiary for life, capital to another
- **Discretionary trust**: Trustees decide distribution among a class (good for vulnerable beneficiaries)
- **Protective trust**: Income until a defined event (bankruptcy, attempted alienation)

### 6. Execution — s.5 Cap 30

**Procedure**:
1. Testator signs at the **foot/end** of the will in the presence of **two witnesses** (both present together)
2. If testator cannot sign: another person signs in testator's presence and at their direction
3. Each witness must sign in the testator's presence
4. Witnesses should be **disinterested** (not beneficiaries or spouses of beneficiaries)
5. Attestation clause: "Signed by the testator in our joint presence and then by us in the testator's presence"

**Common mistakes**:
- Witness signing before the testator
- Testator signing outside the witnesses' presence
- Witness being a beneficiary or their spouse (gift to witness or spouse is void — s.15(3) Cap 30)
- Testator failing to sign at the foot of the will (any gift after signature may fail)

### 7. Cross-Border Wills

**HK resident with foreign assets**:
- HK will does not govern foreign real estate (lex situs applies)
- Foreign real estate requires a separate will in the jurisdiction where the land is located
- **Recommendation**: Have separate wills for each jurisdiction or a single HK will with a foreign property schedule

**HK will for foreign estate administration**:
- A grant of probate in HK must be resealed in the foreign jurisdiction (Commonwealth countries)
- Non-Commonwealth countries: resealing may not be available — need separate proceedings

**Muslim wills**:
- Recognised under s.20(1) Cap 30 — oral wills permitted for Muslims
- Must be made in accordance with Muslim law
- Written Muslim wills should also comply with s.5 formalities

**Chinese customary wills**:
- Recognised if made in accordance with Chinese custom
- Increasingly rare; better to make a formal will under Cap 30

### 8. Post-Execution

- **Safekeeping**: Original should be kept with the testator's solicitor, at a bank, or with the Probate Registry (will storage is offered by the HK Judiciary for a fee)
- **Will review**: Advise client to review will every 3–5 years or on: marriage, divorce, birth of children, significant change in assets, change in law
- **Copies**: Distribute copies (not originals) to executors and trusted family members
- **Letter of wishes**: For trusts, a separate letter of wishes to trustees (non-binding but influential)

### 9. Revocation and Codicils

**Revocation methods**:
- **Marriage**: Automatically revokes a will (unless made in contemplation of the marriage — s.15(1) Cap 30)
- **Subsequent will or codicil**: Express revocation clause is clearest
- **Destruction**: Physical destruction (burning, tearing, obliteration) with animus revocandi — both required
- **Divorce**: Does not revoke the will but severs gifts to the former spouse (s.15A Cap 30)

**Codicils**:
- Used to make minor amendments (change executor, add a legacy)
- Must be executed with the same formalities as a will
- Multiple codicils should refer back to the will

## Common Pitfalls

- **Witness who is a beneficiary**: The gift to that beneficiary is void — always use independent witnesses
- **No survivorship clause**: If a beneficiary predeceases the testator, the gift lapses into residue
- **Ambiguous language**: "I give my shares to X" — which shares? Specify ISIN, company name, number of shares
- **No guardians clause**: Minor children risk being taken into care if both parents die
- **Outdated will**: Marriage revokes the will unless made in contemplation — always check

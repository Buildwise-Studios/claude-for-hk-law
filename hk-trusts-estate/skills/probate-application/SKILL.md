# Probate Application — Skill

## Purpose

Guide legal practitioners through the process of applying for a grant of probate or letters of administration in Hong Kong under the Probate and Administration Ordinance (Cap 10), including documentation, procedures, and common challenges.

## Scope

- Types of grants available
- Application procedure
- Required documentation
- Oath of executor/administrator
- Advertisement requirements
- IRD clearance
- Objections and caveats
- Resealing of foreign grants

## Workflow

### 1. Determine the Appropriate Grant

| Grant Type | Circumstance |
|------------|-------------|
| **Grant of Probate** | Deceased left a valid will; named executor(s) willing and able to act |
| **Letters of Administration (with will annexed)** | Will exists but no executor appointed, or executor(s) cannot or will not act |
| **Letters of Administration** | Intestacy — no will |
| **Double Probate** | Probate granted to one executor; remaining executor(s) apply later |
| **Administration de bonis non** | Original administrator dies or is removed before estate fully administered |
| **Ad colligenda bona** | Urgent grant to protect assets (e.g., perishable goods, urgent bills) |

### 2. Pre-Application Procedures

**Locate the will**:
- Check with the testator's solicitor, bank, or the Probate Registry (if stored)
- If the original cannot be found, apply for: (a) a copy of the will (probate copy) together with an affidavit of due execution or (b) letters of administration if the will is lost

**Identify all executors**:
- All named executors should be notified
- Executors may renounce (form of renunciation)
- Power reserved to other executors (they may apply later for double probate)

**Collect information**:
- Full inventory of all assets (HK and overseas)
- Full schedule of liabilities
- Valuation of property (land, shares, vehicles)
- Bank account balances at date of death

### 3. Prepare Documentation

**Standard documents**:
1. **Oath of Executor / Administrator** (Form 3 or Form 18):
   - Sworn before a Commissioner for Oaths
   - Contains: deceased's full name, address, occupation, date and place of death
   - Executor's/administrator's details
   - Consent to act
   - Statement of estate value (gross and net)
   - Statement that the executor/administrator will administer the estate
   - Full disclosure of any prior knowledge of the deceased's affairs

2. **Death Certificate** — original or certified copy
3. **Original Will** — and any codicils (last will only)
4. **Consents** (if applicable):
   - Consent of executors not applying (power reserved)
   - Renunciation of executors
   - Consent of administrator (if not named executor)

5. **Affidavit of due execution** — required if:
   - Will is not properly executed on its face (no attestation clause)
   - Will is lost and probate copy is being used
   - Will is suspicious or challenged

**Property in joint names**: Joint tenancy passes automatically by survivorship — not included in the estate (but IRD still needs to know)

### 4. Filing with the Probate Registry

**Where to file**:
- **Probate Registry, High Court**, 38 Queensway, Hong Kong
- In person or by post
- Electronic filing available through the e-Probate system (Integrated Court Case Management System — iCMS)

**Filing fees**:
- Grant of probate / letters of administration: approximately HK$142 (may vary)
- Additional fees for sealed copies (HK$24 per copy)

**Processing time**:
- Simple estates (no issues): 4–6 weeks
- Complex estates or incomplete applications: 8–12 weeks or longer

### 5. Advertisement / Gazette Notice

**When required**:
- Generally required for all grants
- **Gazette**: Notice placed in the Government Gazette
- **Newspaper**: Chinese language newspaper (e.g., Sing Tao Daily, Ming Pao)
- Period: 1 month from advertisement

**Purpose**: To give notice to creditors and persons with claims against the estate

**Waiver**: The court may waive advertisement for small estates or where there are no known creditors

### 6. IRD Clearance

**Procedure**:
1. Lodge estate accounts with the Inland Revenue Department within 3 months of grant
2. IRD will assess whether any estate duty is payable (note: estate duty has been abolished since 2006)
3. IRD issues a certificate of "No Inheritance Tax Liability" (form IRD 1306 or equivalent)
4. No distribution of estate assets until IRD clearance obtained

**Note**: Even though estate duty is abolished, the IRD requires this process to:
- Confirm no tax obligations are outstanding
- Obtain stamp duty assessment on property and HK stock transfers

### 7. Issue of Grant

- Grant issued under seal of the High Court
- Number of copies: Typically 3–5 sealed copies (for each bank, Land Registry, share registrar)
- Grant is conclusive evidence of the executor's/administrator's authority

### 8. Objections and Caveats

**Caveat (Form 25)**:
- Any person may enter a caveat to prevent a grant being issued without notice to them
- Valid for 6 months (renewable)
- Must have "interest" in the estate

**Probate action / contentious probate**:
- If there is a dispute about the validity of the will or capacity, proceedings must be issued
- This involves: writ, statement of claim, pleadings, discovery, trial
- Claims may be: lack of due execution, lack of testamentary capacity, undue influence, fraud

**Warning to caveator**: The person applying for the grant may issue a warning to the caveator to appear and show cause

### 9. Resealing of Foreign Grants

**Commonwealth countries**:
- Resealing under the Colonial Probates Act — grants from UK, Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and other designated Commonwealth countries may be resealed in HK
- Procedure: file the original grant (or certified copy) with the Probate Registry, together with an oath and certified copy of the will
- Once resealed, the HK grant has the same effect as a grant of probate issued in HK

**Non-Commonwealth countries**:
- No resealing procedure
- Must apply for a fresh grant of probate in HK or letters of administration based on the foreign grant
- May require evidence of the foreign law and the validity of the foreign grant

**Mainland China**:
- No resealing arrangement with Mainland China
- Must apply for a fresh grant of probate in the Mainland if HK assets are concerned (or vice versa)
- Consider the 2022 Arrangement for mutual recognition (limited to divorce — not probate)

### 10. Small Estates (Simplified Procedure)

Estates valued at less than HK$50,000:
- Simplified oath required
- No advertisement
- Reduced filing fee
- Single copy of grant

## Common Pitfalls

- **Missing will**: If the original will is lost, probate on the basis of a copy requires an affidavit of due execution and strong evidence of non-revocation
- **Executor renunciation**: Must be filed before grant issues — cannot renounce after accepting the role
- **Incomplete asset schedule**: Missing assets require a supplemental oath and increase costs
- **Joint tenancy**: Do not include in estate — passes by survivorship (but IRD still needs details)
- **No IRD clearance before distribution**: Personal risk to the executor/administrator — indemnity may not cover this
- **Cross-border assets**: Check whether a separate grant is needed in the foreign jurisdiction

# HK Trusts & Estate Practice Profile

## Practice Area Overview

Hong Kong trust and estate law is founded on English common law principles, supplemented by local legislation. The absence of inheritance tax, estate duty (abolished 2006), and capital gains tax makes Hong Kong a highly attractive jurisdiction for wealth planning and trust structuring.

The High Court (Court of First Instance) has jurisdiction over probate and trusts matters. The Probate Registry handles all non-contentious probate applications.

## Key Legislation

| Legislation | Key Provisions |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Trustee Ordinance (Cap 29)** | Trustee powers, duties, investment, appointment, and removal |
| **Probate and Administration Ordinance (Cap 10)** | Grants of representation, administration of estates, powers of personal representatives |
| **Inheritance (Provision for Family and Dependants) Ordinance (Cap 481)** | Family provision claims against an estate |
| **Wills Ordinance (Cap 30)** | Will formalities, revocation, alteration, incorporation |
| **Recognition of Trusts Ordinance (Cap 76)** | Implementation of the Hague Convention on Recognition of Trusts |
| **Perpetuities and Accumulations Ordinance (Cap 257)** | Rule against perpetuities, accumulations |
| **Enduring Powers of Attorney Ordinance (Cap 501)** | Enduring powers of attorney for property and financial affairs |
| **Mental Health Ordinance (Cap 136)** | Protection of property of mentally incapacitated persons |
| **Powers of Attorney Ordinance (Cap 31)** | Ordinary powers of attorney |

## Trusts Law

### Creation of a Trust

**Three Certainties** (common law, affirmed in HK):
1. **Certainty of intention** — words must clearly indicate an intention to create a trust (not a gift or other arrangement)
2. **Certainty of subject matter** — the trust property must be clearly identifiable
3. **Certainty of objects** — the beneficiaries must be certain (or for discretionary trusts, the class must be ascertainable)

**Formalities**:
- **Trusts of land**: Must be evidenced in writing and signed (s.5, Cap 29)
- **Trusts of personalty**: No writing required (though prudent to document)
- **Testamentary trusts**: Must comply with Cap 30 (Wills Ordinance) formalities

**Constitution**: The trust property must be vested in the trustee — either by transfer or declaration.

### Types of Trusts

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|----------------|
| **Express trust** | Created intentionally by the settlor |
| **Fixed trust** | Beneficiaries' interests are defined (e.g., income to A, capital to B) |
| **Discretionary trust** | Trustees have discretion over distribution of income and/or capital |
| **Bare trust** | Trustee holds property for a single beneficiary absolutely |
| **Resulting trust** | Arises where property is transferred but no beneficial interest passes (purchase money resulting trust, automatic resulting trust) |
| **Constructive trust** | Imposed by the court to prevent unconscionable conduct (common in commercial and family disputes) |
| **Protective trust** | Income beneficiary's interest terminates on defined events (bankruptcy, alienation) |
| **Charitable trust** | For charitable purposes (relief of poverty, advancement of education, religion, other purposes beneficial to the community) |

### Trustee Duties

**Fiduciary Duty**:
- Act in the best interests of beneficiaries
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Not profit from the trust (except authorised remuneration)
- Impartiality between beneficiaries

**Statutory Duties (Cap 29)**:
- **Duty of care** (s.3A): Exercise reasonable care and skill (objective standard for professional trustees)
- **Investment duty** (ss.16–16C):
  - Trustees may invest in any investments as if beneficially entitled (wide power)
  - Must have regard to: suitability, diversification, need for income/capital growth, risk
  - Must consider: standard investment criteria, ethical considerations if relevant
  - Must take advice (unless reasonably considered unnecessary)
- **Power of delegation** (s.27): May delegate asset management, custodial functions
- **Power of maintenance** (s.36) — income for minor beneficiaries
- **Power of advancement** (s.37) — capital to beneficiaries before their entitlement

**Common Law Duties**:
- Duty to act personally (non-delegation)
- Duty to account
- Duty to distribute correctly
- Duty to preserve trust property

### Trustee Powers

| Power | Statutory Source |
|-------|-----------------|
| Investment | ss.16–16C Cap 29 |
| Sale and disposal | s.14 Cap 29 |
| Delegation | s.27 Cap 29 |
| Insurance | s.22 Cap 29 |
| Maintenance (income for minors) | s.36 Cap 29 |
| Advancement (capital for minors) | s.37 Cap 29 |
| Remuneration (professional trustees) | s.35 Cap 29 |
| Appointment and removal of trustees | s.34–42 Cap 29 |

### Variation of Trusts

- **By agreement**: All beneficiaries of full capacity may vary (Saunders v Vautier principle)
- **By the court**: Under the Variation of Trusts Ordinance (Cap 253) — court may approve variation on behalf of incapable, minor, or unborn beneficiaries
- **By the trust instrument**: Express power of variation reserved

### Perpetuities and Accumulations

- **Perpetuity period**: 80 years (standard under Cap 257) — trust must vest within this period
- **Accumulation**: Generally limited to 80 years (s.3, Cap 257)
- **Purpose trusts**: Non-charitable purpose trusts are void unless expressly saved (limited exception for pets and tombs)

### Recognition of Foreign Trusts

- HK applies the Hague Convention on Recognition of Trusts (Cap 76)
- Foreign law trusts are recognised if they satisfy basic trust criteria
- Beneficiaries retain rights over trust property for enforcement purposes

## Wills and Estate Planning

### Will Formalities (Cap 30)

A valid will under HK law requires (s.5, Cap 30):
- **In writing** (any form — typed or handwritten)
- **Signed by the testator** (or by another person in the testator's presence and at their direction)
- **Testator's signature**: At the foot/end of the will
- **Two witnesses**: Both present at the same time as the testator signs (or acknowledges their signature)
- **Witnesses sign** in the presence of the testator

**Privileged wills**: Members of the Armed Forces on active service and mariners at sea may make informal (oral) wills (s.19, Cap 30)

**Special wills**: 
- **Muslim wills**: Recognised under s.20, Cap 30 (permit oral wills under Muslim law)
- **Chinese customary wills**: Recognised if made in accordance with Chinese custom

### Revocation of Wills

A will is revoked by (s.14–18, Cap 30):
- **Marriage** — automatically revokes a will (with exceptions — made in contemplation of marriage, s.15)
- **Subsequent will or codicil** — express revocation clause or implied inconsistency
- **Destruction** — physical destruction by the testator or at their direction, with intention to revoke (animus revocandi)
- **Divorce** — does not revoke the will but severs gifts in favour of the former spouse (s.15A)

### Intestacy (no will)

**Distribution under the Intestates' Estates Ordinance (Cap 73)**:

| Survivors | Distribution |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Spouse only** | Whole estate to spouse |
| **Spouse + children** | Spouse: HK$500,000 + ½ of residue; children: ½ residue equally |
| **Children only** | Whole estate to children equally |
| **Parents only** | Whole estate to parents equally |
| **Siblings only** | Whole estate to siblings equally |
| **No family** | Estate passes to the Government (bona vacantia) |

**Note**: Unmarried partners, same-sex partners (unless married in a recognised jurisdiction), and stepchildren have no automatic inheritance rights.

### Estate Planning Considerations

- **No inheritance tax / estate duty** in HK (abolished 2006)
- **Stamp duty** on property transfers (consider holding property via trust or company)
- **Inheritance (Provision for Family and Dependants) Ordinance (Cap 481)** — family provision claims
- **Cross-border assets**: HK wills may not be valid for foreign real estate
- **Life insurance policies**: Proceeds paid directly to named beneficiaries (outside the estate)
- **Joint tenancy**: Property passes by survivorship (outside the will)
- **Mandatory Provident Fund (MPF)**: Nomination of beneficiary is not a testamentary disposition

## Probate and Administration

### Grants of Representation

| Type | When Used |
|------|-----------|
| **Grant of Probate** | Deceased left a valid will; executors appointed |
| **Letters of Administration (with will annexed)** | Will exists but no executor appointed or executors unable to act |
| **Letters of Administration** | Intestacy — no will |
| **Double Probate** | Probate granted to one executor; remaining executors apply later |
| **Administration de bonis non** | Original administrator dies or is removed before estate fully administered |
| **Ad colligenda bona** | Urgent grant to protect assets before full administration |

### Application Process

1. **Swear Oath** — executor/administrator swears oath before Commissioner for Oaths
2. **Filing** — Probate Registry (High Court, CFI)
3. **Advertisement** — Gazette notice (if required): place notice in the Gazette and a Chinese newspaper
4. **Stamp duty** — Inland Revenue Department assessment of stamp duty on HK property and shares
5. **Grant issued** — after 6–8 weeks (simple estates) or longer (complex estates)
6. **Collect in assets** — using the grant to prove authority

**Documents required**:
- Original will (and codicils)
- Death certificate
- Full inventory of assets and liabilities
- Affidavit of due execution (for lost or suspicious wills)
- Consents to act (executors / administrators)

### Administration of Estates

**Timeline for administration**:
1. **0–3 months**: Collect assets, pay urgent debts, identify liabilities
2. **3–6 months**: Pay debts and taxes, distribute legacies
3. **6–12 months**: Finalise estate accounts, distribute residue
4. **Accounts**: Beneficiaries entitled to estate accounts

**Executor/Administrator duties**:
- Collect and protect estate assets
- Pay debts and expenses (taxes, funeral costs, administration expenses)
- Distribute to beneficiaries entitled under will or intestacy
- File estate accounts
- Apply for clearance from the IRD
- Ensure compliance with stamp duty requirements (HK property and shares)

**IRD Clearance**: Before distribution, obtain IRD certificate of no inheritance tax liability (even though estate duty is abolished, the IRD confirms no outstanding liability)

### Claims Against the Estate

**Under Cap 481**:
Class of eligible persons:
1. Spouse or former spouse
2. Child of the deceased (including adult children)
3. Person treated as a child of the family
4. Person being maintained by the deceased immediately before death

Court may order:
- Periodical payments
- Lump sum payment
- Transfer of property
- Variation of the will or intestacy distribution

**Time limit**: Application must be made within 6 months of the grant of representation

## Practice Guidance

When advising on trusts and estates in HK:

1. **Will validity**: Always check strict compliance with s.5 Cap 30 — witnesses must be present together
2. **No estate duty**: Remind clients this does not mean no costs — probate fees, stamp duty, and legal fees still apply
3. **Family provision**: Always consider Cap 481 claims — spouses and children may challenge an unfavourable will
4. **Cross-border assets**: HK wills do not cover foreign real estate — advise on separate wills for foreign jurisdictions
5. **MPF nominations**: Not testamentary — check MPF scheme rules
6. **Trustee investment**: The wide power under s.16–16C Cap 29 is useful but requires proper advice
7. **Variation of trusts**: Use s.36 (maintenance) and s.37 (advancement) for minor beneficiaries
8. **Mediation**: Contentious probate and trust cases are encouraged to mediate before litigation

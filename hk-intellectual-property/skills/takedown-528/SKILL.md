# Copyright Takedown — Skill

## Purpose

Guide copyright owners and online service providers (OSPs) through the notice-and-takedown procedure under the Copyright Ordinance (Cap 528) ss.88–91, including OSP safe harbour compliance, takedown notice drafting, and counter-notification procedures.

## Scope

- OSP safe harbour conditions (s.88)
- Limitations on remedies for caching (s.89)
- Limitations on remedies for storage at direction of user (s.90)
- Limitations on remedies for information location tools (s.91)
- Notice-and-takedown procedure
- OSP compliance requirements
- Counter-notification and put-back
- Repeat infringer policy
- Damages and costs for improper takedowns

## Workflow

### 1. Determine Whether the OSP Qualifies for Safe Harbour

**Requirements (s.88(1)–(3)):**

| Condition | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Repeat infringer policy (s.88(1)(a)) | OSP must adopt and implement a policy to terminate accounts of repeat infringers in appropriate circumstances |
| Accommodation of standard technical measures (s.88(1)(b)) | OSP must accommodate and not interfere with standard technical measures used to identify/protect copyright works |
| Designated complaint agent (s.88(2)–(3)) | OSP must designate a representative to receive infringement notifications; publish contact details on its service |

**If these conditions are met**, the OSP may benefit from limitations on remedies for specific activities (ss.89–91).

### 2. Identify the Relevant Activity

| Activity | Section | Limitation on Remedies |
|----------|---------|------------------------|
| Caching | s.89 | No monetary relief for automatic intermediate/transient storage if conditions met |
| Storage at direction of user | s.90 | No monetary relief for storage of infringing material by user if OSP acts expeditiously on notice |
| Information location tools | s.91 | No monetary relief for linking/referring users to infringing material if OSP acts expeditiously on notice |

**Note**: Safe harbour provides a limitation on **monetary remedies** (damages, account of profits) but does not prevent injunctive relief or delivery up orders.

### 3. Draft a Takedown Notice (Copyright Owner)

A proper takedown notice under ss.90–91 should include:

- **Identification of the copyright work claimed to be infringed**
- **Identification of the infringing material** and information sufficient to locate it (URL, file path, screenshot)
- **Statement of good faith belief** that the use is not authorised by the copyright owner, agent, or the law
- **Statement of accuracy** — information in the notice is accurate
- **Signature** (physical or electronic) of the copyright owner or authorised representative
- **Contact information** — name, address, telephone, email of the complaining party

**Sample Notice Structure:**

```
[Date]

To: [OSP Name — Designated Copyright Agent]
[OSP Address / Email]

1. Copyright Work(s) Infringed:
   [Title, author, publication date, registration number if applicable]

2. Infringing Material:
   URL(s): [list of URLs]
   Description: [nature of infringement, e.g., "unauthorised reproduction of the entire work"]

3. Statement of Good Faith Belief:
   I have a good faith belief that the use of the material described above is not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

4. Statement of Accuracy:
   The information in this notification is accurate to the best of my knowledge.

5. Signature:
   [Electronic signature]

6. Contact:
   Name: [Complainant name / company]
   Address: [Full address]
   Email: [Email address]
   Telephone: [Contact number]

7. Relationship to Copyright Work:
   [e.g., "I am the copyright owner" / "I am authorised to act on behalf of the copyright owner"]
```

### 4. OSP Response to Takedown Notice

Upon receipt of a notice that **substantially complies** with s.90/91:

1. **Act expeditiously** to remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material
2. **Notify the user** whose material has been removed (provide copy of notice)
3. **Preserve evidence** — do not destroy the material or related data
4. **Maintain records** of the notice and the OSP's action

**Consequences of non-compliance:**
- OSP loses safe harbour protection for monetary remedies
- OSP may face primary or contributory infringement liability
- OSP may be ordered to disclose subscriber identity

### 5. Counter-Notification (User)

A user whose material was removed may submit a counter-notification:

- Identify the material removed and its location before removal
- Statement of good faith belief that the material was removed due to mistake or misidentification
- Consent to jurisdiction of CFI
- Signature of the user

**OSP response to counter-notification:**

1. **Put-back**: The OSP is not required to restore the material automatically; the OSP should assess the counter-notification
2. **Notice to copyright owner**: Provide the counter-notification to the copyright owner
3. **If the copyright owner files a court action** within 14 business days, keep the material down
4. **Otherwise**: The OSP may restore the material at its discretion

### 6. Repeat Infringer Policy

Under s.88(1)(a), OSPs must implement a policy that:

- Provides for termination of accounts of repeat infringers
- "Appropriate circumstances" — typically after multiple valid takedown notices against the same user
- Courts consider: whether the policy is documented and enforced, whether the OSP acted reasonably

**Best Practice:**
- Publish the policy in the OSP's terms of service
- Track infringement notices per user account
- Implement graduated response: warning → suspension → termination
- Document all enforcement actions
- Apply policy consistently

### 7. Risks for Copyright Owners

- **Improper takedown**: A copyright owner who makes a false or improper takedown notice may be liable for:
  - Damages (including costs) to the OSP or user
  - Potential liability for abuse of process
  - Malicious prosecution if bad faith

- **Costs in counter-notification proceedings**: Court may order costs against the copyright owner if the infringement claim is not borne out

### 8. Risks for OSPs

- **Failing to implement s.88 conditions**: Loss of safe harbour for all four activities
- **Disclosing user identity without order**: Data privacy liability under Cap 486
- **Returning material after counter-notification**: Risk of being joined in infringement proceedings
- **Not acting expeditiously**: Courts consider what is reasonable in the circumstances; typically 24–72 hours for clear notices

## Practice Guidance

1. **Safe harbour is not automatic** — an OSP must proactively adopt and implement the conditions in s.88
2. **Designate a complaint agent** and publish contact details prominently (the IPD website may also be used for IP-related OSP contact, but the OSP's own publication is required)
3. **Notice requirements are not rigid** — courts assess substantial compliance, but clearer notices reduce litigation risk
4. **Expeditious action** — define "expeditiously" based on the nature of the infringement and the OSP's capacity; 24 hours for clear copyright infringements is a standard benchmark
5. **Formal takedown procedures** reduce the risk both for OSPs and copyright owners; have standard notice and counter-notice templates available
6. **Privacy issues** — OSPs should not disclose user identity without a court order or the user's consent; the notice-and-takedown does not require the OSP to disclose identity
7. **Document everything** — OSPs should keep a register of all notices received, actions taken, and counter-notifications
8. **The safe harbour is not the only defence** — OSPs may also rely on s.40 (incidental inclusion) or ss.79–80 (private copying) in appropriate circumstances

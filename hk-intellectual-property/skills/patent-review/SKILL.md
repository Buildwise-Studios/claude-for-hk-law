# Patent Review — Skill

## Purpose

Analyse patent validity and infringement for standard and short-term patents under the Patents Ordinance (Cap 514) in Hong Kong, and develop enforcement or defence strategies.

## Scope

- Standard patents (re-registration of EPO/UK/PRC designated patents)
- Short-term patents (HK-only: 8-year term)
- Patentability requirements (novelty, inventive step, industrial applicability)
- Infringement analysis (literal / equivalents)
- Validity challenges (revocation / invalidity)
- Defences (experimental use, prior use, exhaustion)
- Forthcoming changes under the Patents (Amendment) Ordinance 2024

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the Patent

- Standard patent: note the underlying designated patent (EPO, UK, or PRC grant number) and HK re-registration number
- Short-term patent: note the HK filing date and patent number
- Determine patent status: in force, expired, lapsed, revoked
- Check renewal status and current proprietor
- Check patent family for related rights

### 2. Identify the Alleged Infringing Product/Process

- Obtain product samples, specifications, or process descriptions
- Map product/process features to patent claims
- Identify which claims are allegedly infringed (independent and dependent claims)

### 3. Claim Construction

- Approach: HK courts follow purposive construction (Catnic principles, Improver questions / Actavis approach)
- Read claim in context of specification and drawings
- Determine whether the alleged product/process falls within the scope of the claim (literal infringement) or is a non-textual equivalent

### 4. Assess Infringement

| Type | Test |
|------|------|
| Literal | Alleged product/process falls precisely within claim wording |
| Equivalents (non-textual) | Does the variant achieve substantially the same result in substantially the same way? Would a person skilled in the art understand the patentee intended strict compliance? |

**Acts constituting infringement (s.76–78):**
- Making, disposing of, offering to dispose of, using, importing, keeping a patented product
- Using a patented process or offering for use knowing/when obvious the process is patented
- Disposing of, offering to dispose of, using, importing, keeping a product obtained directly from a patented process

**Contributory infringement (s.77):**
- Supplying means relating to an essential element of the invention
- Knowing or when reasonable to know the means are suitable for putting the invention into effect

### 5. Assess Patentability (for Validity Challenge)

**Requirements (s.8):**
- **Novelty**: absolute novelty (worldwide prior art before filing/priority date)
  - Prior art: everything made available to the public anywhere in the world
  - State of the art includes prior-filed, later-published applications (same jurisdiction)
  - **Grace period**: 6-month pre-filing disclosure in certain cases (certain international exhibitions — s.9)
- **Inventive step**: not obvious to a person skilled in the art having regard to the state of the art
- **Industrial applicability**: capable of being made or used in any kind of industry

**Excluded subject matter (s.8(3)–(4)):**
- Discoveries, scientific theories, mathematical methods
- Aesthetic creations
- Schemes, rules, methods for mental acts, games, business methods
- Computer programs "as such"
- Presentations of information
- Diagnostic, therapeutic, and surgical methods (but not products for use in such methods)

### 6. Validity Challenge for Standard Patents

**Grounds for revocation (s.80):**
- Not patentable (lacks novelty, inventive step, industrial applicability, excluded subject matter)
- Patentee not entitled to the patent
- Specification does not disclose the invention clearly and completely
- Matter in claim extends beyond content of application as filed
- Protection conferred by claim not justified by the specification

**Procedure:**
- Apply to the CFI for revocation
- Counterclaim in infringement proceedings (most common)
- Standard patent validity may also be challenged in underlying designated patent jurisdiction

### 7. Short-Term Patent Specifics

**Features:**
- Maximum 8 claims (1 independent, up to 7 dependent)
- No substantive examination by IPD
- Term: 8 years (initial 4 + renewal of 4)
- Can only be challenged by: revocation petition to CFI or as counterclaim in infringement
- No opposition system

**Strategic Use:**
- Quick protection for incremental innovations with short commercial life
- Useful where designated patent process is too slow (standard patent depends on EPO/UK/PRC grant)
- Higher risk — enforce at own peril if patent is later invalidated

### 8. Defences to Infringement

| Defence | Section | Scope |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Experimental use | s.78B | Acts done for experimental purposes relating to the subject matter of the invention |
| Private non-commercial use | s.78A(2)(a) | Private and for purposes which are not commercial |
| Prior use | s.79 | Continued use where the defendant had already used the invention before the priority date |
| Exhaustion of rights | s.80 | Products put on market in HK by patentee or with consent |
| Compulsory licence | ss.83–86 | Available when patent not being worked in HK |
| Crown use | s.92 | Government use for public services |

### 9. Remedies

| Remedy | Availability | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| Injunction | Discretionary — common if infringement established | Interlocutory or final |
| Damages | Patented's election (or account of profits) | Lost profits or reasonable royalty |
| Account of profits | Alternative to damages | Defendant's actual profits from infringement |
| Declaration | Standard | Court may declare infringement and validity |
| Delivery up / destruction | Available | Usually with injunction |
| Costs | Standard | Typical costs order for successful party |

**Note on infringement after 2024 amendments**: The Patents (Amendment) Ordinance 2024 introduces a copyright-style OSP safe harbour for online infringement, though patent infringement remains civil-only (no criminal liability).

### 10. Enforcement Strategy

- **Primary**: CFI claim for injunction + damages + delivery up
- **Border measures**: Limited — no patent border seizure regime in HK (unlike trade marks/copyright)
- **Alternative**: Short-term patent holders may apply for urgent relief if the patent is under challenge
- **Cross-border**: No cross-border enforcement of HK patent rights in PRC; separate PRC patent needed

## Key Cases

- **Glaxo Group Ltd v Dowelhurst Ltd** — Exhaustion of rights / parallel imports
- **Kabushiki Kaisha Toshiba (Toshiba Corporation) v HKC International Ltd** — Design infringement (patent equivalency)
- **Re Vertex Pharmaceuticals' Patent** — Standard patent re-registration requirements
- **Immuno AG v Immuno Quality Products Ltd** — Passing off (IP overlap)

## Practice Guidance

1. **Standard patent depends on designated patent** — validity of underlying EPO/UK/PRC patent is relevant but not determinative; HK court conducts independent analysis
2. **Short-term patent enforcement carries risk** — if asserted and later invalidated, the patentee may face costs sanctions and damages for unjustified threats
3. **Exhaustion is international** — once the patentee (or licensee) markets the product anywhere, HK patent rights in that product are exhausted (s.80)
4. **No criminal patent enforcement** — unlike trade marks/copyright, patent infringement is purely civil
5. **Claim construction is key** — HK courts apply purposive construction; expert evidence on the person skilled in the art is essential
6. **Short-term patents** — maximum 8 claims; ensure they are carefully drafted as the patent cannot be amended post-grant
7. **Always check the designated patent** — for standard patents, the underlying EPO/UK/PRC patent must be in force

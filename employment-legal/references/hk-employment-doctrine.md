## Legislative Framework

### Employment Ordinance (Cap 57)

The principal legislation governing employment relationships in Hong Kong. Key provisions:

#### Contract of Employment (Part II)
- **Continuous employment:** An employee who has been employed continuously for 4 weeks or more, with at least 18 hours worked per week, is deemed to be under a continuous contract of employment (the "418" rule).
- **Written contract requirements:** Cap 57 s.42C requires employers to provide employees with a written contract containing specified particulars (wage, allowance, end-of-year payment, termination notice, hours, holiday entitlement, etc.).
- **Variation of terms:** Contract terms can only be varied by mutual agreement. Unilateral variation by the employer may constitute constructive dismissal.

#### Wages and Wage Protection (Part III–IVA)
- **Definition of wages:** All remuneration, earnings, allowances, commission, overtime pay, tips, and service charges.
- **Wage period:** Not more than 1 month. Wages fall due on the last day of the wage period.
- **Wage deduction restrictions:** Cap 57 s.32 — only permitted deductions for: absence from work, damage/loss (max HK$300 per incident or 25% of wages in that period), repayment of advances, and deductions required by law (e.g., MPF).
- **Statutory Minimum Wage:** Cap 608 — currently HK$40 per hour `[verify current rate]`.

#### Termination of Employment (Part V)

**Notice periods (Cap 57 s.6):**
| Continuous employment | Notice period |
|---|---|
| 1 month or more | 7 days (or contractual if longer) |
| After 1st month | 7 days |
| 1-3 years | 7 days |
| 3-5 years | 14 days |
| 5+ years | 1 month |

**Summary dismissal (Cap 57 s.9):** Without notice where employee is guilty of gross misconduct (dishonesty, fraud, serious insubordination, wilful disobedience).

**Wrongful dismissal (Cap 57 ss. 32A–32P):** An employee with 2+ years continuous employment who is dismissed for reasons other than those specified in s.32K (redundancy, conduct, capability, etc.) and who considers the dismissal unreasonable may apply for reinstatement or a terminal payment.

**Pregnancy dismissal (Cap 57 s.15):** An employer who dismisses a pregnant employee commits an offence. The employee is entitled to damages of 3x the contract value (up to HK$150,000 or the amount claimed, whichever is higher, in addition to normal entitlements).

**Protected reasons:** Trade union membership, sickness absence (with medical certificate), filing Labour Tribunal claims, giving evidence in employment proceedings.

#### Statutory Holidays and Leave (Parts IIIA, VA, VIIIA, IX)

**Statutory holidays:** 12 per year (Cap 57 Schedule 3). Employer may grant substituted holidays by agreement.

**Paid annual leave (Cap 57 s.41A):**
| Continuous employment | Annual leave entitlement |
|---|---|
| 1-2 years | 7 days |
| 2-3 years | 8 days |
| Increases by 1 day per year of service | Up to a maximum of 14 days at 9+ years |

**Paid sickness days (Cap 57 s.33):**
- Accumulation rate: 2 paid sickness days per month of completed service during first 12 months; 4 days per month thereafter.
- Maximum accumulation: 120 days.
- Entitlement: After collecting the requisite number of paid sickness days (1st stage: 36 days; 2nd stage: 48 days), the employee is entitled to 4/5 pay for each paid sickness day taken, provided the sickness lasts at least 4 consecutive days and a medical certificate is produced.

**Maternity protection (Cap 57 Part VIIIA):**
- Continuous employment: 4 weeks or more.
- Entitlement: 14 weeks of maternity leave (as of 2022 amendments).
- Pay: 4/5 of normal wages for 14 weeks.
- Protection from dismissal during pregnancy and maternity leave (Cap 57 s.15 — criminal offence).
- Breastfeeding breaks: Entitled to a 30-minute paid breastfeeding break per shift.

**Paternity leave (Cap 57 Part IX):**
- Continuous employment: 4 weeks or more.
- Entitlement: 5 days of paternity leave.
- Pay: 4/5 of normal wages.

#### Severance Payment (Part VA, Cap 57 ss. 31B–31P)

**Entitlement:**
- Employee employed under a continuous contract for at least 24 months
- Dismissed by reason of redundancy or laid off
- OR: employee's employment is terminated by the employer or the employee terminates the contract due to redundancy

**Calculation:**
- For employees under a continuous contract:
  - Age 40 or above: 2/3 × (last month's wages or HK$22,500, whichever is lower) × years of service
  - Under 40: 1/2 × (last month's wages or HK$22,500, whichever is lower) × years of service
- Maximum calculation: Cap on monthly wages = HK$22,500; cap on years of service = no statutory cap.
- Maximum severance payment: HK$390,000 `[verify]`.

**Offset:** Severance payment can be offset by employer's MPF contributions (non-voluntary portion).

#### Long Service Payment (Part VB, Cap 57 ss. 31Q–31ZB)

**Entitlement:**
- Employee with 5+ years continuous employment
- Dismissed by employer (not by reason of redundancy or summary dismissal)
- OR: employee resigns on grounds of ill health with medical certification
- OR: employee aged 65+ who retires

**Calculation:** Same formula as severance payment. LSP and severance payment are mutually exclusive.

#### End-of-Year Payment (Part IIA, Cap 57 ss. 13–13C)

- Contractual end-of-year payment (often called "double pay" or "13th month") is protected
- Pro-rata entitlement on dismissal (unless summary dismissal for misconduct)
- Ex-gratia payments are not protected under Cap 57

### Mandatory Provident Fund Schemes Ordinance (Cap 485)

- **Mandatory contributions:** 5% of relevant income (current max HK$1,500 per month `[verify]`) from both employer and employee.
- **Voluntary contributions:** Additional contributions above the mandatory minimum.
- **Relevant income:** Monthly income between HK$7,100 and HK$30,000 (current thresholds `[verify]`).
- **MPF offsetting:** Severance and LSP can be reduced by employer's MPF contributions. Note: this offset mechanism has been abolished as of May 2025 `[verify current status]`.

### Employees' Compensation Ordinance (Cap 282)

**Scope:** Covers employees who suffer injury by accident arising out of and in the course of employment.

**Employer's liability:** Strict liability — no need to prove fault.

**Compensation (s. 6–12):**

| Category | Compensation |
|---|---|
| Permanent total incapacity | Percentage of monthly earnings based on age, capped at certain multiples |
| Permanent partial incapacity | Percentage of total incapacity amount based on loss of earning capacity |
| Temporary incapacity | 4/5 of monthly earnings (max period specified) |
| Medical expenses | Up to prescribed caps |
| Fatal cases | Compensation to dependants (age-based multiples) |

**Notice and claims:** Employee must notify employer within a reasonable time. Claims must be made within 24 months of the accident.

**Employer's duty:** Must maintain employees' compensation insurance under Cap 282 s.40.

**Rehabilitation:** Employer must offer suitable employment for employees recovering from work injuries.

### Anti-Discrimination Framework

| Ordinance | Protected characteristic | Prohibited conduct |
|---|---|---|
| Sex Discrimination Ordinance (Cap 480) | Sex, marital status, pregnancy, breastfeeding | Direct/indirect discrimination, harassment, victimisation |
| Disability Discrimination Ordinance (Cap 487) | Disability (physical, mental, intellectual) | Direct/indirect discrimination, harassment, vilification |
| Family Status Discrimination Ordinance (Cap 527) | Family responsibilities | Direct/indirect discrimination |
| Race Discrimination Ordinance (Cap 602) | Race, colour, descent, ethnic origin | Direct/indirect discrimination, harassment, vilification |

**Enforcement:** Equal Opportunities Commission (EOC) investigates and conciliates. Complaints filed in the District Court.

**Burden of proof:** Once the complainant establishes a prima facie case, the burden shifts to the respondent.

**Remedies:** Damages (including injury to feelings), injunction, declaratory orders, apology, reinstatement or re-engagement.

### Trade Unions

- **Trade Union Ordinance (Cap 332):** Governs registration and regulation of trade unions.
- **Right to join:** Employees have the right to join and participate in trade union activities.
- **Protection from discrimination:** Unlawful to dismiss, penalise, or threaten an employee for trade union membership or activities (Cap 57 s.32B).
- **Collective bargaining:** No statutory framework for collective bargaining in HK (unlike the UK's statutory recognition procedure). Collective agreements are generally not legally enforceable unless incorporated into individual contracts.

### Labour Tribunal

- **Jurisdiction:** Monetary claims arising from employment (up to HK$8,000 per claimant for some claim types; typically no upper limit for Cap 57 claims).
- **Procedure:** Pre-hearing conference, mediation, adjudication hearing. Legal representation is not permitted at the initial stages unless both parties consent or the tribunal grants leave.
- **No costs regime:** Generally no order for costs (unlike District Court / CFI).
- **Time limit:** Claims must be filed within 12 months of the cause of action (Cap 25, s.9).

#### Key HK Labour Tribunal vs District Court comparison

| Aspect | Labour Tribunal | District Court |
|---|---|---|
| Claim limit | Monetary claims (Cap 57) - generally up to HK$8,000 per claimant for some types; no upper limit for Cap 57 matters | HK$3M (civil) / unlimited for employment? |
| Representation | No (unless leave granted) | Yes |
| Costs | No order generally | Costs follow the event |
| Time limit | 12 months | 6 years (contract) / 3 years (PI) |
| Appeals | To CFI on a point of law | CA on law / CFI by leave |

### Common Law Principles

- **Restrictive covenants (non-compete, non-solicit):** Enforceable only if: legitimate business interest, reasonable in scope/geography/duration, and not contrary to public policy. HK courts apply the "blue pencil" test for severance of unenforceable parts.
- **Constructive dismissal:** Employee is entitled to resign without notice where the employer's conduct breaches an essential term of the contract (e.g., unilateral wage reduction, fundamental change in role).
- **Good faith:** Common law implies a duty of mutual trust and confidence into employment contracts (Hong Kong follows English common law on this).
- **Fiduciary duties:** Senior employees may owe fiduciary duties to the employer (duty of loyalty, duty to avoid conflicts of interest).

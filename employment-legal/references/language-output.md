# Language and output (Hong Kong practice)

Read `## Language` from the active plugin practice profile (`~/.claude/plugins/config/claude-for-hk-law/<plugin>/CLAUDE.md`) before client-facing or court-facing deliverables.

## Defaults

- **Primary analysis language:** English unless the profile says otherwise.
- **Client-facing language:** English unless the profile specifies `zh-Hant` or `bilingual`.
- **Court filings and formal pleadings:** English unless the matter or court direction requires otherwise.

## When profile says `zh-Hant` or `bilingual`

- Legal analysis and reviewer notes may stay in English (firm standard).
- Client letters, plain-language summaries, and DSAR cover letters may use **Traditional Chinese** where the profile requests it.
- Use plain language; avoid Latin tags unless the audience expects them.
- For ordinances, cite in English (Cap number + section) and optionally give the Chinese short title in parentheses on first mention.

## Chinese-prevails sources

For Basic Law and NPCSC interpretations, the **Chinese text prevails** if there is a discrepancy (Basic Law Art 9; Interpretation and General Clauses Ordinance (Cap 1)). Flag: `[Chinese text prevails — verify against official Chinese version on elegislation.gov.hk]`.

## What this file does not do

- It does not machine-translate entire contracts or judgments.
- It does not replace a bilingual solicitor review for filings in Chinese.

# /hk-litigation-procedure:enforcement-guide

1. Load `CLAUDE.md` for the enforcement framework (RHC O.45–51).
2. Gather: judgment details, debtor identity and location, known assets, and time since judgment.
3. Walk the enforcement decision tree.
4. Produce an enforcement plan ranked by likely effectiveness and cost.

## Enforcement Decision Tree

### Step 1: Check Preliminary Requirements

| Requirement | Check |
|---|---|
| Judgment is final and subsisting | [ ] |
| Time for appeal has expired (or no stay) | [ ] |
| Within 6-year enforcement period (Cap 347 s.4(4)) | [ ] |
| Judgment debtor identified and locatable | [ ] |
| Assets identified or traceable | [ ] |

- If judgment is more than 6 years old: must apply for leave to enforce (O.46, r.2).
- If leave is required: affidavit explaining delay, why enforcement should be permitted, and that judgment has not been satisfied.

### Step 2: Enforce Within HK

#### 1. Writ of Fieri Facias (fi fa) — O.47

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Seizes and sells the judgment debtor's goods and chattels |
| **When to use** | Debtor has physical assets (vehicles, equipment, inventory, office contents) |
| **Procedure** | Leave to issue writ (O.45, r.1); writ issued; Bailiff executes |
| **Limitations** | Cannot seize land or money; limited to chattels found at the execution address |
| **Costs** | Bailiff fees; typically HK$500–1,000 |
| **Recovery likelihood** | Medium — depends on debtor having seizable goods at a known location |

#### 2. Garnishee Order — O.49

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Seizes debts owed to the judgment debtor by a third party (e.g., bank account, trade debts) |
| **When to use** | Debtor has known bank accounts or receivables |
| **Procedure** | Ex parte application for garnishee order nisi (show cause); if third party confirms the debt, order absolute |
| **Limitations** | Cannot garnish joint accounts; third party must be within HK jurisdiction |
| **Costs** | Application costs (typically HK$2,000–5,000) |
| **Recovery likelihood** | High — if the bank account is identified and has funds |

#### 3. Charging Order — O.50

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Imposes a charge on the judgment debtor's land, securities, or units of trust |
| **When to use** | Debtor owns HK property, shares, or listed securities |
| **Procedure** | Ex parte application for charging order nisi; order absolute unless cause is shown |
| **Limitations** | Charge does not give possession — must be enforced by sale order (separate application) |
| **Costs** | HK$3,000–10,000 |
| **Recovery likelihood** | High — property is difficult to hide, but sale is time-consuming |

#### 4. Examination of Judgment Debtor — O.48

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Court orders the debtor to attend and disclose assets under oath |
| **When to use** | As a preliminary step — learn what assets exist before choosing enforcement method |
| **Procedure** | Ex parte application for examination order; debtor attends court; Transcript obtained |
| **Limitations** | Debtor may be evasive; contempt for non-attendance but enforcement is slow |
| **Costs** | Low (HK$1,000–3,000) |
| **Recovery likelihood** | N/A (information-gathering tool only) |

#### 5. Appointment of Receiver — O.51

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Court appoints a receiver to collect income or manage assets |
| **When to use** | Debtor has income streams (rent, dividends, partnership profits) |
| **Procedure** | Summons supported by affidavit |
| **Limitations** | Expensive (receiver's fees); requires identifiable income stream |
| **Costs** | HK$10,000–30,000 |
| **Recovery likelihood** | Medium — depends on reliable income stream |

#### 6. Stop Order — O.50, r.12

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Prevents dealing with specified securities or funds |
| **When to use** | Debtor holds listed shares or government funds |
| **Procedure** | Ex parte application |

#### 7. Bankruptcy / Winding-Up

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it does** | Bankruptcy petition (individual) or winding-up petition (company) |
| **When to use** | Debtor cannot pay; threats of bankruptcy may prompt payment |
| **Procedure** | Statutory demand → bankruptcy petition → (winding-up petition for companies) |
| **Costs** | HK$5,000–15,000 |
| **Recovery likelihood** | High pressure tool; actual recovery depends on bankruptcy/winding-up realisation. Statutory demand for HK$10,000+ (individual) / HK$5,000+ (company). |

### Step 3: Enforce Outside HK

#### Mainland China

**Arrangement on Reciprocal Enforcement of Judgments in Civil and Commercial Matters (2019):**

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Scope** | HK CFI, DC, CA, CFA judgments — final and enforceable |
| **Excluded** | Employment judgments, matrimonial, some IP, certain bankruptcy orders |
| **Procedure** | Application to the Hong Kong CFI for a certified copy + certificate → file in Intermediate People's Court in the Mainland |
| **Time limit** | 2 years from the date of the HK judgment (or last payment date) |
| **Recognition** | Mainland court examines the judgment for compliance but does NOT re-hear the merits |
| **Grounds for refusal** | Limited — jurisdiction, procedural fairness, fraud, public policy |
| **Recovery likelihood** | High (but time-consuming) |

#### Other Jurisdictions

- **Common law jurisdictions (UK, Australia, Canada, Singapore, etc.):** Action on the HK judgment (simple contract — 6 years).
- **Civil law jurisdictions:** Check bilateral or multilateral enforcement treaties.
- **US:** Separate action on the judgment under state law (varies by state).
- **Enforcement costs:** Higher — local solicitors, translation, service, and filing fees.

### Step 4: Time Limits for Enforcement

| Method | Time limit |
|---|---|
| Writ of fi fa (fresh) | 6 years from judgment (O.46, r.2 — leave required after) |
| Garnishee order | 6 years |
| Charging order | 6 years |
| Bankruptcy petition | Based on judgment debt (statutory debt) |
| China enforcement (Mainland) | 2 years from HK judgment |
| Action on HK judgment abroad | Varies — typically 6 years (common law) |

## Output

### Enforcement Plan

| Rank | Method | Cost | Likelihood | Time | Asset requirement |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | O.48 Examination | HK$1-3K | — | 2-4 weeks | N/A — discovery tool |
| 2 | Garnishee (bank account) | HK$2-5K | High | 4-8 weeks | Identified account |
| 3 | Charging order (property) | HK$3-10K | High | 8-12 weeks | Known HK property |
| 4 | Writ of fi fa | HK$500-1K | Medium | 4-6 weeks | Known chattels |
| 5 | Bankruptcy/Winding-up | HK$5-15K | High (pressure) | 3-6 months | Unsecured debt |
| 6 | China enforcement | HK$15-30K | Medium-High | 6-12 months | Mainland assets |

### Recommended Action Sequence

1. **Examine judgment debtor** (O.48) — identify assets
2. **Freeze bank accounts** (garnishee) — fastest recovery if funds exist
3. **Charge property** (charging order + sale) — if property exists
4. **Consider bankruptcy/winding-up** — pressure tool, may prompt settlement
5. **Consider China enforcement** — if Mainland assets identified

**Append:** `⚠️ Enforcement is fact-dependent and subject to the debtor's asset position, cooperation, and any appeals or challenges. This is a guide only — specific legal advice should be sought before taking enforcement steps. Review with a licensed HK solicitor or barrister.`

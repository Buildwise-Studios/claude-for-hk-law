# Hong Kong Court Structure & Hierarchy

> A reference guide to the Hong Kong court system, jurisdiction limits, appeal routes, and types of matters heard.
>
> **Last updated:** June 2026

---

## Hierarchy Overview

```
Court of Final Appeal (CFA)
  ┌── Chief Justice
  ├── 3 Permanent Judges (PJ)
  └── 1 Non-Permanent Judge (NPJ)*
       ↑ Appeals on final and interlocutory matters

High Court
  ├── Court of Appeal (CA)
  └── Court of First Instance (CFI)
       ↑ Appeals from DC, Magistrates, and statutory tribunals

District Court (DC)
  ├── Family Court
  └── Lands Tribunal
       ↑ Appeals from Magistrates and minor tribunals

Magistrates' Courts
  ├── Juvenile Court
  ├── Coroner's Court
  └── (also: Minor Employment Claims, Obscene Articles, etc.)

Tribunals (see below for full list)
```

*\* NPJs are drawn from a panel of distinguished judges from Hong Kong and other common law jurisdictions (typically UK, Australia, New Zealand, Canada).*

---

## 1. Court of Final Appeal (Cap 484)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Composition** | Chief Justice + 3 Permanent Judges + 1 Non-Permanent Judge (selected from panel of NPJs) |
| **Civil Jurisdiction** | Appeals in final matters where the amount in dispute ≥ HK$1,000,000 **OR** leave is granted by the CFA (or CA if CFA refuses) |
| **Criminal Jurisdiction** | Appeals as of right from CA; leave required for interlocutory matters |
| **Appeal Route** | From CA (civil) or from CA/CFI (criminal) |
| **Finality** | Final court of appeal — no further appeal within Hong Kong |
| **Clerks** | Registrar of the CFA (administrative) |

### Key Notes
- The CFA may refer questions of interpretation of the Basic Law (arts. 158–159) to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress (NPCSC).
- CFA judges are appointed by the Chief Executive on the recommendation of the Judicial Officers Recommendation Commission (JORC).
- CFA hearings are conducted by a panel of 5 judges; in rare cases, a Full Court of 7 may sit.

## 2. High Court

### 2A. Court of Appeal (CA) — Cap 4, Part II

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Composition** | Chief Judge of the High Court (President of CA) + Justices of Appeal |
| **Jurisdiction** | Hears appeals from CFI, DC, and certain tribunals |
| **Panel** | Usually 3 judges (civil); 2 or 3 judges (criminal); single judge for interlocutory |
| **Civil Appeals** | Appeal as of right from CFI; leave required from DC |
| **Criminal Appeals** | Appeal as of right against conviction; leave required for sentence unless specified |
| **Outcome** | May affirm, reverse, vary, order retrial, or substitute decision |

### 2B. Court of First Instance (CFI) — Cap 4, Part III

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Composition** | Chief Judge of the High Court + Judges of the CFI (including Recorders and Deputy Judges) |
| **Civil Jurisdiction** | Unlimited — any amount; includes admiralty, IP, commercial, constitutional, and judicial review |
| **Criminal Jurisdiction** | All indictable offences (most serious: murder, manslaughter, drug trafficking, commercial fraud, NSL cases) — trial by jury |
| **Jury** | 7 jurors (9 with unanimous verdict); majority verdicts (5–2) after 6 hours deliberation |
| **Judicial Review** | Exclusive jurisdiction for applications for leave to apply for judicial review (Order 53, Rules of the High Court) |
| **Constitutional** | Hears Basic Law and HKBORO challenges |

## 3. District Court (DC) — Cap 336

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Composition** | Chief District Judge + Principal Family Court Judge + District Judges |
| **Civil Jurisdiction** | Up to **HK$3,000,000** (since Dec 2024; previously HK$1M) |
| **Criminal Jurisdiction** | Most indictable offences **except** murder, manslaughter, rape, certain serious drug offences — max **7 years' imprisonment** |
| **Trial** | Judge alone (no jury in DC) |
| **Family Court** | Divorce, custody, maintenance, ancillary relief, adoption — no monetary limit for children matters |
| **Lands Tribunal** | Rent, compensation, valuation, building management disputes |

### Key Notes
- DC handles ~90% of civil cases by volume.
- Employment claims up to HK$200,000 may be heard in DC (though most go to Labour Tribunal).
- Appeals from DC go to CA (civil) or CFA (criminal on law/conviction — rare).

---

## 4. Magistrates' Courts — Cap 227

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Composition** | Chief Magistrate + Permanent Magistrates + Special Magistrates |
| **Criminal Jurisdiction** | Summary offences + triable-either-way offences; max **2 years' imprisonment** (or 3 yrs for multiple charges) |
| **Committal** | Sends serious indictable offences to CFI or DC for trial |
| **Procedure** | Judge alone; no jury |
| **Specialist Courts** | — |

### Subordinate Courts within Magistrates' Court System

#### Juvenile Court
- Hears charges against persons aged 10–15 (youth offenders up to 18 in some cases)
- Closed hearings; limited reporting; parental involvement required
- Emphasis on welfare and rehabilitation

#### Coroner's Court
- Investigates violent, unnatural, or sudden deaths; deaths in custody
- Coroner sits with a jury (3 or 5 members) for public interest inquests
- Verdicts: natural causes, accident, misadventure, suicide, unlawful killing, open, or narrative

## 5. Tribunals

A range of specialist tribunals with limited jurisdiction:

| Tribunal | Jurisdiction | Max Amount / Powers | Appeal Route |
|----------|--------------|--------------------|--------------|
| **Small Claims Tribunal** | Monetary claims ≤ **HK$75,000** | HK$75,000 | CFI (on law only) |
| **Labour Tribunal** | Employment claims (wages, holiday pay, severance) — **no monetary limit** but must be ≤ HK$200,000 for expedited track | DM discretion | CFI (on law only) |
| **Lands Tribunal** | Rent, building management, compensation (Crown/university), rating appeals | No limit (property-linked) | CA (civil) |
| **Obscene Articles Tribunal** | Classification of articles as obscene/indecent; exhibition of articles for criminal proceedings | Classification only | CA (judicial review) |
| **Market Misconduct Tribunal** | Insider dealing, market manipulation under SFO (Cap 571) | Disqualification, disgorgement, fines | CA (on law) |
| **Competition Tribunal** | Anti-competitive conduct under CompO (Cap 619) | Fines, disqualification, damages | CA (on law) |

## Appeal Route Summary

```
CFA
 ↑ (final value > HK$1M or leave)
CA
 ↑ (leave required from DC)
CFI
 ↑ (law only)
DC ←─────────────────────←── Magistrates' Courts
 ↑ (leave)                            ↑
Tribunals ←──────────────────── Juvenile / Coroner's Court
```

### Simplified by Matter Type

| Starting Point | First Appeal | Second Appeal | Final Appeal |
|----------------|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| Magistrates (summary) | CFI (case stated/appeal) | CA (if leave granted) | CFA (if leave/ $1M+) |
| DC (civil < HK$3M) | CA (leave required) | — | CFA (if leave/ $1M+) |
| CFI (civil unlimited) | CA (as of right) | — | CFA (if leave/ $1M+) |
| CFI (criminal — indictable) | CA (as of right conviction) | — | CFA (if leave) |
| Tribunals (Labour, Small Claims) | CFI (law only) | — | CFA (if leave) |
| Constitutional / JR (CFI) | CA | — | CFA (if leave) |

## Key Legislation

| Instrument | Cap | Function |
|-----------|-----|----------|
| Hong Kong Court of Final Appeal Ordinance | 484 | Composition, jurisdiction, and procedure of CFA |
| High Court Ordinance | 4 | CFI and CA powers, practice, and procedure |
| District Court Ordinance | 336 | DC civil and criminal jurisdiction limits |
| Magistrates Ordinance | 227 | Summary procedure, committal, sentencing limits |
| Legal Practitioners Ordinance | 159 | Admission and regulation of solicitors and barristers |
| Arbitration Ordinance | 609 | Domestic and international arbitration (UNCITRAL Model Law) |

## Other Notable Courts & Bodies

- **Constitutional and Administrative Court** — formal designation within CFI for Basic Law / HKBORO / judicial review matters (single judge).
- **Inland Revenue Board of Review** — hears tax appeals (independent statutory body, not a court; appeal to CFI on law).
- **Securities and Futures Appeals Tribunal** — reviews SFC decisions (appeal to CA).
- **Equal Opportunities Commission** — not a court; investigates discrimination complaints; formal hearings under anti-discrimination ordinances lead to the DC.

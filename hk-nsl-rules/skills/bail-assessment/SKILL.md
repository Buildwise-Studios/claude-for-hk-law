# Bail Assessment — Skill

## Purpose

Assess bail eligibility and prepare submissions under Article 42 of the Hong Kong National Security Law (NSL), which imposes a significantly higher threshold than the ordinary HK bail regime.

## Scope

- Art 42 test: "No bail unless sufficient grounds to believe the accused will not continue to endanger national security"
- Comparison with ordinary HK bail (s.9D, Criminal Procedure Ordinance, Cap 221)
- Factors relevant to national security risk
- Prosecution submissions and SJ review rights
- Practical considerations for defence practitioners

## Workflow

### 1. Understand the Legal Test

**Ordinary HK bail** (Cap 221, s.9D):
- Presumption in favour of bail
- Court considers: flight risk, reoffending risk, interference with witnesses, failure to appear
- Burden on prosecution to show cause for detention

**NSL bail** (Art 42):
- **No presumption of bail**
- Court may grant bail only where "sufficient grounds" exist to believe the accused will not continue to endanger national security
- Burden on the defence to establish grounds for bail
- The prosecution must be heard before any bail decision
- The Secretary for Justice may apply for review of any bail decision

### 2. Gather Information

- The nature and gravity of the NSL offence charged
- The accused's connection to the alleged conduct (leadership role? participant?)
- The accused's personal circumstances (background, family, employment, community ties)
- Any previous criminal record (especially national security or public order)
- Whether the accused has any foreign connections
- Any evidence of ongoing risk (access to organisational networks, communications)
- The stage of proceedings (pre-trial / trial ongoing / post-conviction pending appeal)

### 3. Assess National Security Risk Factors

Apply the CFA framework from **HKSAR v Lai Chee Ying (2021)**:

**Factors against bail**:
- Strong prima facie case on a serious NSL offence
- Leadership or organisational role in the alleged conduct
- Access to foreign networks or financial resources
- Risk of contact with co-defendants or uncharged persons
- Risk of destruction of evidence or interference with witnesses
- Previous utterances or conduct indicating continued commitment to prohibited activities
- Extraterritorial elements (Art 65 — acts committed outside HK)

**Factors for bail**:
- Weak prima facie case
- Peripheral or limited role
- Strong community ties (family, employment, property in HK)
- Voluntary surrender or cooperation
- Good character and no prior relevant record
- Health or age considerations (with supporting medical evidence)
- Proposed bail conditions sufficiently addressing risk

### 4. Prepare Bail Submissions

**Structure for defence**:
1. Establish that the legal test under Art 42 is met — "sufficient grounds" exist
2. Address each risk factor with specific evidence or undertakings
3. Propose stringent bail conditions tailored to national security concerns:
   - Surrender of travel documents (passport, HKSAR travel permit, foreign passports)
   - No-travel condition (residence in HK)
   - Reporting to police station (daily or twice weekly)
   - No-contact orders (co-defendants, witnesses, specified persons/organisations)
   - No social media or internet restrictions
   - Electronic monitoring or home detention
   - Cash or surety (significant amount)
   - Order not to engage in specified activities
   - Restricted movement (must remain at registered address during specified hours)
4. Demonstrate that with appropriate conditions, national security risk is adequately managed

**Structure for prosecution**:
1. Analyse the Art 42 test strictly
2. Identify specific national security risk factors
3. Argue that no conditions can adequately address the risk
4. Refer to prior NSL bail decisions (Lai Chee Ying, other CFA/CA rulings)
5. Request detention pending trial

### 5. Consider SJ Review

- The SJ may apply to review any bail decision
- The review may be brought before a higher court
- Practitioners should anticipate and prepare for SJ review

### 6. Post-Decision Steps

- If bail granted: Ensure strict compliance with all conditions
- If bail refused: Note rights to appeal bail refusal (CA / CFA with leave)
- Consider periodic review if circumstances change
- Prepare for potential SJ appeal of a bail grant

## Key Precedents

**HKSAR v Lai Chee Ying (2021)** (CFA):
- Confirmed the constitutionality of Art 42's strict bail test
- Established the framework for assessing "national security risk"
- Art 42 overrides the ordinary Cap 221 bail presumption

**Other relevant cases**:
- HKSAR v Ma Chun Wai (2023) — sentencing guidelines, including pre-trial detention considerations
- Tam Sze Leung v Secretary for Justice (2022) — procedural fairness in NSL proceedings

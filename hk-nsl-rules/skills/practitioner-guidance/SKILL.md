# Practitioner Guidance — Skill

## Purpose

Provide structured guidance for legal practitioners (solicitors and barristers) advising on matters touching the Hong Kong National Security Law (NSL), covering professional obligations, ethical considerations, practice directions from the Law Society and Bar Association, and conflict management.

## Scope

- Law Society of Hong Kong guidance on NSL practice
- Hong Kong Bar Association (HKBA) statements and practice directions
- Conflict with the Legal Practitioners Ordinance (Cap 159)
- Duties of confidentiality and reporting obligations
- Duties to the court and the administration of justice
- Taking instructions and declining briefs for NSL matters
- Ethical issues in NSL cases
- Cross-border practice considerations

## Workflow

### 1. Understand the Professional Framework

**Legal Practitioners Ordinance (Cap 159)**:
- Duty of confidentiality to client (professional privilege)
- Duty to protect client interests within the bounds of the law
- Duty not to mislead the court
- Rule against acting in conflict of interest

**NSL obligations**:
- Art 4: Rights and freedoms continue, subject to legal restrictions
- Art 5: Right to defence and fair trial (qualified)
- Art 63: Organisations and individuals must cooperate with NSD
- Duty not to use legal representation to endanger national security

**Friction between Cap 159 and NSL**:
- Where confidentiality obligations conflict with national security duties, practitioners must assess carefully
- The Law Society has issued guidance on this balance
- Generally: privilege continues, but is limited where national security is engaged
- Seek senior counsel or Law Society advice in cases of doubt

### 2. Law Society Guidance (Key Principles)

The Law Society of Hong Kong has issued practice directions on NSL compliance. Key principles:

**Client intake and screening**:
- Before accepting an NSL-related brief, consider whether the matter has a national security dimension
- Assess whether you have the competence and resources to handle NSL issues
- Consider whether conflict of interest arises (direct or indirect)
- Document the screening process

**Advice and documentation**:
- All advice on NSL matters should be clearly documented
- Make contemporaneous attendance notes of client meetings
- Flag NSL risks to the client at the earliest appropriate opportunity
- Consider whether to recommend independent legal advice
- Maintain privilege — be aware of potential statutory overrides

**Duty of confidentiality**:
- Privilege continues to apply in NSL matters
- However, privilege is not absolute where national security is engaged
- The court may order disclosure where national security is prejudiced
- Consider PII in respect of sensitive material

**Reporting obligations**:
- If client instructions or conduct engage national security, consider obligations under the NSL
- The Law Society advises seeking counsel's opinion before any disclosure
- Document the decision-making process
- Do not unilaterally disclose privileged material without court order or clear statutory duty

**Declining or returning briefs**:
- A practitioner may decline a brief on grounds of competence, capacity, or conflict
- Fear of prosecution or reprisal is not a valid basis for declining within guidelines
- If NSL issues are outside your competence, refer to a practitioner with relevant expertise
- Returning a brief: follow Law Society guidelines on proper withdrawal

### 3. HKBA Guidance (Key Principles)

The Hong Kong Bar Association has issued statements on professional conduct in NSL cases:

**Duty to the court**:
- The duty to the court is paramount and overrides the duty to the client
- Counsel must not knowingly mislead the court on NSL matters
- Counsel must not use legal proceedings to further conduct endangering national security

**Professional independence**:
- Counsel must maintain independence in NSL cases
- Do not allow fear of reprisal to compromise proper representation
- Counsel must not be intimidated from advancing legitimate arguments
- If security concerns arise, seek confidential guidance from the Bar Council

**Taking instructions**:
- NSL cases may involve special security measures in taking instructions (e.g., at NSD facilities)
- Counsel must observe all lawful directions from the court and authorities
- Document all instructions and communications
- Be alert to potential monitoring of privileged communications

**Conduct at trial**:
- Respect the designated judge panel and court procedures
- Advocate within the bounds of professional conduct
- Do not make submissions that could be construed as endangering national security
- Consider the Art 47 certificate carefully — it is binding on the court

### 4. Ethical Issues in NSL Cases

**Client with foreign connections**:
- Advise on the Art 47 certificate risk if foreign legal practitioners are involved
- The CFA has confirmed no blanket prohibition on overseas practitioners (Re: Overseas Legal Practitioners)
- However, the court retains discretion to consider national security implications

**Sensitive evidence**:
- PII applications and closed hearings under Art 48
- Counsel's role in closed sessions (may be restricted from full disclosure)
- Special advocates may be appointed for sensitive evidence
- Duty to ensure client understands limitations on disclosure

**Extraterritorial conduct (Art 65)**:
- NSL applies to acts committed outside HK by HK permanent residents or HK organisations
- Practitioners should advise clients travelling or conducting business abroad

**Cooperation with investigations**:
- Duty to cooperate with NSD (Art 63)
- Limit to lawful directions — do not voluntarily disclose privileged material
- Seek independent legal advice if NSD requests disclosure of privileged material

### 5. Cross-Border Practice Considerations

- Foreign law firms operating in HK must comply with NSL obligations
- Advice to clients on Mainland-related matters should consider NSL implications
- Cross-border data flows: Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap 486) and NSL obligations
- International sanctions and NSL compliance
- Reporting to overseas principals or clients may engage NSL considerations

### 6. Practical Safeguards

- Maintain comprehensive attendance notes and file records
- Use secure communication channels for NSL-related matters
- Consider the risk of monitoring of communications
- Regularly review Law Society and HKBA practice directions
- Attend CPD on NSL practice and professional obligations
- Consult senior counsel or Law Society practice advisory service in doubtful cases
- Maintain professional indemnity insurance with adequate NSL coverage
- Know the NSD contact procedures for lawful cooperation

## Key Precedents

- Re: Overseas Legal Practitioners (CFA 2023) — no blanket bar on foreign counsel
- HKSAR v Lai Chee Ying (2021) — constitutional framework for NSL procedure
- Tam Sze Leung v Secretary for Justice (2022) — procedural fairness, Art 47 certificate

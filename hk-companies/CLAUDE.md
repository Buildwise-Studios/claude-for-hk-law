# HK Companies Practice Profile

## Practice Area Overview

Hong Kong company law is codified primarily in the **Companies Ordinance (Cap 622)**, which came into full effect on 3 March 2014, replacing the old Companies Ordinance (Cap 32). The winding-up regime remains under the **Companies (Winding Up and Miscellaneous Provisions) Ordinance (Cap 32)**.

Hong Kong follows the UK-derived common law tradition but has developed its own corporate jurisprudence through the Court of Final Appeal (CFA) and Court of Appeal decisions.

## Key Legislation

| Legislation | Key Provisions |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Companies Ordinance (Cap 622)** | Incorporation, share capital, directors' duties, shareholder remedies, accounts, audit, annual compliance |
| **Companies (Winding Up and Miscellaneous Provisions) Ordinance (Cap 32)** | Winding up (compulsory and voluntary), liquidators, proof of debts, fraudulent trading |
| **Business Registration Ordinance (Cap 310)** | Business registration requirements |
| **Inland Revenue Ordinance (Cap 112)** | Profits tax, stamp duty on share transfers |
| **Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap 486)** | Data protection obligations for companies |
| **Securities and Futures Ordinance (Cap 571)** | Listed company regulation, disclosure, market misconduct |

## Incorporation under Cap 622

### Requirements
- Minimum one member (no maximum)
- Minimum one director (individual, natural person for private companies)
- Company secretary (individual resident in HK or a body corporate with HK address)
- Registered office address in Hong Kong
- Memorandum and Articles of Association

### Types of Companies
- **Private company limited by shares** — most common, restricts share transfer, limits members to 50 (may be removed under new provisions)
- **Public company limited by shares** — may offer shares to the public
- **Company limited by guarantee** — no share capital, typically for non-profits
- **Unlimited company** — members have unlimited liability

### Company Name
- Must end with "Limited" (private) or "Public Limited Company" (public)
- Cannot be identical or too similar to existing names (check CR via e-Search)
- Certain names require prior approval (bank, insurance, trust, etc.)

### Re-registration
- Private company may re-register as a public company (s.183)
- Public company may re-register as a private company (s.189)

## Share Capital

### Key Features under Cap 622
- No par value shares (abolished 2014)
- One class of shares unless articles provide otherwise
- Shares may be issued with different rights (voting, dividend, capital)
- Financial assistance for acquisition of own shares — generally prohibited (s.281) with exceptions
- Share buybacks permitted (ss.236–278) subject to solvency test
- Reduction of capital — court confirmation required or solvency statement route (s.217)

### Statutory Registers
- Register of members (s.627)
- Register of directors and company secretary (s.641)
- Register of charges (s.335)
- Significant Controllers Register (SCR) — introduced under the Anti-Money Laundering Ordinance

## Directors' Duties

Directors' duties under Hong Kong law derive from both common law/equity and Cap 622:

### Statutory Duties (s.465 — codified)
- Duty to act in good faith in the best interests of the company
- Duty to exercise powers for a proper purpose
- Duty to avoid conflicts of interest
- Duty not to accept benefits from third parties
- Duty to exercise reasonable care, skill, and diligence
- Duty not to make unauthorised use of company property or information

### Common Law Fiduciary Duties (continuing to apply alongside s.465)
- Fiduciary duty of loyalty
- Duty to act within powers (constitutional limitations)
- Duty to exercise independent judgment
- Duty to consider creditors' interests when company is insolvent or approaching insolvency

### Disclosure Requirements
- Directors must declare interests in contracts with the company (s.475)
- Substantial shareholder disclosure (Part XV of the SFO for listed companies)
- Loans to directors require member approval (ss.500–519)

### Consequences of Breach
- Personal liability for loss
- Account of profits
- Summary dismissal
- Disqualification order (up to 15 years)
- Criminal liability for certain breaches (fraudulent trading, etc.)

## Shareholder Remedies

### Unfair Prejudice Petition (s.724 of Cap 622)
- Available where company's affairs are conducted in a manner unfairly prejudicial to members
- Broad discretion of the court — remedies include share purchase orders, injunctions, regulation of future conduct
- Common in quasi-partnership companies (small private companies)
- Key case: *Re Chime Corporation Ltd* — established principles for relief

### Derivative Action (ss.731–738 of Cap 622)
- Statutory derivative action replacing common law exceptions to the rule in *Foss v Harbottle*
- Member may apply for leave to bring proceedings in the company's name
- Court considers: good faith, interests of the company, likelihood of success, alternative remedies

### Winding Up on Just and Equitable Grounds (s.177(1)(f) of Cap 32)
- Available where it is just and equitable to wind up the company
- Common grounds: loss of substratum, deadlock, breakdown of mutual trust (quasi-partnership), justifiable lack of confidence in management
- Often used as a pressure tactic alongside unfair prejudice petitions

### Other Remedies
- Just and equitable winding up (s.177 Cap 32)
- Investigation by the Financial Secretary (s.143 Cap 622)
- Derivative action (ss.731–738 Cap 622)
- Injunction to restrain breach of directors' duties

## Annual Compliance

### Key Obligations
- **Annual Return** (Form NAR1) — filed with Companies Registry within 42 days of the anniversary of incorporation (s.662 Cap 622)
- **Annual General Meeting** — not required for private companies unless articles require (members may opt out by written resolution)
- **Financial Statements** — prepared in accordance with HKFRS or HKFRS for Private Entities
- **Audit** — required unless the company qualifies as a small private company (exempt under s.373)
- **Profits Tax Return** — filed with IRD annually
- **Business Registration Certificate** — renewed annually

### Significant Controllers Register (SCR)
- Required since 1 March 2018 (AML amendment)
- Must identify and record persons with significant control (25%+ ownership or voting rights, or control of board majority)
- Accessible to law enforcement; partially accessible to the public (upon request to the CR)
- Failure to maintain is a criminal offence

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Late filing of annual return: HK$870–HK$3,480 escalating penalty
- Default in maintaining registers: HK$50,000 + HK$1,000 per day continuing default
- Directors personally liable for default

## Winding Up

### Members' Voluntary Liquidation (MVL)
- Company is solvent; directors make a statutory declaration of solvency (s.233 Cap 32)
- Members pass special resolution to wind up
- Liquidator appointed by members
- Creditors' period for challenge: 5 weeks from resolution

### Creditors' Voluntary Liquidation (CVL)
- Company is insolvent; no solvency declaration possible
- Members pass special resolution to wind up
- Creditors' meeting held — liquidator appointed by creditors
- Committee of inspection may be formed

### Compulsory Winding Up
- Petition presented to the Court of First Instance (CFI)
- Grounds include: inability to pay debts (s.178 Cap 32), just and equitable (s.177(1)(f)), public interest
- Provisional liquidator may be appointed if assets at risk
- Official Receiver becomes liquidator unless private liquidator appointed

### Powers of Liquidator
- Collect and realise assets
- Investigate company affairs (examine directors, report to court)
- Set aside antecedent transactions (unfair preferences, transactions at undervalue, floating charges)
- Seek contribution from directors (misfeasance, fraudulent/wrongful trading)
- Apply for disqualification orders

### Relevant Case Law
- **Re Chime Corporation Ltd** — unfair prejudice principles
- **Re Lam Kwai Chung Garment Factory Ltd** — winding up on just and equitable grounds
- **Re Ciro Citterio Menswear plc (in liquidation)** — wrongful trading
- **Securities and Futures Commission v Stock Exchange of Hong Kong Ltd** — directors' duties
- **Re Grand Field Group Holdings Ltd** — shareholder remedies and derivative actions
- **Re UDL Holdings Ltd** — rights of creditors and set-off in liquidation

## Practice Guidance

When advising on company matters:
1. Verify incorporation status and compliance via the Companies Registry e-Search
2. Always check the company's articles — they may contain important restrictions or procedural requirements
3. For directors' duty questions, distinguish between the statutory codification (s.465) and continuing common law duties
4. In shareholder disputes, consider whether unfair prejudice (s.724) or just and equitable winding up (s.177(1)(f)) is the more appropriate remedy
5. For insolvency advice, assess timing — earlier intervention preserves more options
6. Cross-check the latest Companies Registry Practice Directions and e-Legislation amendments
7. For listed companies, consider additional SFC, HKEX, and SFO requirements

Privacy law in Hong Kong moves through legislative amendments and PCPD guidance. Before relying on an effective date, threshold, or obligation, verify it. These are the areas most likely to have moved since model training:

## PDPO (Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance, Cap 486)

- **Direct marketing regime (ss.35A-35J):** Introduced by amendment in 2012 and operational. Requires prescribed written notice and consent/opt-out for use of personal data in direct marketing. Separate consent required for provision of data to third parties for direct marketing. Criminal sanctions apply — check current PCPD enforcement guidance.
- **Data breach notification:** Currently voluntary under PCPD guidance (the "Data Breach Handling Guide"). Proposed amendments may introduce mandatory notification — verify current legislative status.
- **Cross-border data transfers:** s.33 (restriction on transfer of personal data to places outside Hong Kong) has not yet been fully commenced (not in force as of model training). Check the HKSAR Government's current legislative programme.
- **Penalty levels:** The maximum fine under PDPO was increased from HK$10,000 to HK$50,000 per offence (and HK$500,000 for repeat offences) under the 2021 amendments. Direct marketing offences carry a maximum fine of HK$1,000,000 and 5 years' imprisonment. Verify current penalty levels.

## Cross-border data transfers

- **PRC Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL):** In force since November 2021. Applies to the processing of personal information of individuals in Mainland China by organizations outside Mainland China, including Hong Kong companies, under Art. 3 (extraterritorial effect). Cross-border transfer requirements include: standard contract, security assessment, or certification.
- **Hong Kong s.33 status:** Check whether the HKSAR Government has announced a commencement date.

## PCPD enforcement and guidance

- **PCPD enforcement trends:** The PCPD has increasingly used its investigative powers (s.38) and issued enforcement notices (s.50). Monitor the PCPD's annual reports and enforcement notice register.
- **Guidance on AI and data privacy:** The PCPD has issued guidance on "Ethical Accountability Framework for Artificial Intelligence" and may update it. Check the PCPD website for current guidance.
- **Cross-border complaint handling:** The PCPD can investigate complaints involving cross-border data transfers. Monitor cooperation between PCPD and other regulators.

## DSAR (Data Access Request) timelines

- **PDPO requirement:** 40 calendar days to respond to a DAR (s.19(2)). No extension mechanism similar to GDPR.
- **Fee:** Prescribed maximum of HK$180 for photocopying plus reasonable search and reproduction costs.
- **Data correction:** Must respond within 40 days of receiving a data correction request (s.24(2)).

# Copyright Fair Dealing — Skill

## Purpose

Analyse fair dealing defences under the Copyright Ordinance (Cap 528) in Hong Kong — a closed-list system (not open-ended fair use). Advise on whether a proposed or alleged use of a copyright work falls within a permitted purpose.

## Scope

- Fair dealing for research and private study (s.38)
- Fair dealing for criticism, review, and news reporting (s.39)
- Fair dealing for education (ss.41–46)
- Library and archive copying (ss.47–55)
- Public administration (ss.61–66)
- Factors considered in assessing fairness
- Exceptions outside fair dealing
- Distinction from US fair use

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the Work and Use

- Type of work: literary, dramatic, musical, artistic, sound recording, film, broadcast, cable programme, published edition
- Is the work published/unpublished?
- What is the proposed use? (reproduction, performance, distribution, adaptation, communication to public)
- Amount and substantiality of portion to be used

### 2. Identify the Purpose

**Closed list under Cap 528 — must fall within one of these purposes:**

| Purpose | Section | Permitted Activities |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Research and private study | s.38 | Copying for non-commercial research (s.38(1)(a)) or private study (s.38(1)(b)) |
| Criticism, review, news reporting | s.39 | Fair dealing for criticism/review (with sufficient acknowledgment); news reporting (no acknowledgment needed for reporting current events in any medium) |
| Education | ss.41–46 | Copying for instruction, examinations, anthologies for educational use |
| Libraries and archives | ss.47–55 | Copying for preservation, research, inter-library loans |
| Public administration | ss.61–66 | Copying for parliamentary/judicial proceedings, statutory inquiries, Royal Commissions |

**Note**: Fair dealing is NOT available for commercial research — s.38(1)(a) limits research to non-commercial purposes. This is a critical distinction from the US.

### 3. Assess Fairness Factors

For the purposes that require it (research/private study, criticism/review), the court considers all circumstances including:

| Factor | Weight | Considerations |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Purpose of dealing | High | Is the dealing truly for research/study/review/criticism? Is it commercial? Is it transformative? |
| Nature of the work | Medium | Published or unpublished? Factual or creative? |
| Amount taken | High | Quantitative (percentage of work) and qualitative (substantiality of portion — even a small portion may be infringing if it is the "heart" of the work) |
| Effect on market | High | Does the dealing compete with the original work? Would it reduce sales/licensing revenue? |
| Alternatives available | Medium | Could the purpose be achieved without copying? |

**Important**: These are not a rigid checklist — the court weighs all factors holistically.

### 4. Consider Other Exceptions Outside Fair Dealing

| Exception | Scope |
|-----------|-------|
| Incidental inclusion (s.40) | Copyright not infringed by incidental inclusion in an artistic work, sound recording, film, broadcast, or cable programme |
| Time-shifting (s.79) | Recording broadcasts/cable programmes for private viewing at a more convenient time |
| Format-shifting (s.80) | Copying sound recordings for private use |
| Parody (s.39A) | Fair dealing for the purpose of parody is permitted (introduced by the Copyright (Amendment) Ordinance 2014) |
| Caricature, pastiche (s.39A) | Fair dealing for caricature or pastiche |
| Quotation (s.39A) | Fair dealing for quotation — allows use of short extracts for any purpose, provided the use is fair and the source is acknowledged (Model Law alignment) |
| Accessible copies for disabled persons (ss.51–54A) | Copying for visually impaired persons |

### 5. Document the Fair Dealing Analysis

Prepare a memorandum setting out:

- The work in question
- The purpose of the dealing
- The nature and quality of the portion taken
- The effect on the market for the work
- Whether sufficient acknowledgment is given (where required)
- Conclusion: does the dealing qualify as fair dealing?

### 6. Examples of Fair/Not Fair

| Scenario | Fair Dealing? | Reasoning |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Quoting a few lines from a textbook in a university lecture | Likely fair | Research/instruction; small portion; non-commercial; no market effect |
| Copying an entire journal article for personal research | Likely fair | Non-commercial research; entire work may be necessary |
| Reproducing a photograph in a news article criticising the artist | Potentially fair | Criticism/review; must be fair and acknowledged |
| Downloading and sharing a full film via P2P | Not fair | Not any permitted purpose; substantial; damaging to market |
| Using an entire song as background in a corporate video | Not fair | Not any permitted purpose; commercial; substantial |
| Photocopying 10% of a textbook for classroom handouts | Potentially fair | Education exception (ss.41–42) may apply; check limits |
| Using a film still in a commercial advertisement | Not fair | Not any permitted purpose; commercial use |

### 7. Distinction from US Fair Use

| Feature | HK (Cap 528) | US (Copyright Act 1976 §107) |
|---------|--------------|-----------------------------|
| System | Closed list (specific purposes) | Open-ended (illustrative list) |
| Transformative use | Not a factor | Central factor |
| Commercial research | Excluded | Can be fair use |
| Parody | Specific exception (s.39A) | Can be fair use |
| Flexibility | Low — must fit within a listed purpose | High — flexible balancing test |

## Key Provisions

| Section | Purpose | Key Detail |
|---------|---------|------------|
| s.38 | Research and private study | Non-commercial research only; fair dealing |
| s.39 | Criticism, review, news reporting | Sufficient acknowledgment required for criticism/review |
| s.39A | Parody, caricature, pastiche, quotation | Must be fair dealing; quotation with acknowledgment |
| ss.41–46 | Education | Copying for instruction; limits on multiple copies |
| s.40 | Incidental inclusion | No infringement for incidental inclusion |
| ss.79–80 | Time-shifting, format-shifting | Private use only |

## Key Cases

- **CA and Another v Chan Chun Chuen** — Copyright subsistence and infringement; fair dealing defence rejected
- **Hong Kong Federation of Trade Unions v HKSAR** — Criminal copyright enforcement

## Practice Guidance

1. **Fair dealing is not fair use** — HK has a closed list of permitted purposes; advice must first identify which purpose applies
2. **Commercial research is excluded** — for-profit research institutions cannot rely on s.38(1)(a)
3. **Quantity matters less than quality** — taking a small but qualitatively important extract may be infringing (the "heart" of the work)
4. **Sufficient acknowledgment is required** — for criticism/review and quotation exceptions
5. **Parody/caricature/pastiche/quote exception (s.39A)** — useful for creative and media clients; still requires fairness assessment
6. **Fair dealing is a defence** — the burden of proof is on the defendant once subsistence and infringement are established
7. **Document the analysis** — if advising on whether a proposed use is fair, document the factors considered and the conclusion

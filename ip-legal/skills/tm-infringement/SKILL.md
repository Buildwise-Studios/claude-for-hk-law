# Trade Mark Infringement — Skill

## Purpose

Analyse trade mark infringement and passing off claims under the Trade Marks Ordinance (Cap 559) and common law in Hong Kong, advise on remedies, and develop enforcement or defence strategies.

## Scope

- Trade mark infringement under ss.17–21 Cap 559
- Passing off (common law)
- Comparative advertising
- Defences and exceptions
- Remedies (injunctions, damages, account of profits, delivery up)
- Enforcement strategy (civil v criminal)
- Counterfeiting and Customs & Excise enforcement

## Workflow

### 1. Establish the Registered Rights

- Confirm registration details (mark, class, goods/services, registration date, renewal status)
- Check if the registration is valid (no pending non-use revocation, invalidity proceedings)
- If unregistered, assess passing off claim instead
- Consider well-known mark protection under s.7

### 2. Identify the Alleged Infringing Use

- What sign is being used? (identical/similar to registered mark)
- For what goods/services? (identical/similar to registered specification)
- In what context? (trade mark use, descriptive use, comparative advertising, domain name, company name, metatags)
- Where in HK is the use occurring? (territoriality — HK courts have jurisdiction over use in HK)

### 3. Assess Infringement Under s.18

| Scenario | Test | Likely Outcome |
|----------|------|----------------|
| Identical sign + identical goods/services | Strict liability (s.18(1)) | Infringement |
| Identical/similar sign + identical/similar goods | Likelihood of confusion (s.18(2)) | Infringement if confusion likely |
| Identical/similar sign + dissimilar goods | Well-known mark + unfair advantage/detriment (s.18(3)) | Infringement if mark well-known and use takes unfair advantage or is detrimental |

### 4. Consider Defences and Exceptions

| Defence | Section | Scope |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Use of own name/address | s.20(1)(a) | Individual's own name or address |
| Descriptive use | s.20(1)(b) | Indication of kind, quality, quantity, intended purpose, value, geographical origin, time of production, other characteristics |
| Intended purpose | s.20(1)(c) | Where necessary to indicate intended purpose (e.g., spare parts, accessories) |
| Honest practices | s.20(2) | Use must be in accordance with honest commercial practices |
| Comparative advertising | s.20(3) | Honest comparative advertising; must not give unfair advantage or be misleading |
| Exhaustion of rights | s.21 | Goods placed on market in HK by proprietor or with consent |

### 5. Assess Passing Off Claim (if applicable)

Establish the classic trinity:
- **Goodwill**: Does the claimant have business reputation in HK?
- **Misrepresentation**: Is the defendant's conduct likely to deceive the public?
- **Damage**: Is there actual or likely damage to the claimant's goodwill?

### 6. Evidence Gathering

- Sealed samples of infringing goods (purchase, investigator)
- Photographs, screenshots, website captures
- Investigator reports (private investigators)
- Customs seizure records
- Survey evidence (for passing off / confusion)
- Customer confusion evidence
- Witness statements

### 7. Choose Enforcement Route

| Factor | Civil (CFI) | Criminal (Customs) |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Burden of proof | Balance of probabilities | Beyond reasonable doubt |
| Remedies | Injunction, damages, account, delivery up | Fines, imprisonment, forfeiture |
| Speed | Interim injunction (ex parte possible) | Raids, arrests |
| Control | Claimant controls proceedings | DOJ/Customs control |
| Costs | Claimant bears costs risk | No costs to IP owner |
| Best for | Unclear infringement, passing off, patent/design | Clear counterfeiting, trade mark forgery |

### 8. Draft Letter of Demand

- Identify the rights
- Detail the infringing acts
- Demand cessation, delivery up, undertakings
- Set deadline for response (typically 7–14 days)
- Propose settlement (injunction + costs + damages)
- Consider without prejudice negotiations

### 9. Urgent Court Applications

| Order | Purpose | Test |
|-------|---------|------|
| Ex parte interim injunction | Immediate stop to infringing activity | Serious question to be tried + balance of convenience |
| Anton Piller order | Search and seizure of infringing goods/evidence | Strong prima facie case + real risk of destruction |
| Mareva injunction | Freeze defendant's assets | Good arguable case + risk of dissipation |
| Norwich Pharmacal order | Disclosure of infringer's identity | Innocent party mixed up in wrongdoing + requirement for information |

### 10. Trial and Final Relief

| Remedy | Purpose | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Permanent injunction | Prohibit future infringement | Standard if infringement established |
| Damages | Compensation for loss | Measure: lost profits or reasonable royalty; additional damages for flagrant infringement (s.18(5)) |
| Account of profits | Defendant's profits | Alternative to damages (cannot elect both) |
| Delivery up / destruction | Remove infringing goods from market | With injunction; costs of compliance |
| Costs | Litigation costs | Standard order if successful |

## Key Provisions

| Section | Subject | Key Detail |
|---------|---------|------------|
| s.18 | Infringement | Acts constituting infringement |
| s.20 | Defences | Own name, descriptive use, comparative advertising |
| s.21 | Exhaustion | Exhaustion of rights within HK |
| s.18(5) | Additional damages | Flagrant infringement — punitive element |
| s.7 | Well-known marks | Protection without registration |

## Key Cases

- **Société des Produits Nestlé SA v Unicafe Inc** — Likelihood of confusion
- **Levi Strauss & Co v Kimbyr Investments Ltd** — Well-known marks and passing off
- **Reckitt & Colman Products Ltd v Borden Inc** — Jif Lemon trinity (passing off)
- **Whiteaway Laidlaw & Co Ltd v Hong Kong Land Co Ltd** — Goodwill requirement

## Practice Guidance

1. **Register verification is critical** — check registration is subsisting, specification covers the goods/services, and no pending revocation proceedings
2. **Ex parte orders are exceptional** — full and frank disclosure required; duty to disclose all material facts
3. **Damages or account** — choose before judgment; damages compensate loss, account disgorges profits
4. **Additional damages** — only for flagrant infringement (s.18(5)); consider factors: deterrence, defendant's conduct, duration
5. **Passing off is harder to prove** — goodwill requirement can be challenging for international brands with limited HK presence
6. **Criminal enforcement is faster** — for clear counterfeiting, Customs & Excise raids are more cost-effective than civil litigation
7. **Settlement always an option** — costs of CFI litigation are high; consider mediation (IPD mediation service available)

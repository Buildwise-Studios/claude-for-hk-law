# HK Intellectual Property Practice Profile

## Practice Area Overview

Hong Kong's intellectual property (IP) regime operates as a separate system under the "one country, two systems" framework, distinct from Mainland China's IP system. HK maintains its own IP registration offices, enforcement mechanisms, and common law jurisprudence while aligning with international treaties through PRC membership extended to HK.

## Key Legislation

| Legislation | Key Provisions |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Trade Marks Ordinance (Cap 559)** | Registration, infringement, well-known marks, licensing, assignment |
| **Patents Ordinance (Cap 514)** | Standard patents (re-registration of designated patents), short-term patents |
| **Copyright Ordinance (Cap 528)** | Original works, fair dealing, OSP liability (ss.88–91), criminal sanctions |
| **Registered Designs Ordinance (Cap 522)** | Design registration, infringement |
| **Trade Descriptions Ordinance (Cap 362)** | Criminal enforcement against false trade descriptions, forged trademarks |
| **Prevention of Copyright Piracy Ordinance (Cap 544)** | Border measures, anti-circumvention provisions |

### International Treaties Applicable to HK

- **Paris Convention** — priority rights for patents, trade marks, designs
- **Berne Convention** — copyright protection (via PRC membership)
- **TRIPS Agreement** — WTO minimum standards (via PRC membership)
- **Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT)** — extended to HK for standard patent re-registration
- **Madrid Protocol** — extended to HK for trade mark registration (from 29 July 2024)
- **Hague Agreement** — extended to HK for industrial design registration (from 5 August 2024)

**Key Distinction**: Unlike PRC, HK does not have a separate "well-known mark" registration system; well-known marks are protected under Cap 559 s.7 — protection against registration and use of identical/similar marks.

## Trade Marks (Cap 559)

### Registration Process

**Filing Requirements:**
- Hong Kong operates a multi-class filing system (Nice Classification)
- Filing date: date of receipt of application at IPD
- Priority may be claimed from Paris Convention application (within 6 months)

**Examination:**
- Formal examination (filing requirements)
- Substantive examination (absolute grounds s.11, relative grounds s.12)
- **Absolute Grounds** (s.11): Devoid of distinctive character, descriptive, customary, shapes resulting from nature/necessary for function, contrary to public policy/morality, deceptive
- **Relative Grounds** (s.12): Conflict with earlier marks (same/similar marks for same/similar goods/services); earlier well-known marks (s.12(3))

**Registration:**
- Publication in HK Intellectual Property Journal
- Opposition period: 3 months from publication
- Registration valid for 10 years from filing date
- Renewal: every 10 years (no limitation on renewals)

**Non-Use Cancellation:**
- Any person may apply to revoke registration if mark not used within 3 years from registration, with no genuine use in the succeeding 3 years (s.52)
- If registered for 5+ years with proven prior use and awareness by the applicant, a trade mark may be declared invalid on relative grounds — but subject to the **5-year limitation period for invalidation** on relative grounds (s.56)

### Infringement (ss.17–21)

| Section | Activity | Requirement |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| s.18 | Use of identical sign for identical goods/services | Strict liability |
| s.18 | Use of identical/similar sign for identical/similar goods | Likelihood of confusion |
| s.18 | Use of identical/similar sign for dissimilar goods | Taking unfair advantage/detriment (well-known marks) |
| s.20 | Comparative advertising | Exception; must be honest |
| s.21 | Use of company name, domain name, descriptive use | Exceptions where use is in accordance with honest practices |

**Remedies**: Injunction, damages (or account of profits), delivery up/destruction, costs. Additional damages for flagrant infringement (s.18(5)).

### Well-Known Marks

Protected under s.7 of Cap 559 against:
- Registration of identical/similar marks for identical/similar or dissimilar goods/services
- Assessment: knowledge/recognition in the relevant sector of the public in HK; no requirement of registration in HK

### Passing Off (Common Law)

Protects unregistered marks under common law. The classic trinity must be established:
1. **Goodwill** — established business reputation in HK (not necessarily physical presence)
2. **Misrepresentation** — defendant's conduct likely to deceive
3. **Damage** — actual or likely damage to goodwill

**Key Cases**: *Whiteaway Laidlaw & Co Ltd v Hong Kong Land Co Ltd* — goodwill requirement; *Reckitt & Colman Products Ltd v Borden Inc* — Jif Lemon trinity; *Levi Strauss & Co v Kimbyr Investments Ltd* — well-known brand passing off.

## Patents (Cap 514)

### Standard Patent (s.7–21)

HK standard patents are **re-registration patents**, not examination-based. The process:
1. **Designate HK** in a European Patent Office (EPO), UK (GB), or PRC patent application (filing of "designated patent application")
2. File request to record in HK within 6 months of grant of designated patent
3. File request for grant and publish registration within 6 months of recordal

**Term**: 20 years from filing date of designated patent application.
**Renewal**: Annually from the end of the year following grant.

### Short-Term Patent (s.22–32)

A standalone HK innovation patent, examined only for formality requirements (not substantive):
- Maximum 8 claims (1 independent, 7 dependent)
- Subject matter: any invention (same as standard patent)
- **Unitary term reversal**: As of the 2024 amendments, the term is **8 years** from filing date:
  - Initial term: 4 years
  - Renewal: 4 years (maximum)
- **No opposition system** — can only be challenged by revocation petition to the CFI or as a counterclaim in infringement proceedings

### Patent Infringement

Patent infringement is **not a criminal offence** in HK — civil remedies only (injunction, damages or account of profits). Key features:
- Acts of making, disposing of, offering to dispose of, using, importing, keeping product
- Direct and joint tortfeasor liability
- No criminal liability for patent infringement (unlike trade marks/copyright)
- **Defences**: Experimental use (s.78B), compulsory licence (ss.83–86), prior use rights (s.79), exhaustion (s.80 for products put on market in HK by patentee/with consent)

### Patentability Requirements (s.8)
1. Novelty — absolute novelty (worldwide prior art)
2. Inventive step — not obvious to person skilled in the art
3. Industrial applicability
4. Not excluded subject matter (discoveries, scientific theories, mathematical methods, aesthetic creations, schemes/rules/methods for mental acts, games, business methods, computer programs "as such", presentations of information; diagnostic, therapeutic and surgical methods — but not the products used in such methods)

## Copyright (Cap 528)

### Key Features

Copyright in HK arises **automatically** upon creation — no registration system exists:
- **Original works**: literary, dramatic, musical, artistic works (including computer programs)
- **Entrepreneurial works**: sound recordings, films, broadcasts, cable programmes, published editions
- **Term**: Life of author + 50 years (literary, dramatic, musical, artistic); 50 years from making (sound recordings, films, broadcasts); 25 years (published editions)
- No requirement of registration — copyright subsists from the moment the work is recorded

### Fair Dealing (NOT Fair Use)

HK follows the UK-style **fair dealing** system (not the US fair use doctrine). Permitted purposes:
1. **Research and private study** (s.38)
2. **Criticism, review, and news reporting** (s.39)
3. **Education** (ss.41–46)
4. **Libraries and archives** (ss.47–55)
5. **Public administration** (ss.61–66)

**Assessment Factors** (where applicable):
- Purpose and nature of dealing
- Nature of the work
- Amount and substantiality of portion taken
- Effect on potential market for work

### Criminal Sanctions

HK has strong criminal copyright provisions:
- Making or distributing infringing copies for trade/business — criminal offence
- Possession of infringing copies for trade/business — criminal offence
- Manufacturing/possession of circumvention devices — criminal offence
- Penalties: up to HK$500,000 fine per copy and 4 years imprisonment

### OSP Liability (Sections 88–91)

Online Service Provider (OSP) safe harbour provisions:

- **s.88**: Conditions for limitation of liability — OSPs must:
  - Adopt and implement a repeat infringer termination policy
  - Accommodate standard technical measures
  - Designate a copyright infringement complaint agent/contact
- **s.89**: Limitations on remedies in relation to caching
- **s.90**: Limitations on remedies in relation to storage at direction of user
- **s.91**: Limitations on remedies in relation to information location tools

**Compliance Requirements**:
- Publish contact details of designated complaint agent
- Implement notice-and-takedown procedure
- Neither party can interfere with evidence
- OSP must act expeditiously upon receiving notice

## Registered Designs (Cap 522)

### Registration
- HK-specific design registration (separate from PRC design patent system)
- Locarno Classification
- Term: 25 years (initial 5 years, renewable every 5 years)
- **Substantive examination**: IPD examines for novelty and individual character
- **Novelty**: Design must not be identical to any design made available to the public before the filing/priority date
- **Individual character**: Overall impression on informed user must differ from prior designs

### Infringement
- Making, importing, selling, or hiring articles incorporating the registered design
- Defence: private non-commercial use, experimental use
- Remedies: injunction, damages, delivery up

## Enforcement

| Mechanism | IP Type | Forum |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Civil action | Trade marks, patents, copyright, designs | Court of First Instance |
| Urgent relief | All IP (ex parte) | CFI — Mareva, Anton Piller, Norwich Pharmacal |
| Interim injunctions | All IP | CFI — balance of convenience |
| Customs & Excise | Trade marks, copyright | Criminal — border measures, raids |
| Mediation | All IP | HKMC, IPD mediation services |
| Border measures | Trade marks, copyright | Customs controls at ports |

### Customs & Excise Enforcement
- CDI (Customs Drug Investigation) Bureau handles IP criminal enforcement
- Powers: search, seizure, arrest without warrant for IP offences
- Focus: counterfeit luxury goods, pirated software, films and music
- Offences under Cap 362 (Trade Descriptions) and Cap 528 (Copyright)
- Customs may initiate prosecution or assist IPD/rights holders

### IPD (Intellectual Property Department) Role
- Trade mark, patent, design registration administration
- Promotion of IP awareness and policy advice
- No IPD enforcement role (enforcement by Customs & Excise and CFI)
- Provides mediation services for IP disputes

## PRC-HK IP Cross-Border Issues

| Issue | Summary |
|-------|---------|
| **Exhaustion of rights** | HK adopts international exhaustion (once product marketed anywhere by patentee/licensee, no HK patent rights) |
| **Parallel imports** | Copyright — permitted for certain works; trade marks — complex; no blanket prohibition |
| **Co-existence of HK and PRC rights** | Separate registrations needed in HK and PRC; no automatic cross-recognition |
| **Well-known marks** | PRC has "well-known mark" administrative recognition; HK protects under s.7 Cap 559 without separate register |
| **Enforcement across boundary** | IP rights are territorial; separate proceedings in PRC and HK; no cross-border enforcement of IP judgments without the Arrangement |
| **Madrid Protocol (from 2024)** | HK extension allows international registration designating HK for trade marks |
| **Hague Agreement (from 2024)** | HK extension allows international design registration designating HK |

## Practice Guidance

1. **IP rights are territorial** — HK registration is independent of PRC; separate filings required
2. **Trade mark clearance** — search IPD Registry, CR company names, domain names, marketplace, common law use
3. **Patent strategy** — for HK, standard patents derive from EPO/UK/PRC patents; short-term patents for quick protection (8-year term)
4. **Copyright no registration** — but document creation evidence (firm records, timestamped copies, SWIFT evidence deposit)
5. **OSP compliance** — ensure notice-and-takedown procedures, complaint agent contact published, repeat infringer policy
6. **Fair dealing not fair use** — closed list of purposes; no open-ended "fair use" defence
7. **Enforcement choice** — consider civil (CFI injunction + damages) vs criminal (Customs & Excise raids) based on evidence strength and commercial objectives
8. **Cross-border strategy** — often need coordinated HK-PRC advice; consider jurisdiction for litigation
9. **Check IPD Practice Directions** and latest IPD journal for procedural updates
10. **Consider ADR** — HKIAC may arbitrate IP disputes; IPD offers mediation services

# HK Litigation Procedure — Hong Kong Civil Litigation Knowledge Base

*This file ships with the plugin and is the canonical reference for Hong Kong civil procedure. Skills in this plugin read this file as the primary knowledge source.*

---

## Court Hierarchy

```
Court of Final Appeal (CFA) — final appellate court
    └── Court of Appeal (CA) — appeals from CFI and DC
          ├── Court of First Instance (CFI) — unlimited civil jurisdiction
          │     ├── High Court actions (writ, originating summons)
          │     └── Appeal from Labour Tribunal, Lands Tribunal, Small Claims
          ├── District Court (DC) — civil jurisdiction up to HK$3M
          │     └── Employment claims, discrimination claims, personal injury
          └── Tribunals
                ├── Labour Tribunal — employment monetary claims
                ├── Lands Tribunal — landlord/tenant, rating
                └── Small Claims Tribunal — claims up to HK$75,000
```

### Civil Jurisdiction Limits

| Court | Monetary limit |
|---|---|
| Court of Final Appeal | Appeals only (leave required) |
| Court of Appeal | Appeals only (leave for most) |
| Court of First Instance (CFI) | Unlimited |
| District Court (DC) | Up to HK$3,000,000 (equitable: HK$3,000,000) |
| Labour Tribunal | Monetary claims from employment (generally no upper limit for Cap 57) |
| Small Claims Tribunal | Up to HK$75,000 |
| Lands Tribunal | Rent and rates (no monetary cap within jurisdiction) |

## Rules of Court

| Court | Governing rules |
|---|---|
| CFI & CA | Rules of the High Court (Cap 4A, subsidiary legislation) — "RHC" |
| District Court | Rules of the District Court (Cap 336H) — "RDC" |
| CFA | Hong Kong Court of Final Appeal Rules (Cap 484A) |

## Commencing Proceedings

### Writ of Summons (RHC O.6 / RDC O.6)

- **Standard procedure** for cases involving substantial dispute of fact.
- **Contents:** Indorsement of claim (general or special), statement of claim (either indorsed or served subsequently).
- **Validity:** 12 months (renewable on application).
- **Service:** Personal, postal, or substituted service (O.65).

### Originating Summons (RHC O.7 / RDC O.7)

- **Use:** Where the main issue is a question of law or construction of a document (no substantial factual dispute).
- **Procedure:** Summons supported by affidavit; trial of an issue ordered if factual dispute emerges.

### Originating Motion (RHC O.8)

- **Use:** Appeals, judicial review, applications under specific statutes.

## Pleadings (RHC O.18 / RDC O.18)

### Purpose

Pleadings define the issues to be tried. Each party must plead every material fact, not evidence.

### Key Rules

| Pleading | Purpose | Time limit |
|---|---|---|
| Statement of Claim (SoC) | Sets out plaintiff's case: facts relied on, cause(s) of action, relief claimed | Usually indorsed on writ or served within 14 days of defence being ordered |
| Defence | Responds to each allegation — admits, denies (must specify reasons), or requires proof | 28 days from service of SoC (or 14 days from time for appearance) |
| Reply | Responds to new matters raised in Defence | 14 days from service of Defence |
| Counterclaim | Defendant's claim against plaintiff | Within the Defence |
| Defence to Counterclaim | Plaintiff's response to counterclaim | 14 days from counterclaim |

**Scandalous or unnecessary matter:** May be struck out (O.18, r.19).

**Striking out (O.18, r.19):** Court may strike out a pleading that:
- Discloses no reasonable cause of action or defence
- Is scandalous, frivolous, or vexatious
- May prejudice, embarrass, or delay the fair trial
- Is otherwise an abuse of process

### Amendment (RHC O.20)

- Without leave: Once before close of pleadings.
- With leave: Subsequent to close of pleadings (court has broad discretion).
- After limitation period expired: Only if the new claim arises out of substantially the same facts.

## Discovery / Disclosure (RHC O.24 / RDC O.24)

### Scope

**Discovery in Hong Kong is narrower than US discovery.** It follows the English approach:

- **Standard discovery (O.24, r.1):** Each party must disclose documents (or classes of documents) on which they rely, which may adversely affect their own or another party's case, or which may support another party's case — the **Peruvian Guano** test.
- **Specific discovery (O.24, r.7):** If standard discovery is inadequate, a party may apply by summons for specific discovery of particular documents or classes.

### Procedure

| Step | Description |
|---|---|
| Discovery list (O.24, r.5) | List of documents in a specified form (Schedule 1, Part I: documents in party's control) |
| Affidavit of documents (O.24, r.6) | Sworn statement verifying the list, specifying documents no longer in control |
| Inspection (O.24, r.9) | Right to inspect documents in the list (unless privilege claimed) |
| Pre-action discovery (O.24, r.7A) | Discovery before proceedings commence — requires showing potential claim, limited to specific documents |
| Discovery against non-parties (O.24, r.7A) | Available in personal injury and fatal accident claims |

### Privilege

**Legal Professional Privilege (LPP):**

| Type | Scope |
|---|---|
| Legal advice privilege | Communications between solicitor and client for the dominant purpose of giving/receiving legal advice |
| Litigation privilege | Communications (including with third parties) for the dominant purpose of reasonably contemplated or pending litigation |

**Common interest privilege:** May extend to communications with related parties sharing a common interest.

**Without prejudice privilege:** Communications made in a genuine attempt to settle are inadmissible (subject to exceptions).

## Summons for Directions (RHC O.25 / RDC O.25)

After close of pleadings, the plaintiff must take out a summons for directions within 1 month (O.25, r.1). The court gives directions for:

- Discovery (if not yet done)
- Exchange of witness statements and expert reports
- Inspection of documents and property
- Place and mode of trial
- Adjournment to ADR/mediation

**Sanctions for non-compliance:** Dismissal of action or striking out of defence (O.25, r.1(8)).

## Case Management (RHC O.1A, O.25, O.34)

Hong Kong courts exercise active case management. Key features:

- **Case management track:** Post-discovery case management conferences to set trial dates, fix time estimates, and narrow issues.
- **Sanctions:** Costs orders, wasted costs orders against parties or solicitors for non-compliance.
- **ADR:** Court may stay proceedings for ADR; will consider failure to mediate in costs decisions.
- **Kwan v Tritek:** Encapsulates the court's approach — active case management, proportionality.

## Trial (RHC O.35 / RDC O.35)

### Procedure

- **No jury in civil cases (except defamation, false imprisonment, malicious prosecution — rare).**
- **Order of trial:** Plaintiff opens → plaintiff's evidence → defendant's evidence → closing submissions.
- **Witness statements:** Stand as evidence-in-chief (unless the court orders otherwise). Cross-examination on statement.
- **Expert evidence:** Limited to one expert per party per discipline (unless court orders more); experts' concurrent evidence (hot-tubbing) increasingly used.
- **Bundle preparation:** Agreed bundle of documents for the trial judge.
- **Skeleton arguments:** Usually required for trials and appeals.

## Costs (RHC O.62 / RDC O.62)

### Costs Shifting

Hong Kong follows the **English costs-shifting rule**: the losing party generally pays the winning party's costs.

### Basis of Assessment

| Basis | Description | Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| **Party and party** | Standard basis — costs reasonably and necessarily incurred | ~60–70% of actual costs typically recovered |
| **Indemnity basis** | All costs except those unreasonably incurred or of an unreasonable amount | ~80–90% of actual costs |
| **Common fund basis** | Applies to legal aid cases; costs assessed on a more generous basis | |

### Costs Orders

| Order | Effect |
|---|---|
| Costs in the cause | Costs follow the eventual result |
| Costs in any event | This party gets costs regardless |
| Plaintiff's/Defendant's costs in the cause | Only this party gets costs if they win overall |
| No order as to costs | Each party bears own costs |
| Costs reserved | The trial judge decides later |
| Wasted costs order | Against the solicitor personally for improper/unreasonable conduct |

**Security for costs (O.23):** Where the plaintiff is resident outside HK or is a shell company.

### Summary Assessment

- **Grounded bill:** Itemised bill of costs.
- **Form L (High Court) / Form 21 (District Court):** Formal bill of costs for taxation.
- **Taxation:** Detailed assessment by the Registrar.

## Offers to Settle (RHC O.22 / RDC O.22)

- **Part 22 offer:** Formal offer to settle (similar to English Part 36 offers).
- **Consequences:** If a plaintiff fails to beat a defendant's Part 22 offer, the plaintiff pays the defendant's costs on the indemnity basis from the date the offer expired.
- **Without prejudice save as to costs:** The offer letter must expressly state this.

## Appeals

| From | To | Leave required? | Time limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Master's decision (CFI) | Judge in Chambers | No (unless interlocutory) | 14 days (unless otherwise ordered) |
| Judge in Chambers | Court of Appeal | Usually no for final orders; yes for interlocutory | 14 days |
| District Court | Court of Appeal | Yes (for amount < HK$1M) | 14-28 days |
| CFI (final) | Court of Appeal | Yes (for certain interlocutory orders) | 14-28 days |
| Court of Appeal (final) | Court of Final Appeal | Yes (strict — CFA Rules) | 28 days |
| Labour Tribunal | CFI | Yes (on point of law) | 7 days |
| Small Claims Tribunal | CFI | Yes (on point of law or jurisdiction) | 7 days |

### Appeal Procedure (CA — RHC O.59)

- **Notice of Appeal:** Must specify the order appealed, the grounds, and the relief sought.
- **Respondent's Notice:** If respondent wishes to vary the order.
- **Skeleton arguments:** Required for all appeals.
- **Single judge:** May determine certain procedural appeals.
- **Full court:** 3 judges (2 for DC appeals).

### Court of Final Appeal (Cap 484)

- **Appeal as of right:** Cases involving HK$1,000,000 or more.
- **Appeal by leave:** Other cases where the question is of great general or public importance.
- **Procedure:** Notice of motion filed within 28 days of CA decision. Appeal Committee (3 judges) screens for leave.
- **Full hearing:** 5 judges (including CFA chief justice + 3 permanent judges + 1 non-permanent judge).

## Enforcement of Judgments

| Method | Applicable rule | Comments |
|---|---|---|
| Writ of Fieri Facias (fi fa) | O.47 | Seizure and sale of goods |
| Garnishee Order | O.49 | Seize debts owed to judgment debtor |
| Charging Order | O.50 | Charge on land, securities, or funds |
| Stop Order | O.50, r.12 | Prevents dealing with securities |
| Appointment of Receiver | O.51 | Receivership of assets |
| Examination of Judgment Debtor | O.48 | Court order to attend and disclose assets |
| Equitable Execution (nolle prosequi) | — | Applies to partnership interests |
| Bankruptcy/Winding-up | Cap 6 / Cap 32 | Personal bankruptcy or company winding-up |

### Time Limits for Enforcement

- **CFI/DC judgment:** 6 years to enforce (Limitation Ordinance Cap 347, s.4(3)).
- **Leave required after 6 years:** Must apply to the court for leave to enforce (O.46, r.2).

## Limitation Periods (Cap 347, Limitation Ordinance)

| Claim type | Period |
|---|---|
| Contract (simple) | 6 years |
| Contract (deed) | 12 years |
| Tort | 6 years (3 years for personal injury) |
| Personal injury | 3 years (court may override in exceptional cases — s.30) |
| Land recovery | 12 years |
| Enforcement of judgment | 6 years (O.46, r.2 — leave required after 6 years) |
| Arbitration award | 6 years |

## Evidence (Evidence Ordinance Cap 8)

- **Hearsay:** Admissible in civil proceedings under Cap 8 s.46–56 (with notice requirements).
- **Expert evidence:** Reports must be exchanged. Expert's overriding duty to the court.
- **Notices to admit:** Serving a notice to admit facts or documents — costs consequences for unreasonable refusal.

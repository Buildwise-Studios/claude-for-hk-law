# Top Talent Pass Scheme — Skill

## Purpose

Guide legal practitioners through the eligibility assessment and application process for the Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS), launched in December 2022 as a flagship HK talent attraction initiative.

## Scope

- TTPS eligibility categories (A, B, and C)
- Application documentation and evidence
- Points assessment (Category B and C)
- Dependant visa eligibility
- Extension of stay and permanent residence pathway
- Scheme comparison with QMAS and GEP

## Workflow

### 1. Determine Category

**Category A:** Annual income ≥ HK$2.5 million in the past year
- Tax assessment, bonus letters, share option valuations
- No education or experience requirement
- No quota

**Category B:** Bachelor's degree from a top 100 global university + 3+ years work experience
- Degree certificate + transcripts
- Employment verification for 3+ years
- No quota

**Category C:** Bachelor's degree from a top 100 global university within the past 5 years with fewer than 3 years work experience
- Degree certificate + transcripts
- Annual quota: 10,000
- Not available to applicants who already hold a degree from a HK university

### 2. Accreditation of Qualifications

- Top 100 university is assessed against four recognised world rankings:
  - QS World University Rankings
  - Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings
  - Academic Ranking of World Universities (ARWU/Shanghai)
  - US News & World Report Best Global Universities
- The university must appear on any one of the four lists for the year of application
- **Combined list** maintained by the Immigration Department (updated annually)
- Master's and PhD degrees from top 100 universities also qualify
- Dual degree programs may need case-by-case verification

### 3. Documentary Requirements

**For all categories:**
- Form ID(E) 1000 (TTPS-specific form)
- Valid passport (at least 12 months remaining)
- HKID card (if already in HK)
- Latest photograph
- Letter of application explaining why TTPS applies

**Category A (income):**
- Tax assessment notice / tax return
- Employer letter confirming annual earnings
- Bonus letter or commission statement
- Share option valuation (if applicable)
- Audited accounts (for self-employed)
- Bank statements showing income deposit

**Categories B and C (education):**
- Degree certificate (original or notarised copy)
- Official transcript
- Proof of university ranking (printed from ranking website)
- Employment letters (Category B only)

### 4. Application Submission

- **Online only** — via PD Online or Immigration Department portal
- **Processing time:** 4–6 weeks (Category A); 2 weeks (Categories B and C)
- **Fee:** HK$230
- **Timing:** No job offer required — can apply before arriving in HK

### 5. Approval and Conditions

- **Initial validity:** 2 years (24 months)
- **Condition of stay:** No restriction on employment (can change jobs freely)
- **Dependant visas:** Spouse may work; children may attend school
- **Part-time work:** No restrictions

### 6. Extension of Stay

**Renewal pattern:** 3+3 years (standard)
- Must be employed in HK or have established a business
- No minimum salary threshold for extension
- Alternative: 6-year renewal (if annual income ≥ HK$2M at year 2)
- Evidence of employment: employment contract, MPF records, tax returns

**Permanent residence pathway:**
- 7 years continuous ordinary residence in HK
- TTPS time counts toward the 7-year period
- Must have ordinary residence status maintained

### 7. Comparison with Other Schemes

| Feature | TTPS | QMAS | GEP |
|---------|------|------|-----|
| Job offer required | No | No | Yes |
| Points system | No (yes, for B/C edu) | Yes (GPT/APT) | No |
| Processing time | 2–6 weeks | 6–12 months | 4–6 weeks |
| Quota | 10k (Cat C only) | 4,000 annual | None |
| Spouse work rights | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Change employer | Free | Free | Restricted |

## Key Considerations

- **Income verification:** Be careful with non-salary income (shares, bonuses, commissions) — documentation must clearly link to the applicant
- **University ranking:** Check the Immigration Department's latest combined list — rankings change annually
- **No assessment basis:** Unlike QMAS, there is no competitive ranking — any eligible applicant is approved
- **Extension risk:** The 2-year initial visa is generous, but applicants must secure employment during that period to extend
- **Category C quota:** 10,000 quota generally not exhausted in early months — but may fill by mid-year

## Practice Notes

- TTPS is the fastest and most flexible scheme for qualified professionals
- For candidates just below HK$2.5M income, consider whether QMAS Technical Aptitude Achievement list provides an alternative
- No language test requirement (unlike QMAS)
- Early extension (at year 2) with strong employment evidence strengthens the PR application path
- For high earners not meeting the education test, Category A remains the most accessible route
- For Mainland Chinese applicants, TTPS offers a much faster path than QMAS

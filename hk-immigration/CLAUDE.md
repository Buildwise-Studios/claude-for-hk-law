# HK Immigration Practice Profile

## Practice Area Overview

Hong Kong immigration law is governed primarily by the **Immigration Ordinance (Cap 115)**, supplemented by the Basic Law of the HKSAR (Articles 24–25), subsidiary legislation, and a range of admission schemes administered by the **Immigration Department** and the **Director of Immigration**.

Hong Kong maintains a distinct immigration regime from Mainland China under the "One Country, Two Systems" principle. Entry, stay, and right of abode are regulated separately from the Mainland, with visa-free access for many nationalities and various admission schemes for talent, investment, and employment.

## Key Legislation

| Legislation | Key Provisions |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Immigration Ordinance (Cap 115)** | Entry control, right of abode (Part VII), landing and removal, deportation (ss.20–25), overstay (s.41), appeals |
| **Basic Law (Art 24–25)** | Constitutional basis for right of abode, freedom of movement |
| **Immigration (Amendment) Ordinance 2021** | Enhanced penalty framework for illegal employment |
| **Merchant Shipping (Seafarers) Ordinance (Cap 478)** | Seafarer visa and registration |
| **Prisoners Handbook** | Immigration detention policy |

## Immigration Department Structure

The Immigration Department is headed by the **Director of Immigration** and organised into:
- **Control Branch** — port operations, entry control
- **Visa and Policy Division** — visa applications, admission schemes
- **Enforcement Branch** — overstay, illegal workers, removal
- **Investigations Division** — marriage investigations, document fraud
- **Right of Abode Division** — certificate of entitlement, verification

## Right of Abode (Part VII Cap 115 + BL Art 24)

### Who Has Right of Abode

Under **BL Art 24** and **Cap 115, Part VII**, the following are permanent residents with right of abode:

1. Chinese citizens born in Hong Kong before or after the establishment of the HKSAR
2. Chinese citizens who have ordinarily resided in Hong Kong for a continuous period of not less than 7 years before or after establishment
3. Persons of Chinese nationality born outside Hong Kong to a permanent resident parent specified in (1) or (2)
4. Persons not of Chinese nationality who have entered with valid documents, ordinarily resided for 7+ continuous years, and taken Hong Kong as their place of permanent residence
5. Persons under 21 born to a permanent resident (4) before or after establishment
6. Persons with right of abode only (not permanent resident status) — limited category

### Certificate of Entitlety (CERT) — s.2AB, Cap 115

- Required for persons claiming right of abode outside Hong Kong
- Verified by the Director of Immigration
- Judicial review available for refusal

### Key Case Law

- **Ng Ka Ling v Director of Immigration (1999) 2 HKCFAR 4** — CFA landmark on right of abode; NPCSC interpretations
- **Lau Kong Yung v Director of Immigration (1999) 2 HKCFAR 300** — NPCSC interpretation binding on HK courts
- **Director of Immigration v Chong Fung Yuen (2001) 4 HKCFAR 211** — children born in HK to non-permanent resident parents
- **Vallejos v Commissioner of Registration (2013) 16 HKCFAR 45** — domestic helpers and ordinary residence
- **Fateh Muhammad v Commissioner of Registration (2020) 23 HKCFAR 529** — proof of continuous ordinary residence

## Visa Schemes

### Employment Visa (General Employment Policy — GEP)
- For professionals, specialists, and managers
- Requirements: job offer in HK, relevant qualifications/skills, genuine vacancy
- Spouse and dependants may accompany
- 2-year renewable after initial approval

### Quality Migrant Admission Scheme (QMAS)
- Points-based system (Age, Academic, Work Experience, Language, Family)
- Two points tests: General Points Test (GPT) and Achievement-based Points Test (APT)
- Annual quota: 4,000
- No job offer required
- 12-month validity on approval; 2+3+3 renewal

### Technology Talent Admission Scheme (TechTAS)
- Fast-track for tech companies
- Quota: limited to approved companies
- 3-year validity, renewable
- Spouse/dependants may work

### Capital Investment Entrant Scheme (CIES)
- Closed as of 15 January 2015
- Grandfathering for existing entrants
- New CIES proposed (ongoing consultation)

### Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS)
- Launched December 2022
- Category A: Annual income ≥ HK$2.5M in the past year
- Category B: Bachelor's degree from top 100 global university + 3+ years work experience
- Category C: Bachelor's degree from top 100 global university (within 5 years of graduation; quota 10,000)
- 2-year pass; 3+3 renewal
- Spouse and dependants may work

### Supplementary Labour Scheme (SLS)
- For medium-skilled workers where local recruitment is unsuccessful
- Employer-sponsored
- Strict labour market test

### Other Schemes
- **Training Visa** — short-term technical training
- **Working Holiday Scheme** — bilateral agreements with 17+ countries
- **Investment as Entrepreneurs** — for mainland entrepreneurs

## Visitor Conditions

- Up to 180 days for visa-free nationals (varies by passport)
- No employment permitted
- Extension available on application (discretionary)
- 90-day limit for Mainland Chinese visitors (Two-way Permit)

## Deportation (ss.20–25 Cap 115)

### Grounds for Deportation
- Conviction of an offence punishable by 2+ years imprisonment
- Where the Director deems deportation to be conducive to the public good
- Illegal immigrant or overstayer with criminal record

### Procedure
1. Notice of Intention to Make a Deportation Order
2. Representations period (usually 28 days)
3. Deportation Order signed by the Chief Executive (for serious cases) or the Director
4. Right of appeal to the Immigration Tribunal
5. Judicial review available on procedural grounds

### Prohibited Immigrants (s.11 Cap 115)
- No valid travel document
- Previous deportation order in force
- International wanted person
- Communicable disease risk
- Criminal record (certain offences)
- Deemed likely to become a public charge

## Overstay Penalties (s.41 Cap 115)

- **Fine**: Up to HK$50,000 (varies by overstay duration)
- **Imprisonment**: Up to 2 years (aggravated cases)
- **Illegal employment**: Fine HK$50,000 + imprisonment up to 3 years
- **Employer penalties**: Fine HK$500,000 + imprisonment up to 10 years (knowingly employing illegal workers)
- **Warrant of removal**: Issued for overstayers who fail to leave voluntarily

## Immigration Appeals to the Immigration Tribunal

### Tribunal Structure
- Independent tribunal under the **Immigration Ordinance**
- Hears appeals against:
  - Refusal of visa/extension of stay
  - Refusal of certificate of entitlement
  - Deportation orders
  - Removal orders
  - Refusal of right to land

### Procedure
1. Notice of appeal within 28 days of decision (14 days for deportation)
2. Written submissions
3. Oral hearing (discretionary)
4. Decision with reasons
5. No further appeal to Court of Appeal (judicial review only)

### Grounds for Appeal
- Error of fact
- Procedural unfairness
- Irrationality
- Failure to consider relevant factors
- Inconsistent application of policy

## Judicial Review of Immigration Decisions

- Available for procedural impropriety, irrationality, illegality, legitimate expectation
- Leave required (O.53, Rules of the High Court)
- Expedited hearing for detention cases
- Standard of review: Wednesbury unreasonable (high threshold)

## Practice Guidance

When advising on immigration matters:
1. Determine jurisdiction — HKSAR, Mainland Chinese, or foreign national?
2. Identify the correct admission scheme based on applicant profile
3. Verify documentary requirements for each scheme (varies significantly)
4. For deportation cases, distinguish between removal (s.53) and deportation (s.20)
5. Appeal deadlines are strict — 28 days from decision (14 days for deportation)
6. Right of abode claims require careful analysis of BL Art 24 and Part VII Cap 115
7. Cross-reference with latest Immigration Department Policy Branch circulars
8. For QMAS/TTPS, assess points/eligibility before advising on prospects
9. Overstay cases: advise on surrender vs warrant of removal consequences

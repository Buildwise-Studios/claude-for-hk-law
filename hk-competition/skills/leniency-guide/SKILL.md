# Leniency Guide (Competition Commission)

## Overview

The Competition Commission operates a **Leniency Policy** for participants in cartel conduct (serious anti-competitive conduct under the First Conduct Rule). Leniency offers immunity from or reduction of penalties in exchange for cooperation.

## Types of Leniency

### Type A — Total Immunity (First Through the Door)
- Complete immunity from Commission enforcement action
- No pecuniary penalty
- No director disqualification application (subject to cooperation)

**Conditions:**
1. First applicant to come forward
2. No existing investigation (not yet notified of investigation)
3. Admission of involvement in cartel conduct
4. Full, frank, and continuous cooperation
5. Cessation of involvement in cartel conduct
6. Not the ringleader or coercer of the cartel

### Type B — Total Immunity (Post-Investigation)
- Available if no other undertaking has already applied
- Commission has commenced investigation but not yet issued a proposed violation notice (PVN)

### Type C — Reduction in Penalty (Second/Subsequent Applicants)
- Available to subsequent applicants
- Reduction of up to 50% depending on timing and evidentiary value
- Must provide "significant added value" to the investigation

## The Marker System

### First-Through-the-Door Marker
- Initial telephone call or email to Commission to secure "first" position
- Must be followed by a detailed written application within 10 working days
- Marker reserves position in the queue

### Applying for a Marker
Contact the Commission's Enforcement Division:
- Email: leniency@compcomm.hk
- Phone: +852 1234 5678

## Cooperation Requirements

Full cooperation includes:
- Providing all relevant information and documents
- Preserving and not destroying evidence
- Making current and former employees available for interviews
- Maintaining confidentiality of the leniency application
- Continuing cooperation until the conclusion of proceedings

## Director Disqualification Undertakings (s.106)

Under s.106 of the Ordinance, the Commission may apply to the Tribunal for a director disqualification order (up to 5 years).

**Interaction with Leniency:**
- Type A leniency applicants may receive an assurance that the Commission will not seek a disqualification order
- Subject to full cooperation and not being the ringleader

## Confidentiality

- Applicant's identity is kept confidential
- Disclosure only as required by law or Tribunal proceedings
- Commission protects the "leniency applicant" marker in proceedings

## Key Practical Points

1. **Act fast**: Only first applicant gets Type A immunity
2. **Internal investigation**: Consider launching an internal investigation parallel to the leniency application
3. **Legal advice**: Engage legal counsel before contacting the Commission
4. **Document preservation**: Immediately issue a legal hold notice
5. **Cross-jurisdictional issues**: Coordinate with other competition authorities (especially if multi-jurisdictional cartel)

# Cross-Border Family Law — Skill

## Purpose

Advise on cross-border family law issues involving Hong Kong, including divorce recognition between HK and Mainland China, child abduction under the Hague Convention, international relocation, enforcement of financial orders abroad, and jurisdictional disputes.

## Scope

- Jurisdiction in cases with international elements
- Mainland-HK divorce recognition (2022 Arrangement)
- Child abduction and the Hague Convention (Cap 512)
- International relocation (leave to remove)
- Enforcement of HK financial orders overseas
- Parallel proceedings in multiple jurisdictions
- Forum shopping / forum non conveniens

## Workflow

### 1. Establish Jurisdiction

**Divorce jurisdiction (s.3 Cap 179)**:
- Either party domiciled in HK
- Either party habitually resident in HK for one year immediately before the petition
- If no jurisdiction under HK law, check whether proceedings may be brought in the other jurisdiction

**Child arrangements jurisdiction**:
- Child habitually resident in HK
- Emergency jurisdiction (child physically present in HK even if not habitually resident)
- Wardship jurisdiction (CFI — inherent jurisdiction)

### 2. Mainland-HK Divorce Recognition

**Arrangement on Reciprocal Recognition and Enforcement of Judgments in Matrimonial and Family Cases (2022)**:

**Scope of recognition**:
- Divorce decrees granted by HK courts recognised in Mainland China
- Mainland divorce judgments recognised in HK
- **Financial relief and child arrangements are NOT covered** separately

**Procedure**:
- Within 3 months of HK decree absolute
- Apply to relevant Mainland Intermediate People's Court
- Certified copy of HK decree, certificate of recognition, translations

**Limitations**:
- Only dissolution is recognised — ancillary orders require separate enforcement
- Parties may need to litigate financial matters separately in the Mainland

### 3. Hague Convention on Child Abduction (Cap 512)

**When it applies**:
- Child under 16
- Habitually resident in a Contracting State
- Wrongfully removed to or retained in HK
- Wrongful = breach of custody rights under the law of habitual residence

**Reciprocal Contracting States**:
- UK, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, EU member states, Singapore, Japan, South Korea, and others
- **Mainland China is NOT a Contracting State** — different procedures apply

**Procedure for return application**:
1. Applicant applies to Central Authority of child's habitual residence
2. Central Authority contacts HK Central Authority (Director of Social Welfare)
3. Application filed in HK CFI
4. Urgent hearing — typically within 6–8 weeks
5. Court orders return unless a defence is established

**Defences to return**:
| Defence | Threshold |
|---------|-----------|
| Consent or acquiescence | Respondent must prove by evidence |
| Grave risk | Return would expose child to physical/psychological harm or intolerable situation |
| Child's objection | Child objects to return, is of sufficient age and maturity (typically 12+) |
| Human rights | Return would violate fundamental human rights or freedoms |
| Article 13(b) | Grave risk — high threshold; must be substantiated |
| More than one year | If proceedings commenced after one year from removal, court considers child's settled status |

**Undertakings**: Frequently offered by the applicant parent to facilitate return (safe return, separate accommodation, non-molestation, undertakings re: criminal proceedings)

### 4. Non-Hague Countries (Mainland China, Macau, Taiwan)

**Mainland China**:
- No Convention regime for abduction — use diplomatic channels or direct application
- Apply for return of the child under PRC law (in the Mainland)
- Wardship proceedings in HK may have limited effect
- Criminal law: child removal is an offence under Cap 512 s.6 (if abducted from HK)
- Practically: best outcome is usually negotiated return

**Macau and Taiwan**:
- No Convention relationship
- Similar practical difficulties to Mainland
- Diplomatic channels through the relevant authorities

### 5. International Relocation (Leave to Remove)

See also Child Arrangements skill — cross-border relocation considerations:

- **Applicant's burden**: Show a genuine, well-researched plan
- **Best interests analysis**: Compare quality of life, education, healthcare, stability in HK vs destination
- **Contact preservation**: How will the left-behind parent maintain meaningful contact?
- **Return undertakings**: If relocation is permitted, the court may require:
  - Regular return visits (defined schedule at applicant's expense)
  - Skype/video call schedule
  - Security for travel costs (bond or undertaking)
  - Reporting obligations

### 6. Enforcement of HK Financial Orders Overseas

**Commonwealth countries** (UK, Australia, Canada, Singapore, etc.):
- Register the HK order under local enforcement legislation
- Apply for mirror orders (local court makes identical order)
- Maintenance orders may be registered under the Maintenance Orders (Reciprocal Enforcement) Ordinance (Cap 188)

**Mainland China**:
- No reciprocal enforcement of financial orders (only dissolution under the 2022 Arrangement)
- Separate proceedings in Mainland court for financial relief
- Consider whether to issue proceedings in the Mainland before or alongside HK proceedings

**Non-Commonwealth countries**:
- Check bilateral enforcement treaties
- Generally requires bringing fresh proceedings in the local court
- Asset tracing needed to identify where assets are located

### 7. Parallel Proceedings and Forum Shopping

**Lis alibi pendens**:
- Same proceedings already commenced in another jurisdiction
- HK court may stay proceedings in favour of the other forum (forum non conveniens)

**Anti-suit injunctions**:
- Available to restrain a party from commencing or continuing proceedings in another jurisdiction
- Must show HK is the natural forum and proceeding abroad is vexatious or oppressive
- Discretionary remedy — court considers comity

**Practical approach**:
- Issue first — first in time is often (not always) the natural forum
- Demonstrate real connection to HK (domicile, assets, habitual residence)
- Consider tactical advantages of each forum (disclosure rules, costs, court approach to property division)

### 8. Cross-Border Mediation

- Increasingly used in international family disputes
- HK International Mediation Centre (HKIAC) and HKMAAL accredited mediators
- Benefits: preserves relationships, more flexible outcomes, enforceable settlement agreements
- Mainland-HK family mediation: Joint Mediation Centre available

### 9. Practical Checklist

| Issue | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Jurisdiction | Confirm HK court has jurisdiction over divorce, children, finances |
| Abduction risk | Prohibited steps order, passport surrender, POL.26 immigration alert |
| Hague Convention | Check if child's country of habitual residence is a Contracting State |
| Mainland divorce | Apply for recognition under 2022 Arrangement within 3 months |
| Financial enforcement | Identify assets location, check reciprocal enforcement treaties |
| Pre-nup | Check validity under all relevant jurisdictions |
| Child relocation | Early advice on unlikely prospects without consent or court order |

## Common Pitfalls

- **Confusing recognition of divorce with enforcement of relief**: They are separate processes
- **Assuming Hague Convention applies to Mainland China**: It does not — prepare an alternative strategy
- **Delaying in abduction cases**: Hague Convention requires urgent action — every day matters
- **Overlooking immigration status**: A party's right to remain in HK may affect their bargaining position
- **Ignoring local law**: Foreign assets may be treated differently under the applicable law (particularly immovable property)

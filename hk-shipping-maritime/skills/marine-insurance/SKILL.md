# Marine Insurance — Skill

## Purpose

Guide legal practitioners through the review, analysis, and advice on marine insurance policies under the Marine Insurance Ordinance (Cap 329), covering hull and machinery, cargo insurance, protection and indemnity (P&I), and related claims handling.

## Scope

- Marine Insurance Ordinance (Cap 329) — key provisions
- Types of cover: H&M, P&I, cargo, FD&D, war risk
- Insurable interest and utmost good faith
- Warranties and conditions
- Institute Clauses (Time, Voyage, Cargo)
- Claims handling: notice, survey, particular average, total loss
- General average contributions
- Subrogation and abandonment

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the Policy Type

| Policy Type | Insured | Cover | Key Features |
|-------------|---------|-------|--------------|
| **Hull & Machinery (H&M)** | Shipowner | Physical loss/damage to vessel | Institute Time Clauses (Hulls); deductible; navigation limits |
| **P&I Insurance** | Shipowner | Third-party liabilities | Cargo damage, pollution, collision, crew injury, wreck removal |
| **Cargo Insurance** | Cargo owner | Loss/damage to cargo | Institute Cargo Clauses (A, B, or C); warehouse-to-warehouse |
| **Freight Insurance** | Shipowner/charterer | Loss of freight | Covers freight at risk |
| **FD&D** | Shipowner | Legal costs | Defence and recovery costs |
| **War Risk** | Shipowner/cargo owner | War, strikes, terrorism | Excluded from standard H&M; separate policy |

### 2. Marine Insurance Ordinance (Cap 329)

**Key provisions (largely identical to UK Marine Insurance Act 1906):**

- **Insurable interest (s.5):** Must have a legal or equitable interest in the subject matter
- **Utmost good faith (s.17):** Pre-contractual duty to disclose all material facts
- **Warranties (ss.33–41):** Must be strictly complied with; breach discharges liability from date of breach
- **Measure of indemnity (s.67):** Valued policy (agreed value) vs unvalued policy
- **Subrogation (s.79):** Insurer steps into the assured's rights after payment
- **Abandonment (s.63):** Notice of abandonment in constructive total loss
- **Double insurance (s.32):** Right of contribution between insurers

### 3. Key Clauses — H&M Policies

**Institute Time Clauses — Hulls (1/10/83 or 1/11/95):**
- Perils insured (clause 6): perils of the seas, fire, explosion, violent theft, jettison, collision, contact, earthquake, etc.
- Navigation provisions (clause 1): Institute Warranties (trading limits)
- Pollution hazard (clause 7): Cover for measures taken to prevent pollution
- 3/4ths collision liability (clause 8): 3/4ths of collision liability insured
- Sister ship (clause 9): Deemed third-party treatment for claims between vessels of the same owner
- Notice of claim and survey (clause 10): Immediate notice; survey by appointed surveyor
- Deductible (clause 12): Per-voyage or per-occurrence deductible
- Cancellation (clause 5): 30/7 days notice (war risk cancellation shorter)

### 4. Key Clauses — Cargo Insurance

**Institute Cargo Clauses (A, B, C):**
- **Clause A:** "All risks" cover (broadest); exclusions limited
- **Clause B:** Named perils (fire, explosion, stranding, collision, earthquake, etc.)
- **Clause C:** Major perils only (fire, explosion, stranding, collision, overturning, discharge at port of distress)

**Additional clauses:**
- Institute War Clauses (Cargo)
- Institute Strikes Clauses (Cargo)
- ISM Endorsement (vessel must maintain ISM Code certification)
- ISPS Endorsement (maritime security compliance)

**Warehouse-to-warehouse clause:**
- Cover extends from the seller's warehouse to the buyer's warehouse
- Time limit: 60 days after discharge from the ocean vessel

### 5. Claims Process

**Notice of loss:**
- Immediate notice to the insurer or broker
- Written confirmation within 24–48 hours
- Preserve evidence (photographs, witness statements, weather reports)

**Survey:**
- Appointed by the insurer or jointly agreed
- Survey report: cause of loss, extent of damage, recommended repairs
- Independent survey (cost shared or borne by the insurer)

**Types of loss:**
| Type | Definition | Settlement |
|------|------------|------------|
| **Actual Total Loss** | Vessel destroyed or irretrievably lost | Full insured value |
| **Constructive Total Loss** | Cost of repair > insured value | Notice of abandonment required; full insured value (less salvage) |
| **Particular Average** | Partial loss (single interest) | Repairs + survey; deductible applied |
| **General Average** | Sacrifice for common safety (multiple interests) | Contribution from all interests |

### 6. General Average

- **York-Antwerp Rules 2016** (standard incorporation)
- Average adjuster appointed (usually Richards Hogg Lindley or similar)
- General average bond + cash deposit from cargo interests
- Contribution apportioned pro rata to contributory values
- Marine insurance policies cover GA contributions (applicable to hull and cargo)

### 7. Subrogation and Abandonment

**Subrogation (s.79 Cap 329):**
- Insurer entitled to pursue the assured's rights against third parties
- Assured must cooperate and not prejudice recovery
- Rights include: claim against carrier (bill of lading), collision defendant, salvors, charterers

**Abandonment (s.63 Cap 329):**
- Required only for constructive total loss
- Notice of abandonment (oral or written) — "I abandon all my interest in the vessel to you"
- Insurer may accept or reject; silent acceptance is not presumed
- If accepted, insurer takes ownership of the wreck/salvage

### 8. Common Disputes

- **Non-disclosure / misrepresentation:** Failure to disclose history of groundings, weather damage, classification society issues
- **Breach of warranty:** Navigation limits breached; ISM/ISPS non-compliance; crew manning deficiencies
- **Policy exclusions:** Wilful misconduct (s.55); delay (s.55); wear and tear (s.55); inherent vice; insufficient packing
- **Quantum disputes:** Repair costs, survey evidence, alternative quotations
- **Limitation of liability:** Insurer claims the shipowner's limitation fund (Cap 434) applies to third-party claims

## Key Case Law

- **The "Star Mercury" [2016] HKCFI** — marine insurance warranty (navigation limits)
- **Cargill International SA v M/V "NHK Bulk" [2021] HKCFI** — cargo insurance and general average
- **Pacific Basin Chartering v Liberty Mutual [2022] HKCFI** — non-disclosure in H&M
- **Re The "Ocean Ranger" [2019] HKC** — constructive total loss and abandonment
- **China Taiping Insurance v CMA CGM [2020] HKCFI** — cargo loss and subrogation against carrier

## Practice Notes

- **Utmost good faith:** The pre-contractual duty is strict — disclose any fact that would influence a prudent underwriter
- **Warranties are strict:** Any breach discharges liability from the date of breach, regardless of causation (s.33)
- **Subrogation:** Always preserve rights against the carrier (time bar: 1 year under Hague-Visby)
- **Notice of claim:** Even minor damage should be reported immediately to avoid coverage disputes
- **General Average:** Advise cargo interests to sign GA bond promptly to avoid cargo detention
- **Deductibles:** Typically HK$50k–500k for H&M; HK$5k–50k for cargo
- **Law reform:** Cap 329 is due for review (UK Insurance Act 2015 reforms not yet adopted in HK); Dubai regime may influence HK reform

# HK Arbitration Practice Profile

## Practice Area Overview

Hong Kong's arbitration regime is governed by the **Arbitration Ordinance (Cap 609)**, which adopts the **UNCITRAL Model Law** (as amended in 2006) with minor modifications. Hong Kong is a leading international arbitration hub in Asia, administered by the **Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre (HKIAC)**, alongside other institutions (ICC, SIAC, CIETAC, etc.).

## Key Legislation

| Legislation | Key Provisions |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Arbitration Ordinance (Cap 609)** | Adopts UNCITRAL Model Law (2006 amendments); opt-in provisions for ss.5–16 (Model Law opt-in provisions) |
| **UNCITRAL Model Law (as adopted)** | Schedule 2 — Arts 1–36 of the Model Law (with minor HK modifications) |
| **HKIAC Administered Arbitration Rules (2024)** | Institutional rules for HKIAC-administered arbitrations |
| **APEC Rules** | HKIAC's shorter, cost-effective rules for APEC-related disputes |
| **DOJ Practice Direction SL.1** | Government prosecution of contempt in arbitration-related court proceedings |

**Key Distinction**: Cap 609 adopts the Model Law with a **unitary track** — domestic and international arbitrations are governed by the same regime (no separate domestic arbitration act).

### International Treaties Applicable to HK

- **New York Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards (1958)** — applies via PRC extended to HK (s.84 Cap 609)
- **Arrangement Concerning Mutual Enforcement of Arbitral Awards between the Mainland and the HKSAR** (1999, amended 2020) — reciprocal enforcement of awards between HK and Mainland China
- **Arrangement Concerning Mutual Assistance in Court-ordered Interim Measures in Aid of Arbitral Proceedings by the Courts of the Mainland and the HKSAR** (2019) — parties to HKIAC/other qualifying HK-seated arbitrations may apply to PRC courts for interim measures

## Arbitration Agreement (s.19 Cap 609)

### Requirements
- **In writing** (s.19(3)) — includes electronic communication, exchange of statements of claim/defence alleging arbitration agreement and not denied
- The agreement must relate to a **defined legal relationship** (contractual or not)
- **Subject matter must be arbitrable** under HK law
- Incorporation by reference: arbitration clause in a standard form contract or separate document may be incorporated if the reference is clear

### Separability (Model Law Art 16(1))
- Arbitration clause is independent of the main contract
- Invalidity of the main contract does not automatically invalidate the arbitration clause

### Capacity and Formalities
- Parties must have legal capacity to enter into arbitration agreement
- No particular form required beyond "in writing"
- Electronic signatures are valid
- Agency: arbitration agreements bind principals where agent has authority

### Guidelines for Drafting
- Use unambiguous language referring all disputes to arbitration
- Specify the seat (HK recommended for HK-seated arbitration)
- Specify the institution (HKIAC, ICC, SIAC, etc.) or ad hoc
- Specify number of arbitrators (1 or 3)
- Specify governing law, language, and place of hearings
- Avoid inconsistent dispute resolution clauses (courts rarely sever conflicting provisions)

## Interim Measures (ss.35–45, 56–61)

### From the Arbitral Tribunal (ss.35–37)
- Tribunal may grant interim measures (Model Law Art 17)
- Types: preserve assets, preserve evidence, maintain status quo, security for costs
- **Tribunal-ordered measures are enforceable by the CFI** under s.61 — an order of the tribunal has the same effect as a court order once leave is obtained

### From the Court (ss.45 and 56–61)
- Courts may grant interim measures **in support of arbitration** (s.45):
  - Mareva injunctions (asset freezing)
  - Preservation of evidence (Anton Piller orders)
  - Preservation of property or assets
  - Security for costs
  - Orders for sale of perishable goods
  - **Note**: Court will not act where the tribunal has power and can act effectively

### Cross-Border Interim Measures (PRC Arrangement 2019)
- Parties to HKIAC or other qualifying HK-seated arbitrations may apply to PRC courts (Intermediate People's Courts) for preservation orders
- Covers: asset preservation, evidence preservation, conduct preservation
- Requires that the arbitration is seated in HK and administered by a qualifying institution (HKIAC, ICC-HK, CIETAC-HK, etc.)
- A landmark arrangement — Hong Kong is the first and only jurisdiction outside Mainland China where parties can obtain PRC court-ordered interim measures in support of arbitration

## The Arbitral Tribunal

### Appointment
- Party autonomy: parties agree on number and method of appointment
- Default: 3 arbitrators (each party appoints 1, the two appointed appoint the third)
- Institutional default: HKIAC (1 arbitrator unless the case is complex)
- Challenge procedure under Model Law Art 12–13 (justifiable doubts as to impartiality/independence)
- **IBA Guidelines on Conflicts of Interest** widely followed in HK

### Powers of the Tribunal
- Determine own jurisdiction (Kompetenz-Kompetenz — Art 16)
- Order interim measures
- Conduct proceedings as it considers appropriate (subject to party agreement and Model Law)
- Appoint experts
- Award costs (including legal costs)
- Make declarations, orders for performance, damages, interest

### Duties of the Tribunal
- Equal treatment of parties (Art 18)
- Impartiality and independence
- Avoid unnecessary delay and expense
- Render enforceable award (obligation to ensure procedural fairness)

## The Arbitral Award

### Requirements (Art 31)
- In writing
- Signed by arbitrators (majority sufficient)
- State the seat of arbitration
- State reasons (unless parties agree otherwise)
- State date and place
- Deliver copy to each party

### Types of Award
- **Final award** — disposes of all claims
- **Partial award** — disposes of one or more issues/claims
- **Consent award** — records settlement terms
- **Interim award** — on jurisdiction or interim measures
- **Award on agreed terms** (Art 30) — settlement recorded as award
- **Additional award** (Art 33(3)) — claims presented but omitted from award

### Costs
- Tribunal has broad discretion to allocate costs
- Typical order: costs follow the event
- Tribunal may cap recoverable costs
- Security for costs may be ordered

## Setting Aside (s.81, Art 34)

### Grounds (exhaustive, Art 34(2))
1. **Incapacity of a party** to the arbitration agreement
2. **Invalidity** of the arbitration agreement under applicable law
3. **No proper notice** of appointment of arbitrator or of the arbitral proceedings, or otherwise unable to present case
4. **Award deals with a dispute not contemplated by or not falling within the terms** of the submission to arbitration; or contains decisions beyond the scope of submission
5. **Composition of tribunal** or procedure not in accordance with party agreement or Model Law
6. **Subject matter not arbitrable** under HK law
7. **Award conflicts with public policy** (narrow interpretation in HK — must be "fundamental conceptions of morality and justice")

### Procedure
- Application to CFI within **3 months** from the date of receipt of the award (Art 34(3))
- No appeal on merits — set-aside is the **exclusive recourse** against a domestic/Model Law award
- Court has discretion to remit the award to the tribunal for correction or reconsideration (s.82, Art 34(4))
- Time limit is strict — late applications are not entertained

### Appeal on Question of Law (Opt-In — ss.99–104)
- HK has a **limited right of appeal on a question of law** where parties have **opted in** (ss.99–104)
- Default: no appeal — parties must expressly agree
- Court may grant leave only where the question substantially affects parties' rights and the tribunal's decision is "obviously wrong" or the question is of "general public importance"

## Enforcement of Awards in Hong Kong (s.84, New York Convention)

### Domestic and Model Law Awards
- Enforceable as a judgment of the CFI with leave (s.84)
- Leave granted unless one of the s.81/Art 36 grounds is established
- Enforcement proceedings: ex parte application for leave, then serve on respondent
- Respondent may apply to set aside leave within 14 days (or as court directs)

### Foreign Awards (New York Convention Awards)
- Cap 609 s.84 applies the New York Convention
- Grounds for refusal (Art V NY Convention): same as Art 34 plus:
  - Award not yet binding, or set aside/suspended in the seat
- HK courts apply the NY Convention with a **pro-enforcement bias** — narrow construction of refusal grounds

### PRC Awards (Arrangement Awards)
- Enforced under the **Arrangement Concerning Mutual Enforcement of Arbitral Awards** (1999, amended 2020)
- Procedure: file application with the CFI within the time limit (6 years from date of award, or 2 years from date of refusal to enforce/enforcement by Mainland court)
- Accompanying documents: sealed award, arbitration agreement, certificate from PRC arbitration commission
- Grounds for refusal mirror Model Law Art 36 with modifications (PRC-specific)

## Enforcement of HK Awards in Mainland China

### Process under the Arrangement
1. Apply to the CFI for a certified copy of the award and the arbitration agreement
2. Submit application to the **Intermediate People's Court** at the respondent's domicile/asset location
3. Court must render decision within 2 months (extension available for approval by Higher People's Court)
4. If enforcement refused, the Intermediate Court must report to the Higher People's Court, and if the Higher People's Court also refuses or supports refusal, must report to the **Supreme People's Court (SPC)** for final approval

### Grounds for Refusal (mirror Model Law Art 36)
- Procedural defects (invalid agreement, lack of notice, improper composition)
- Award beyond scope
- Public policy (narrow interpretation)
- **Note**: Review of merits is not permitted

### Practical Risks
- Delay despite the 2-month timeline
- Need for local PRC counsel
- "Public policy" may be interpreted differently in PRC
- Asset tracing in PRC before enforcement
- The 2020 amendments extend the enforcement time limit to 6 years from the date of the award

## Court Supervision

| Court | Role |
|-------|------|
| Court of First Instance (CFI) | Interim measures, appointment of arbitrators, challenge of arbitrators, set-aside, enforcement, appeal on question of law (opt-in) |
| Court of Appeal (CA) | Appeals from CFI decisions on arbitration matters (leave required — s.87) |
| Court of Final Appeal (CFA) | Final appeal (leave required — generally only on questions of general public importance) |

### Leave Requirements
- Appeal from CFI in arbitration matters requires leave of the CFI or CA (s.87)
- The test: arguable ground of appeal with reasonable prospects of success
- Policy: limited court intervention, finality of arbitration

### Confidentiality
- Arbitration proceedings are **confidential** in HK (implied by common law)
- Court proceedings relating to arbitration: judgments are published but parties may apply for anonymisation/restrictions
- HKIAC Rules impose confidentiality obligations on parties and tribunal members

## Practice Guidance

1. **Seat matters** — Choosing HK as the seat ensures Model Law procedural regime, court support, and access to the New York Convention and PRC Arrangement
2. **Institutional vs ad hoc** — HKIAC offers robust case management; ad hoc may be more flexible but requires careful drafting
3. **Arbitration agreement review** — Check four elements: seat, institution, number of arbitrators, governing law
4. **Interim measures strategy** — Consider both tribunal-ordered (enforceable under s.61) and court-ordered (s.45) measures; for PRC assets, use the 2019 Arrangement
5. **Set-aside is exclusive recourse** — No appeal on merits; use it only for procedural/ jurisdictional defects
6. **Enforcement in PRC** — Requires certified award from CFI, PRC counsel, and patience; the SPC reporting mechanism adds procedural protection against local protectionism
7. **Confidentiality** — HK common law implies confidentiality; include explicit clause in the arbitration agreement for certainty
8. **Costs** — Tribunal has broad discretion; cost orders may be challenged only on set-aside grounds
9. **Check the latest HKIAC Rules and Practice Notes** for procedural updates
10. **DOJ Practice Direction** — be aware of SL.1 for contempt in arbitration-related court proceedings

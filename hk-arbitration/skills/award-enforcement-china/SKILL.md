# Award Enforcement in China — Skill

## Purpose

Guide legal practitioners through the enforcement of Hong Kong arbitral awards in Mainland China under the Arrangement Concerning Mutual Enforcement of Arbitral Awards between the Mainland and the HKSAR (1999, as amended 2020).

## Scope

- Procedural framework under the Arrangement
- Application to the Intermediate People's Court (IPC)
- Required documents and certification
- Grounds for refusal (mirror Model Law Art 36)
- SPC reporting mechanism
- Time limits and practical timelines
- Common obstacles and strategies

## Workflow

### 1. Confirm the Award Qualifies Under the Arrangement

**Requirements:**
- Award must be made by a **recognised arbitral institution** seated in Hong Kong (HKIAC, ICC-HK, CIETAC-HK, etc.)
- Award must be made **after 1 February 2000** (effective date of the 1999 Arrangement)
- Dispute must be **arbitrable** under PRC law
- Award must be **final and binding** (no pending set-aside proceedings in HK)

**Forthcoming 2020 amendments expanded:**
- The time limit for enforcement is now **6 years** from the date of the award (previously 2 years under PRC Civil Procedure Law)
- The scope of eligible awards was broadened

### 2. Obtain Certified Copies from Hong Kong CFI

**Documents required:**

1. **Certified true copy of the award** — certified by the CFI Registrar (application to CFI for certification)
2. **Certified true copy of the arbitration agreement** — also certified by the CFI
3. **Certificate from the arbitration institution** confirming the award is final and binding (HKIAC/other institution provides this)
4. **Translation** — if the award/agreement is not in Chinese, provide a PRC notarised translation

**Application to CFI for certification:**
- Ex parte application for certified copies of the award and arbitration agreement
- Include a letter from the arbitration institution confirming enforceability
- CFI issues a certificate under seal

### 3. Engage PRC Counsel

**This is mandatory** — only PRC-qualified lawyers may:
- Appear before the Intermediate People's Court
- Prepare and file the enforcement application
- Handle the certified translation and notarisation
- Liaise with court officials and execute enforcement

### 4. Submit Application to the Intermediate People's Court

**Which court?**
- The IPC at the **respondent's domicile** (户籍所在地/household registration) OR
- The IPC at the **location of the respondent's assets or property**

**Timing:**
- **Before arbitration**: Not possible — application can only be made post-award
- **After award**: Within 6 years from the date of the award (2020 amendment — previously 2 years)
- **Court must render a decision** within 2 months from acceptance (extendable with approval)

**Contents of application:**
1. Names and addresses of applicant and respondent
2. Nature and amount of the award
3. Assets/property details (to the extent known)
4. The certified documents (award, agreement, institution certificate)
5. Translated and notarised copies

### 5. Court Review Process

| Stage | Detail | Timeline |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Filing and acceptance** | Court reviews formalities; accepts or rejects application | 7 days |
| **Review** | Court examines grounds for refusal | Up to 2 months |
| **If enforcement refused** | IPC must report to Higher People's Court; if Higher Court affirms refusal, must report to SPC | 4–6 months (additional) |
| **If enforcement granted** | Court issues enforcement order; proceeds to execution | Varies — weeks to months |
| **Execution** | Asset freeze, seizure, auction, etc. | Varies |

### 6. Grounds for Refusal

The Arrangement mirrors Model Law Art 36 (identical to Cap 609 s.81 grounds):

| Ground | Detail | Risk Level |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Invalid arbitration agreement** | Under applicable law | Low (if properly drafted under HK law) |
| **No proper notice** | Of appointment or proceedings | Low (if HK procedural fairness followed) |
| **Award beyond scope** | Exceeds submission to arbitration | Low (if award addresses only referred disputes) |
| **Composition/procedure irregular** | Not in accordance with party agreement or seat law | Low (if HKIAC/ICC rules followed) |
| **Award not yet binding** | Or set aside/suspended in HK | Medium (risk if debtor challenges in HK) |
| **Non-arbitrability** | Subject matter not arbitrable under PRC law | **High** — land/natural resource disputes, certain IP validity matters |
| **Public policy** | Conflicts with fundamental principles of PRC law | **Medium-High** — may be invoked for disputes involving state interests, foreign exchange controls, anti-trust |

**Critical**: "Public policy" under the Arrangement is narrower than the general PRC concept; the SPC has directed courts to interpret it restrictively. However, local courts may still apply it broadly unless the SPC reporting mechanism intervenes.

### 7. SPC Reporting Mechanism (Key Protection)

**If the IPC intends to refuse enforcement**, it must:

1. Write to the Higher People's Court for review
2. If the Higher Court also agrees to refuse, it must report to the **Supreme People's Court (SPC)**
3. The SPC has the final word — its interpretation of the Arrangement is authoritative

**Effect:**
- Prevents local protectionism — the SPC has consistently directed courts to enforce awards
- Adds 3–6 months to proceedings
- But **significantly increases** the likelihood of enforcement (SPC approval rate for enforcement is high)

### 8. Practical Obstacles

| Obstacle | Mitigation |
|----------|------------|
| **Asset tracing** — need to identify assets in PRC | Engage local investigators before filing; obtain discovery orders |
| **Local protectionism** — IPC may favour local respondent | Use SPC reporting mechanism; cite SPC guidance |
| **Translation/notarisation** — documents must be properly translated and notarised in PRC | Engage PRC counsel early; start translation process while obtaining CFI certification |
| **Public policy argument** — respondent may raise broad public policy arguments | Defence: narrow SPC interpretation; cite SPC Q&A on Arrangement |
| **Delay** — courts may take longer than 2 months | Regular follow-up; PRC counsel engages court periodically |
| **Award debtor insolvency** — no assets to enforce | Consider bankruptcy petition in PRC (if cross-border recognition is available) |
| **Service issues** — respondent avoidance | Use court service; consider publication service |

### 9. Enforcement Measures Upon Grant

Once the IPC issues an enforcement order:

| Measure | Effectiveness |
|---------|---------------|
| Asset freeze (bank accounts) | High — freeze first, then execution |
| Seizure of movable property | Medium — need to locate assets |
| Attachment of real property | Medium-High — but need registered ownership |
| Auction of assets | Low-Medium — time-consuming; low recovery rate |
| Earnings garnishment | Low — rarely successful |
| Blacklisting (失信被执行人) | High — practical pressure on individuals/companies |
| Travel restrictions (出境限制) | High — prevents debtor from leaving PRC |

### 10. Costs

| Item | Estimated Range |
|------|-----------------|
| PRC legal fees | RMB 50,000–300,000+ (depends on complexity and asset value) |
| CFI certification fee | HK$1,500–5,000 |
| Translation and notarisation | RMB 5,000–20,000 |
| Court filing fee | 0.5%–1% of enforcement amount |
| Asset investigation | RMB 20,000–100,000 |
| Enforcement costs | Percentage of recovered amount |

## Key Cases

- **Xiamen Xinjingdi Group Co Ltd v Eton Properties Ltd** — SPC upheld enforcement; set standard for pro-enforcement approach
- **Re: Hunan Valin Steel v HKIAC Award** (SPC Q&A) — SPC confirmed narrow public policy construction
- **Shandong Hongri Acron Chemical Joint Stock Co Ltd v PetroChina** — SPC enforced over public policy objection

## Practice Guidance

1. **Plan ahead** — start the enforcement process immediately after receiving the award; the 6-year window is generous but asset tracing takes time
2. **Get certified HK documents first** — the CFI certification process is straightforward but takes 1–2 weeks
3. **PRC counsel is essential** — enforcement in PRC is a local process; always hire experienced PRC litigation counsel
4. **Asset tracing before filing** — identify assets before starting enforcement; filing without asset knowledge may alert the debtor to move assets
5. **SPC mechanism is your friend** — if the IPC refuses, the SPC reporting mechanism provides a strong check against local protectionism
6. **Public policy argument is weak** — SPC has repeatedly directed courts to construe public policy narrowly; cite SPC guidance
7. **Blacklisting is powerful** — the 失信被执行人 (Shixin) list is publicly accessible and can seriously damage a debtor's business operations in PRC; use it as leverage
8. **Patent enforcement vs award enforcement** — the Arrangement applies to awards only, not court judgments; if you have a HK court judgment (not award), the process is different (see HKSAR-Mainland China Judgment Arrangement 2024)

# Interim Measures — Skill

## Purpose

Advise on interim measures available from the arbitral tribunal and Hong Kong courts in support of arbitration under the Arbitration Ordinance (Cap 609), including cross-border measures under the 2019 Arrangement with Mainland China.

## Scope

- Tribunal-ordered interim measures (ss.35–37, Model Law Art 17)
- Court-ordered interim measures in support of arbitration (ss.45, 56–61)
- Preliminary orders (ex parte tribunal orders)
- Cross-border interim measures under 2019 Arrangement
- Types: asset preservation, evidence preservation, security for costs, Mareva, Anton Piller

## Workflow

### 1. Determine the Type of Interim Measure Required

| Type | Purpose | Available From |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Asset preservation | Prevent dissipation of assets pending award | Tribunal (s.35) + Court (s.45) |
| Evidence preservation | Prevent destruction of evidence | Tribunal (s.35) + Court (s.45) |
| Status quo maintenance | Maintain or restore status quo pending resolution | Tribunal (s.35) |
| Security for costs | Secure potential costs award | Tribunal (s.36) + Court (s.45) |
| Sale of perishable goods | Preserve value of goods at risk of deterioration | Court (s.45) |
| Anton Piller order | Search and seizure of evidence | Court only (s.45) |
| Mareva injunction | Freeze assets (including bank accounts, property) | Court only (s.45) |
| Conduct preservation (PRC) | Preserve order to act/refrain from acts | PRC court (2019 Arrangement) |
| Anti-suit injunction | Restrain proceedings in breach of arbitration agreement | Court (s.45) |

### 2. Tribunal-Ordered Interim Measures (ss.35–37)

**Requirements for tribunal order (Art 17A):**

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Substantial probability of success | Applicant must satisfy tribunal that it is likely to succeed on the merits (not a prediction of the final outcome, but a reasonable prospect) |
| Harm not adequately compensable by damages | Risk of serious harm if measure not granted |
| Balance of convenience | Risk of harm to applicant outweighs risk of harm to respondent from the measure |
| Security for costs | Tribunal may require security from applicant as condition for granting interim measure |

**Procedure:**

- Application to the tribunal (typically in the Terms of Reference or a separate application)
- Tribunal decides on paper or after oral hearing
- Order takes effect upon issuance
- **Enforcement of tribunal order (s.61)**: With leave of the CFI, a tribunal-ordered interim measure has the same effect as a court order

**Preliminary orders (Art 17B–17E):**

- Ex parte order (without notice to respondent) — available only in exceptional circumstances
- Disclosure of all material facts (full and frank duty)
- Duration: limited — typically 20 days, after which the tribunal must give the respondent an opportunity to be heard
- Enforceable only if confirmed after the inter partes hearing

### 3. Court-Ordered Interim Measures in Support of Arbitration (s.45)

**Court powers (s.45(1)–(2)):**

The CFI has the same power to grant interim measures in relation to arbitration proceedings as it has in relation to court proceedings, including:
- Mareva injunctions (asset freezing)
- Anton Piller orders (search and seizure)
- Preservation of property or documents
- Sale of perishable goods
- Security for costs
- Anti-suit injunctions

**Key features:**

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Court will not act | Where the tribunal has power and the tribunal can act effectively (s.45(6)) |
| Urgency | Court intervention appropriate where there is urgency and the tribunal has not yet been constituted |
| No appeal | CFI's decision to grant or refuse interim measures is final (s.45(3)) — no appeal |
| Full and frank duty | For ex parte applications, the applicant must disclose all material facts |

**Anti-suit injunctions:**

- Court may grant anti-suit injunction to restrain a party from commencing or pursuing proceedings in another jurisdiction
- The injunction enforces the arbitration agreement (not the jurisdiction of the HK court)
- Test: the foreign proceedings are in breach of the arbitration agreement; strong reason to not grant injunction

### 4. Cross-Border Interim Measures Under the 2019 Arrangement

**Arrangement Concerning Mutual Assistance in Court-Ordered Interim Measures in Aid of Arbitral Proceedings by the Courts of the Mainland and the HKSAR** (effective 1 October 2019, amended 2020)

**Scope:**

- Parties to **qualifying HK-seated arbitrations** may apply to PRC Intermediate People's Courts for interim measures
- Qualifying institutions: HKIAC, ICC International Court of Arbitration — HK, CIETAC HK Arbitration Centre, Hong Kong Maritime Arbitration Group, and others listed by the PRC Supreme People's Court
- The arbitration must be seated in Hong Kong and administered by a qualifying institution

**Types of measures available from PRC courts:**

| Type | PRC Equivalent |
|------|----------------|
| Asset preservation | 财产保全 (cái chǎn bǎo quán) — freeze bank accounts, attach assets |
| Evidence preservation | 证据保全 (zhèng jù bǎo quán) — preserve relevant evidence |
| Conduct preservation | 行为保全 (xíng wéi bǎo quán) — order to preserve certain behaviour |

**Procedure for PRC application:**

1. File application with PRC Intermediate People's Court at the place where the respondent is domiciled or where the assets/evidence are located
2. Application may be made **before** or **after** the arbitration is commenced
3. If before commencement: file arbitration application within 30 days of the PRC court order
4. Provide security to the PRC court (typically 30% of the amount preserved or full)
5. PRC court decides within 48 hours (urgent cases)
6. Duration of freeze: typically 1 year (assets) or until conclusion (evidence)

**Key practical points:**

- This is the **only** mechanism for obtaining PRC court interim measures in support of arbitration seated outside Mainland China
- No corresponding measure for PRC-seated arbitration parties to seek HK court interim measures (though such parties may apply to HK courts under s.45 if the arbitral tribunal cannot act effectively)
- The Arrangement is landmark — Hong Kong is the only jurisdiction outside the Mainland where parties can obtain PRC court-ordered interim measures

### 5. Enforcement of Interim Measures (s.61)

| Measure | Enforcement Mechanism |
|---------|----------------------|
| Tribunal order | Leave of CFI (s.61(1)); order has same effect as court order |
| CFI order | Direct enforcement via court (contempt, execution) |
| PRC court order | PRC enforcement mechanisms (court order, execution) |

**Procedure under s.61:**
- Ex parte application to CFI for leave to enforce
- Leave granted unless order debtor can show grounds for refusal
- Once leave granted: order has effect of court order

### 6. Security for Costs

**Tribunal (s.36):**
- Tribunal may require the applicant to provide security as a condition for granting the interim measure
- Consider: applicant's financial resources, ability to pay adverse costs

**Court (s.45):**
- Court has inherent jurisdiction to order security for costs in arbitration-related proceedings
- Order against the party seeking interim relief

### 7. Practical Strategy

| Scenario | Recommended Approach |
|----------|---------------------|
| Urgent, before tribunal constituted | Apply to CFI for interim relief (s.45) |
| Tribunal constituted, respondent likely to dissipate assets | Apply to tribunal for asset preservation order (s.35); seek CFI leave to enforce (s.61) if respondent ignores |
| Assets in Mainland China | Apply to PRC Intermediate People's Court under 2019 Arrangement |
| Evidence risk | Apply to CFI for Anton Piller order (s.45) |
| Respondent bringing foreign proceedings | Apply to CFI for anti-suit injunction (s.45) |

## Key Provisions

| Section / Article | Subject | Key Detail |
|-------------------|---------|------------|
| s.35 / Art 17 | Tribunal's power to grant interim measures | Types: preserve assets, evidence, status quo |
| s.36 / Art 17A | Conditions for granting | Substantial probability, balance of convenience |
| s.37 / Art 17B–17E | Preliminary orders | Ex parte measures; limited duration |
| s.45 | Court's power | Same as in court proceedings; anti-suit injunctions |
| s.61 | Enforcement of tribunal orders | Leave of CFI; same effect as court order |
| 2019 Arrangement | PRC court interim measures | Only for qualifying HK-seated institutional arbitrations |

## Practice Guidance

1. **Act quickly** — the strongest interim measure is useless if assets or evidence have disappeared
2. **Consider PRC assets early** — if the respondent may have assets in Mainland China, the 2019 Arrangement provides a unique tool unavailable in any other jurisdiction outside the Mainland
3. **Full and frank disclosure** — on ex parte applications, the duty is paramount; failure to disclose can lead to the order being set aside with costs and damages
4. **Security for costs** — be prepared to provide security, especially if applying for asset preservation
5. **Tribunal orders need court assistance** — tribunal-ordered measures have no coercive power; the CFI must give leave under s.61 for enforcement
6. **Anti-suit injunctions are powerful but controversial** — HK courts have granted them in support of arbitration; but be aware of EU/MSJ objections
7. **Costs risk** — interim measures applications are costly; offering cross-undertaking in damages is standard for court-ordered measures
8. **Document evidence** — maintain a clear chain of evidence for the application; lack of concrete evidence will defeat the application

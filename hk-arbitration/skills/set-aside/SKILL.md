# Set-Aside — Skill

## Purpose

Advise on the grounds and procedure for setting aside arbitral awards under s.81 of the Arbitration Ordinance (Cap 609) / Model Law Art 34 in Hong Kong. Set-aside is the **exclusive recourse** against a Model Law award — no appeal on the merits.

## Scope

- Grounds for setting aside (Art 34(2)) — exhaustive and narrowly construed
- Procedure (application to CFI within 3 months)
- Standard of review
- S.82 — court's power to remit/to vary
- Opt-in appeal on question of law (ss.99–104)
- Public policy ground
- Resisting set-aside: strategy

## Workflow

### 1. Determine Whether Appeal or Set-Aside Applies

| Category | Set-Aside (s.81 / Art 34) | Appeal on Question of Law (ss.99–104) |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Scope** | Procedural/jurisdictional defects only | Error of law on the face of the award |
| **Availability** | Always (default) | Only if parties **opted in** |
| **Time limit** | 3 months from receipt of award | 28 days from award (or later with leave) |
| **Grounds** | Exhaustive list (Art 34(2)) | Question of law substantially affecting rights; tribunal decision "obviously wrong" or "general public importance" |
| **Court power** | Set aside, remit, or confirm | Vary or remit the award |

**Important**: Unless parties have expressly opted into the appeal regime (ss.99–104), set-aside is the **only** recourse. No appeal on the merits.

### 2. Identify Whether a Ground Exists (Art 34(2))

**Ground 1: Incapacity (Art 34(2)(a)(i))**
- Party lacked legal capacity under the law applicable to them
- Considerations: mental incapacity, corporate capacity (ultra vires), sovereign immunity waiver

**Ground 2: Invalid arbitration agreement (Art 34(2)(a)(ii))**
- Agreement not valid under the law to which the parties have subjected it (or failing that, under HK law)
- Common arguments: fraud, misrepresentation, lack of consideration, no meeting of minds, non est factum
- **Note**: Separability (Art 16(1)) means invalidity of the main contract does not invalidate the arbitration clause

**Ground 3: No proper notice (Art 34(2)(a)(ii))**
- Party was not given proper notice of the appointment of an arbitrator or of the arbitral proceedings
- Party was otherwise unable to present its case
- **Standards**:
  - Proper notice: actual receipt or reasonable steps to notify
  - Unable to present case: serious procedural irregularity — must have been deprived of a real opportunity to present the case
  - HK courts require "serious procedural unfairness" — not mere procedural irregularity (*Pacific China Holdings Ltd v Grand Pacific Holdings Ltd*)

**Ground 4: Award beyond scope (Art 34(2)(a)(iii))**
- Award deals with a dispute not contemplated or not falling within the terms of the submission to arbitration
- Contains decisions beyond the scope of the submission
- **Severability**: If decisions beyond scope can be separated from other decisions, only the offending part is set aside

**Ground 5: Composition or procedure irregular (Art 34(2)(a)(iv))**
- Composition of the tribunal or procedure not in accordance with party agreement
- Failing such agreement, not in accordance with the Model Law
- **Test**: Was the irregularity material? Would it have affected the outcome?

**Ground 6: Subject matter not arbitrable (Art 34(2)(b)(i))**
- Dispute cannot be settled by arbitration under HK law
- Examples: criminal matters, certain IP validity (patent/design validity in rem), certain competition/antitrust issues, certain winding-up petitions

**Ground 7: Public policy (Art 34(2)(b)(ii))**
- Award conflicts with the public policy of Hong Kong
- **Narrow construction**: "Fundamental conceptions of morality and justice" — not an avenue to challenge the merits
- HK takes the narrowest view: must "shock the conscience" or be "clearly injurious to the public good"
- Examples where successfully raised: fraud, corruption, breach of natural justice, illegality in the underlying contract

### 3. Standard of Review — Set-Aside Is Not an Appeal

| Not Permitted | Permitted |
|---------------|-----------|
| Re-examination of facts | Review of procedural fairness |
| Reconsideration of legal analysis | Review of jurisdiction |
| Review of tribunal's exercise of discretion | Review of due process compliance |
| Challenge to quantum (unless beyond scope) | Challenge for excess of mandate |
| Disagreement with tribunal's reasoning | Challenge for procedural irregularity |

**The court will not substitute its own view for that of the tribunal** on the merits.

### 4. Procedure

#### Step 1: File Application

- **Time limit**: Within **3 months** from the date of receipt of the award (Art 34(3))
- **Hard deadline**: The court has no discretion to extend (strict limitation)
- Where there has been a request for correction, interpretation, or additional award (Art 33), the 3 months run from the date the tribunal disposes of that request

**Filing requirements:**
- Originating summons (Form 8)
- Supporting affidavit exhibiting:
  - The award
  - The arbitration agreement
  - Any relevant correspondence (notice of proceedings, tribunal rulings)
  - Evidence supporting the ground for set-aside
- Draft order setting out the relief sought

#### Step 2: Court Directions

- Court may direct an inter partes hearing
- Case management directions: evidence, skeleton arguments, hearing date
- Court may order a stay pending further evidence

#### Step 3: Hearing and Decision

- Burden of proof: on the applicant (the party seeking set-aside)
- Standard: balance of probabilities
- Court may:
  - **Confirm** the award (set-aside refused)
  - **Set aside** the award (in whole or in part — s.82)
  - **Remit** the award to the tribunal (s.82(1)) — for correction, reconsideration, or a new decision
  - Grant **leave to vary** (in limited circumstances under s.82(2))

#### Step 4: Appeal

- CFI decision on set-aside: **appealable** only with leave (s.87)
- Leave sought from CFI or CA
- Test: arguable ground with reasonable prospects of success
- Further appeal to CFA: leave required (general public importance)

### 5. S.82: Power to Remit

Instead of setting aside the award in whole, the court may:

- **Remit the award** to the tribunal for reconsideration or correction
- **Partly set aside** — sever the offending part while confirming the rest
- **Vary the award** — only where the variation is so minor that it does not affect the substance

**When to seek remission:**
- Procedural defect that can be cured (e.g., a gap in scope, failure to address a claim)
- Tribunal willing and able to correct the defect
- Remission preserves the award rather than requiring fresh arbitration

### 6. Resisting Set-Aside: Strategy for the Award Creditor

| Strategy | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Limitation** | Time bar is absolute — enforce if 3 months have passed |
| **No-merits defence** | Applicant cannot re-litigate the dispute — insist on procedural focus |
| **Narrow grounds** | Argue that the applicant has not met the high threshold for any ground |
| **Severability** | If only part is challenged, argue the balance should stand |
| **Remission** | Offer remission where the defect can be cured by the tribunal |
| **Costs** | Set-aside is a high-cost, low-success route; strong costs case if unsuccessful |

### 7. Appeal on Question of Law (ss.99–104 — Opt-In)

**When available:**
- Parties must have **expressly opted in** to the appeal regime
- Default: no appeal

**Procedure:**
- Application to CFI for leave to appeal
- Deadline: 28 days from award (or with leave, later)
- Test for leave:
  1. The question of law substantially affects the rights of one or more parties
  2. The tribunal's decision is "obviously wrong" OR the question is of "general public importance"
- If leave granted: CFI hears the appeal
- CFI may: confirm, vary, remit, or set aside the award in whole or in part

**Appeal from CFI decision:**
- With leave of CFI or CA
- Only if the question is of "general public importance"

**Practical note**: Most commercial contracts do not opt into this regime. Institutional rules (HKIAC, ICC) default to no appeal. Always check the arbitration agreement.

### 8. Costs

| Scenario | Likely Costs Order |
|----------|---------------------|
| Applicant succeeds in full set-aside | Respondent pays applicant's costs |
| Applicant succeeds in part set-aside | Court may apportion; applicant may get 50–70% |
| Applicant fails | Applicant pays respondent's costs (indemnity basis possible if frivolous) |
| Respondent consents to remission | Costs in the remission (may be costs of the application) |

## Key Cases

- **Gao Haiyan v Keeneye Holdings Ltd** — Public policy ground; narrow construction
- **Pacific China Holdings Ltd (In Liquidation) v Grand Pacific Holdings Ltd** — "Unable to present case" — serious procedural unfairness required
- **Astro Nusantara International BV v PT Ayunda Prima Mitra** — Scope of arbitration agreement; excess of mandate
- **L v M** — Set-aside on public policy grounds; corruption in underlying contract
- **G v D** — Opt-in appeal on question of law; strict approach
- **S Co v B Co** — Award set aside at seat; enforcement considerations

## Practice Guidance

1. **3 months is a hard deadline** — no extension possible; mark the calendar from the date of receipt of the award
2. **No appeal on merits** — the set-aside application must be grounded in procedural or jurisdictional defects; do not attempt to re-argue the case
3. **Public policy is an uphill battle** — HK courts interpret it narrowly; only exceptional cases succeed (fraud, corruption, serious procedural injustice)
4. **Remission is a useful alternative** — if the defect can be cured, seek remission to preserve the award rather than starting over
5. **Costs risk is high** — an unsuccessful set-aside application is expensive; applicants should only proceed with strong grounds
6. **Opt-in appeal is rare** — most commercial contracts do not include it; check the arbitration agreement before advising on appeal options
7. **Evidence must be procedural** — the court will not consider evidence going to the merits of the dispute
8. **Full disclosure** — on set-aside applications, the applicant must disclose all material facts, including any parallel proceedings

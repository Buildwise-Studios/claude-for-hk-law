# Award Enforcement in HK — Skill

## Purpose

Guide legal practitioners through the enforcement of domestic, Model Law, and foreign arbitral awards in Hong Kong under the Arbitration Ordinance (Cap 609) and the New York Convention.

## Scope

- Enforcement of domestic and Model Law awards (s.84, Art 35–36)
- Enforcement of New York Convention awards (s.84, Art V)
- Enforcement of PRC awards under the Arrangement
- Enforcement procedure (leave of CFI)
- Grounds for refusal (exhaustive — pro-enforcement bias)
- Setting aside leave
- Appeal and costs

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the Type of Award

| Type | Applicable Regime | Governing Articles |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Domestic award** | Award made in HK under Cap 609 (domestic arbitration — pre-Model Law opt-in) | s.84, Model Law Art 35–36 |
| **Model Law award** | Award made in HK under Model Law (Cap 609 default) | s.84, Model Law Art 35–36 |
| **New York Convention award** | Award made in a NY Convention state outside HK | s.84, Art V NY Convention |
| **PRC Arrangement award** | Award made in Mainland China | Arrangement (1999, amended 2020) |

**Note**: Cap 609 applies the Model Law to all HK-seated arbitrations (domestic and international) — there is no separate domestic arbitration regime.

### 2. Confirm Enforceability

**Checklist:**
- Award is in writing and signed by the arbitrators
- Award is binding and final (no pending set-aside proceedings in the seat)
- Award deals with arbitrable subject matter
- No ground for refusal is apparent
- Time limit: 6 years from the date of the award (HK limitation period; for state immunity claims, consider application to the CFI)
- Award debtor is within the jurisdiction or has assets in HK

### 3. Enforcement Procedure

#### Step 1: Application to CFI

- Make an **ex parte** application to the CFI for leave to enforce the award as a judgment
- File:
  1. Originating summons (Form 8 or 10)
  - Written supporting affidavit exhibiting:
     - Duly authenticated original award or certified copy
     - Original arbitration agreement or certified copy
     - Translation (if not in English or Chinese — must be certified)
     - Proof of service of award on award debtor
  2. Draft order for leave to enforce
  3. Statement confirming no pending set-aside proceedings

#### Step 2: Court Grants Leave

- Court considers only whether the resisting party has any ground to refuse enforcement
- If no ground apparent: **leave granted**
- If ground apparent: court may direct an inter partes hearing

#### Step 3: Service of Order

- Serve the sealed order and the originating summons on the award debtor
- Time for compliance: typically **14 days** from service (unless the court orders otherwise)
- The order must state: the amount awarded, interest, costs, and that the debtor may apply to set aside the leave within 14 days

#### Step 4: Enforcement as Judgment

- If no application to set aside within 14 days: the award is enforceable as a CFI judgment
- Enforcement methods: writ of fieri facias (seizure of assets), garnishee proceedings, charging order, bankruptcy/winding-up petition, examination of judgment debtor

### 4. Grounds for Refusal (Exhaustive)

**Model Law Art 36 / New York Convention Art V:**

| Ground | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Incapacity of a party | Party lacked legal capacity to enter into arbitration agreement |
| Invalidity of arbitration agreement | Agreement not valid under law applicable to it |
| No proper notice | Of appointment of arbitrator or proceedings; or otherwise unable to present case |
| Award beyond scope | Contains decisions beyond the scope of submission (severable if separable from valid parts) |
| Composition or procedure | Not in accordance with party agreement or Model Law |
| Award not yet binding | Or set aside/suspended in the seat |
| Subject matter not arbitrable | Under HK law |
| Public policy | Narrow construction — must be "fundamental conceptions of morality and justice" |

**HK courts apply a pro-enforcement bias** — refusal is exceptional and the grounds are narrowly construed.

### 5. Setting Aside Leave to Enforce

- Application to set aside must be made **within 14 days** (or as ordered by the court)
- Grounds: same as for refusing enforcement (Art 36)
- The award debtor bears the burden of proof
- **Important**: The court will not rehear the merits of the dispute — enforcement is not an appeal

### 6. Specific Issues

#### Awards Set Aside at the Seat
- Enforcement may still be possible in HK even if the award has been set aside at the seat
- Court has discretion — considers factors: whether the set-aside was on grounds recognised in HK, procedural integrity of the seat court, international comity
- HK courts have taken a pragmatic approach (e.g., *S Co v B Co*)

#### Awards Against Sovereign States
- State immunity: The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region follows the PRC's restrictive theory of state immunity
- Commercial activity exception applies
- Service: under s.84A Cap 609 — requires alternative service procedure through diplomatic channels
- Enforcement against state assets: only commercial assets, not sovereign/military assets

#### Interest
- Contractual interest (if provided in award or underlying contract)
- Pre-award interest: included in award
- Post-award interest: HK Judgments (Interest) Order applies — Judgment rate
- Post-judgment interest: at the judgment rate specified in the High Court Ordinance

### 7. Costs

| Item | Recoverable? |
|------|--------------|
| Legal costs of enforcement application | Yes (costs in the application) |
| Pre-award legal costs (included in award) | Yes (part of award amount) |
| Enforcement costs (seizure, garnishee) | Yes (as enforcement costs) |
| Interest on costs | Yes (from date of order/approval) |

## Key Provisions

| Section / Article | Subject | Key Detail |
|-------------------|---------|------------|
| s.84 | Enforcement of awards | Model Law awards and NY Convention awards |
| Art 35 | Recognition and enforcement | Binding effect; application to court |
| Art 36 | Grounds for refusing | Exhaustive grounds; pro-enforcement bias |
| s.84A | State immunity | Restrictive theory; commercial exception |
| s.34 | No appeal | CFI decision on enforcement is final |

## Key Cases

- **Xiamen Xinjingdi Group Co Ltd v Eton Properties Ltd** — Pro-enforcement approach; PRC award enforcement
- **Gao Haiyan v Keeneye Holdings Ltd** — Public policy ground; narrow construction
- **Astro Nusantara International BV v PT Ayunda Prima Mitra** — Enforcement against non-signatories
- **S Co v B Co** — Award set aside at seat; enforcement in HK remains possible
- **G v D** — Appeal on question of law; enforcement proceeding finality

## Practice Guidance

1. **Pro-enforcement bias** — HK courts strongly favour enforcement; refusal is exceptional
2. **Ex parte application is standard** — no need for inter partes hearing unless the award debtor has a credible defence
3. **14-day window is tight** — award debtors must apply to set aside quickly; late applications are not entertained
4. **No review of merits** — the court does not re-examine the correctness of the award
5. **Award set aside at seat — not fatal** — but will complicate enforcement; consider whether the set-aside judgment is recognised in HK
6. **State immunity** — treat with care; commercial activity exception is available but procedural steps (service) are more stringent
7. **Costs follow the event** — a successful enforcing party is generally entitled to costs
8. **Limitation period** — enforcement action must be brought within 6 years of the award

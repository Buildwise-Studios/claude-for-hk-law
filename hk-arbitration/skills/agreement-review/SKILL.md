# Arbitration Agreement Review — Skill

## Purpose

Review, draft, and advise on arbitration agreements for Hong Kong-seated arbitrations under the Arbitration Ordinance (Cap 609) and UNCITRAL Model Law, ensuring enforceability under the New York Convention.

## Scope

- Validity requirements under Cap 609 s.19 and Model Law Art 7
- Essential elements of a well-drafted arbitration agreement
- Institutional v ad hoc arbitration
- Pathological clauses and how to fix them
- Multi-tier and escalation clauses
- Non-signatories and binding effect
- Arbitration agreement and jurisdiction challenges

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the Existing or Proposed Clause

Review the contractual dispute resolution clause and identify:

- Is there an arbitration agreement at all, or is it a hierarchical/multi-tier clause?
- Is it an institutional arbitration (referring to HKIAC, ICC, SIAC, CIETAC, etc.) or ad hoc?
- Does it specify the seat of arbitration?
- Does it specify the governing law of the arbitration agreement?
- Does it specify the number of arbitrators?
- Does it specify the language of proceedings?
- Are there any inconsistencies, gaps, or contradictory provisions?

### 2. Assess Validity Under s.19 Cap 609

**Requirements (s.19, Model Law Art 7):**

| Requirement | Detail | Check |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| In writing | Recorded in any form (electronic communication, exchange of statements of claim/defence alleging arbitration and not denied) | [ ] |
| Defined legal relationship | Contractual or non-contractual relationship identified | [ ] |
| Disputes referable | All or certain disputes which have arisen or may arise | [ ] |
| Subject matter arbitrable | Capable of settlement by arbitration (disputes involving criminal matters, certain IP validity, winding up — may not be arbitrable) | [ ] |

### 3. Review Essential Elements Checklist

| Element | Recommended | Risk if Missing |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Seat** | "Hong Kong" explicitly stated | Ambiguity — may require court determination |
| **Institution/Administering body** | HKIAC, ICC, SIAC, CIETAC (HK), etc. | Ad hoc may be too costly or unstructured |
| **Governing law of arbitration agreement** | "The law of Hong Kong" | May default to seat law, but helpful to specify |
| **Number of arbitrators** | 1 or 3 | Default: 3 (HKIAC default if not specified) |
| **Language** | English, Chinese (Cantonese/Mandarin), or bilingual | Default: language of arbitration agreement |
| **Scope of disputes** | "All disputes arising out of or in connection with this agreement" | Narrow wording may leave gap |
| **Applicable law for merits** | Governing law of contract specified | Tribunal determines if not specified |

### 4. Spot Pathological Clauses

| Pathology | Why It's Pathological | Fix |
|-----------|----------------------|-----|
| "Arbitration in Hong Kong under HK law" | No institution or rules specified | Add HKIAC Rules or UNCITRAL Rules ad hoc |
| "Arbitration at HKIAC" | Use "under HKIAC Rules" | Clarify institutional rules |
| "Disputes to be resolved by arbitration or litigation at claimant's option" | Unenforceable — inconsistent | Choose one; arbitration with opt-out |
| No seat specified | Jurisdiction ambiguity | Add seat: Hong Kong |
| "Arbitration in Hong Kong; courts of Singapore have exclusive jurisdiction" | Direct conflict | Remove court jurisdiction clause |
| Reference to a non-existent institution or outdated rules | Institutional gap | Specify current institution and rules version |

### 5. Draft a Proper Clause

**HKIAC Model Clause (2024):**

"Any dispute, controversy, claim or difference of any kind whatsoever arising out of or in connection with this agreement, including any question regarding its existence, validity or termination, shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration administered by the Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre (HKIAC) under the HKIAC Administered Arbitration Rules in force when the Notice of Arbitration is submitted.

The seat of arbitration shall be Hong Kong.

The number of arbitrators shall be [one/three].

The arbitration proceedings shall be conducted in [English/Chinese/bilingual]."

**For ad hoc arbitration:**

"Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this agreement shall be finally settled by arbitration in Hong Kong in accordance with the UNCITRAL Arbitration Rules.

The appointing authority shall be [HKIAC / Secretary-General of the Permanent Court of Arbitration].

The number of arbitrators shall be one.

The language of the arbitration shall be English."

### 6. Multi-Tier / Escalation Clauses

Common in construction, infrastructure, and commercial contracts:

```
Step 1: [Senior management negotiation — 30 days]
Step 2: [Mediation at HKIAC — 30 days]
Step 3: [Arbitration under HKIAC Rules]
```

**Issues:**
- Are the pre-arbitration steps mandatory or optional in nature?
- Are the time limits clear?
- Are condition precedents to arbitration? (HK courts generally enforce pre-conditions as conditions precedent — *L.X.X. v O.O.O.*)
- Practical solution: Use "may" for some steps rather than "shall" for all

### 7. Non-Signatories

Who may be bound by the arbitration agreement?

| Theory | Application in HK |
|--------|-------------------|
| Group of companies doctrine | Not definitively adopted; depends on facts |
| Agency | Agent binds principal if acting within authority |
| Estoppel | Party may be estopped from denying they are bound |
| Incorporation by reference | Clear reference to another document containing arbitration clause |
| Successors in title/assignees | Generally bound if assignment validly made |

### 8. Jurisdiction Challenge (Kompetenz-Kompetenz)

- The tribunal has power to rule on its own jurisdiction (Art 16)
- **Plea as to jurisdiction must be raised no later than the defence on the merits** (Art 16(2))
- Tribunal may rule on jurisdiction as a preliminary question or in the final award
- Either party may apply to the CFI within 30 days of the tribunal's jurisdictional ruling (Art 16(3))
- CFI decision is final — no appeal (s.34 Cap 609)

## Key Provisions

| Section / Article | Subject | Key Detail |
|-------------------|---------|------------|
| s.19 / Art 7 | Arbitration agreement | Form requirements: in writing |
| s.34 | No appeal on jurisdiction ruling | CFI decision on jurisdiction is final |
| Art 16 | Kompetenz-Kompetenz | Tribunal rules on own jurisdiction |
| Art 8 | Stay of court proceedings | Court must refer parties to arbitration unless agreement null/void |
| s.20 | Death of a party | Agreement not discharged by death; enforceable by/against personal representatives |

- **Pacific China Holdings Ltd (In Liquidation) v Grand Pacific Holdings Ltd** — Jurisdiction challenge; Kompetenz-Kompetenz
- **L.X.X. v O.O.O.** — Pre-conditions to arbitration (multi-tier clauses)

## Practice Guidance

1. **Get the seat right** — the seat determines the procedural law and court supervision regime
2. **Never mix arbitration and litigation** — an "arbitration or litigation" clause is unenforceable
3. **Clear scope** — "arising out of or in connection with" is the broadest; use it for commercial contracts
4. **Number of arbitrators** — 1 for smaller claims; 3 for complex/high-value claims (cost/benefit analysis)
5. **Pre-arbitration steps** — make them mandatory only if the client is committed; consider time limits
6. **Choose institution carefully** — HKIAC is preferred for HK-seated arbitrations due to familiarity with HK procedural law, but ICC, SIAC, and others operate in HK too
7. **Review for consistency** — check the entire agreement for conflict with the arbitration clause (exclusive jurisdiction clauses, governing law, dispute resolution in schedules)
8. **Consider arbitration of non-contractual claims** — "arising out of or in connection with" covers tort, restitution, and statutory claims related to the contract

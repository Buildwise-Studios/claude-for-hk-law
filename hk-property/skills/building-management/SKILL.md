# Building Management — Skill

## Purpose

Advise on building management matters under the Building Management Ordinance (Cap 344), including Deed of Mutual Covenant (DMC) review, Incorporated Owners (IO) formation and governance, management disputes, enforcement actions, and building management litigation.

## Scope

- Deed of Mutual Covenant (DMC) structure and review
- Incorporated Owners (IO) formation under Cap 344
- IO management committee governance
- Management fee collection and arrears recovery
- Building maintenance and safety obligations
- Unauthorised alterations and enforcement
- Administrator appointment
- Land Tribunal — Building Management jurisdiction

## Workflow

### 1. Review Deed of Mutual Covenant (DMC)

The DMC is the **constitutional document** of a multi-owner building. It:
- Defines common areas vs private lots
- Sets out management rights and obligations
- Establishes management fee sharing proportions
- Contains covenants on use, alterations, assignment

#### Key DMC Clauses to Review
- **Common areas**: definition and scope (lobbies, corridors, lifts, roofs, clubhouse)
- **Exclusive use areas**: rooftop gardens, external walls, podium areas
- **Management rights**: who manages — developer (initial), IO (post-incorporation), management company
- **Management fees**: apportionment formula (by share / by floor area / equal)
- **Car park**: ownership structure — common area vs separate lot
- **Facilities**: clubhouse, swimming pool, gym — ownership and access rights
- **Alterations**: restrictions on unit-altering works (structural changes, facade)
- **Signage**: regulation of external signage
- **Assignment**: pre-emption rights, notice to IO on sale
- **Insurance**: obligation to insure building, contents, third-party liability
- **Dispute resolution**: DMC disputes, arbitration clause
- **Variation**: mechanism for amending DMC (typically unanimous consent)

#### DMC Checklist
- [ ] DMC registered at Land Registry?
- [ ] Clear definition of common areas?
- [ ] Management fee apportionment clear?
- [ ] Car park ownership clearly established?
- [ ] Alteration restrictions reasonable?
- [ ] IO incorporation mechanism automatic (upon 20%+ conveyance)?
- [ ] Insurance requirements specified?
- [ ] Pre-emption rights on transfer?

### 2. IO Formation and Governance

#### Formation Triggers
- **Automatic**: under s.3(1) of Cap 344, an IO is formed automatically when 20% or more of the shares in the building have been conveyed by the developer
- **Voluntary**: owners may apply to the Land Tribunal for an order incorporating the IO (s.3A)

#### Management Committee
- Elected at Annual General Meeting (AGM)
- Minimum composition: 3 members (or as required by DMC)
- Chairperson, Treasurer, Secretary
- Term: typically 1–3 years
- Vacancies: filled at general meeting or by co-option

#### IO Powers (Cap 344)
- Collect management fees (s.22)
- Enforce DMC covenants (s.34A)
- Serve notice for DMC breaches (s.34B)
- Apply to Land Tribunal for enforcement orders (s.34C)
- Insure the building
- Conduct building maintenance and safety works
- Engage and terminate management company
- Bring legal proceedings in the IO's name (s.16-18)

#### Meeting Procedures
- **AGM**: within 15 months of last AGM
- **EGM**: on requisition by 5% of owners (or DMC threshold)
- **Quorum**: 10% of owners (or DMC provisions)
- **Resolutions**: ordinary (simple majority) vs special (75% or as DMC)
- **Proxy voting**: permitted unless DMC restricts

### 3. Management Fee Collection and Arrears

#### Collection Process
1. Issue monthly/quarterly demand
2. Send reminder notice (7–14 days)
3. Send formal demand letter (solicitor)
4. File claim at Land Tribunal (for claims up to HK$3,000,000)
5. Obtain judgment and enforce (garnishee order, charging order, winding-up petition)

#### Arrears Recovery Options
- **Land Tribunal claim**: summary procedure (Form 1/2)
- **Charging order**: on the lot of the defaulting owner (subject to Land Registry registration)
- **Garnishee order**: against rental income received by defaulting owner
- **Winding-up petition**: for corporate owners (drastic, requires debt > HK$10,000)
- **Attachment of earnings**: limited use in HK

### 4. Building Maintenance and Safety

#### Obligations
- **Landlord's covenant**: DMC typically obliges IO to maintain common areas
- **Building Ordinance (Cap 123)**: mandatory window inspection (MWIS), mandatory building inspection (MBIS)
- **Fire safety**: Fire Services Ordinance (Cap 95) compliance
- **Lifts and escalators**: periodic inspection and maintenance
- **Plumbing and drainage**: maintenance obligations

#### Major Works
- Requires owners' resolution (special resolution for significant expenditure)
- Tendering procedures: three quotes minimum
- Contract supervision
- Progress reporting to owners

### 5. Unauthorised Alterations

#### Types of Breach
- Structural alterations without approval (removal of walls, beams)
- External works (awning, window replacement, air-con installation)
- Roof or podium encroachment
- Common area obstruction
- Change of use (residential to commercial)
- Fire safety breaches (blocked exits, removal of fire doors)

#### Enforcement Steps
1. Serve notice of breach (s.34B Cap 344)
2. Require rectification within specified period
3. Apply to Land Tribunal for enforcement order (s.34C)
4. Seek costs order
5. Carry out works in default and recover costs

### 6. Administrator Appointment

Grounds for appointment (s.24 Cap 344):
- IO fails to exercise its functions
- IO fails to comply with DMC
- Serious dispute preventing IO functioning
- Mismanagement or misappropriation of funds
- Building safety at risk

Appointment: Land Tribunal order
Powers: full management authority (subject to court order)
Duration: typically 6–12 months, renewable
Costs: paid out of management fund (or owners proportionally)

### 7. Land Tribunal Proceedings

#### Types of Claims
- Management fee arrears (up to HK$3,000,000)
- Enforcement of DMC (s.34C)
- Administrator appointment (s.24)
- Interpretation of DMC (declaration)
- Variation of DMC (limited grounds)
- Appeal from IO decisions

#### Procedure
1. Originating application (Form 1)
2. Statement of claim / defence
3. Case management conference
4. Hearing (usually before Building Management Adjudicator)
5. Order and costs
